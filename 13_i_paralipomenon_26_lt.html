Versus 1: THE OFFICERS OF THE ARMY

Vers. 1. Magistratus exercitus, id est principes tam politici quam ecclesiastici; exercitus enim revocatur totus populus tam laicorum quam Levitarum ob multitudinem; erant enim multi et constipati, iique per ordines quasi acies dispositi, uti sunt milites in exercitu. Magistratus Levitarum, cap. xxiv, 6, vocantur « principes Sanctuarii. »

WHO SHOULD PROPHESY (that is, who should sing and praise God) ON HARPS, ACCORDING TO THEIR NUMBER, EACH SERVING IN THE OFFICE ASSIGNED TO THEM — that is to say, each group sang in their turn in the number to which they had been assigned for singing; for one more numerous family constituting one order of singers had more cantors than the one that contained fewer members in its household. In Hebrew it is 'men of work in their ministry,' that is, diligent men, skillfully performing their duty — that is to say, all these were most expert musicians, says Vatablus.

Sub manu (sub regimine et directione) Asaph prophetantis (canentis Dei laudes) juxta regem, scilicet in arce Sion coram arca, juxta sedem Davidis, ubi nimirum ipsi sedere, et Dei laudibus interesse solebat. Nam verisimile est alios cantores fuisse deputatos ad canendum in Tabernaculo Moysis, quod erat in Gabaon; unde aliqui, ut utrosque comprehendant, explicant to juxta regem, q. d. Juxta regis praescriptum et distributionem, ut hi in Sion, illi in Gabaon canerent.

Versus 3: THE SONS OF IDITHUN: GODOLIAS, SORI, SESEIAS, AND HASABIA...

Verse 3. THE SONS OF IDITHUN: GODOLIAS, SORI, SESEIAS, AND HASABIAS, AND MATHATHIAS, SIX. — Only five are listed here, as is evident to anyone counting them. Therefore the sixth is missing, and it is likely that he is 'Semeias,' who is named in verse 17 in the tenth lot; for Semeias is not named among the sons of Asaph nor among the sons of Heman. He was therefore a son of Idithun; for the sons of these three are described here, as is clear from verse 1.

Versus 5: ALL THESE WERE SONS OF HEMAN, THE KING'S SEER IN THE WORD...

Verse 5. ALL THESE WERE SONS OF HEMAN, THE KING'S SEER IN THE WORDS OF GOD. — Heman is called 'Seer,' that is, prophet — that is, a singer of divine praises before David. 'In the words of God' means: when the words of God, that is, His deeds and praises, were being sung. In other words, David had in his court and at his table his own musicians who sang secular music for the king's respectable recreation; but here Heman took care only that sacred songs were sung in the Tabernacle when David devoted himself to divine worship. 'The words of God' therefore were psalms, hymns, and canticles of divine praise.

Ut exaltaret cornu, id est ut canendo exaltaret magnitudinem, magnificentiam, et immensam majestatem, sapientiam, misericordiam, potentiam, clementiam et beneficentiam Dei. Ita Abulensis. Secundo, et magis præcise, q. d. Heman curabat cani psalmos, in quibus David orat, et prædicit exaltandum «cornu,» id est regnum, potentiam, et gloriam tam imperii sui, quam antitypi Christi. Hoc enim audire David erat gratissimum et desideratissimum. Unde crebro in psalmis de hac cornu exaltatione agit. Hinc Vatablus sic vertit: Qui erat regis videns in verbis divinis ut erigeretur cornu; idque sic explicat, q. d. Heman prophetavit de Davide quod assequeretur magnam famam et gloriam. «Cornu» enim significat dignitatem et gloriam, item robur et vires, ac si dicatur, ad exaltandam dignitatem et potentiam regiam. Asaph enim et Idithun canebant alios psalmos, qui ad populum erudiendum et consolandum spectabant, eumque ad pietatem et ad Deum secum laudandum excitabant.

Versus 7: WHO WERE INSTRUCTED IN SINGING

Verse 7. WHO WERE INSTRUCTED IN SINGING — that is, by 'teachers,' as follows, who taught the rest to sing and play skillfully, both with voice and with musical instruments.

Versus 8: THE LEARNED EQUALLY WITH THE UNLEARNED

8. Doctus pariter et indoctus, q. d. Aeque peritus canendi ac imperitus, aeque doctor ac discipulus, q. d. David distribuit cantores in 24 classes, et cuique classi dedit duodecim magistros musices peritos, qui caeteros docerent canere, eosque in cantu dirigerent et perficerent. Quocirca magistri hi universim fuere 288, ut dictum est vers. 7. Multiplica enim 24 per 12, resultabit numerus 288.

Versus 5: BECAUSE THE LORD BLESSED HIM (Obededom)

5. Quia benedixit illi (Obededom) Dominus ob arcam quam domi suae religiose habuit, et sedulo custodivit, II Reg. cap. vi. Hac de causa ipse caeteros Levitas filiorum numero, aeque ac robore tum corporis tum animi superavit, unde vers. 8, dicitur: « Omnes hi de filiis Obededom: ipsi et filii et fratres eorum fortissimi ad ministrandum 62 de Obededom. »

Versus 10: FOR HE HAD NOT HAD A FIRSTBORN

10. Non enim habuerat primogenitum, q. d. Hosa tunc temporis non habebat primogenitum, quia is jam erat mortuus; quare ejus loco constituit principem familiae suae Semri, qui erat secundo genitus.

Versus 12: THESE WERE DIVIDED AS DOORKEEPERS, SO THAT THE CHIEFS OF ...

12. Hi divisi sunt in janitores, ut semper principes custodiarum, etc., ministrarent in domo Domini. Erat ergo magna Janitorum horum dignitas; primo, quia ipsi erant « principes custodiarum, » q. d. Sicut praetoriani milites praesunt custodiae regis et domus regiae, sic hi janitores praeerant custodiae et custodibus omnibus domus Dei, scilicet templi. Secunda, quia iidem praeerant custodiae thesauri tam templi quam regis, ut patet vers. 20, 26 et 30. Item sartis tectis templi, vers. 27. Tertio, quia ex iis creabantur populi doctores, praefecti, et judices in causis tam sacris quam profanis, ut patet vers. 29, 30 et 32.

Versus 14: SELEMIA

14. Selemia. Hic est ille qui vers. 1 et seq. vocatur « Meselemias. » Ille enim decurtate ad brevitatem vocatur « Selemias, » sicut Jechonias a Jeremia cap. xxii, vocatur « Chonias. »

Versus 15: IN WHICH PART OF THE HOUSE WAS THE COUNCIL OF THE ELDERS

15. In qua parte domus erat seniorum concilium, q. d. In parte Australi templi erat locus summi concilii, quod « Sanhedrim » dicebatur, et 72 primoribus, quasi senatoribus constabat, vide dicta Num. xi.

Versus 16: WHICH LEADS TO THE ROAD OF ASCENT

16. Quae ducit ad viam ascensionis qua ascendebatur in montem Sion, et in tabernaculum ibidem erectum; ac deinde in templum in eodem a Salomone fabricatum. Custodia contra custodiam. Vatablus, custodia e regione custodiae, q. d. Una excubiarum custodia respondebat alteri ex adverso positae. Videtur enim haec porta templi Occidentalis prae caeteris duplicem habuisse custodiam, ob majorem in ea populi frequentiam, ideoque majus in ea tumultus periculum. Id ita esse liquet ex vers. 18.

WATCH CORRESPONDING TO WATCH — Vatablus says: a watch opposite to another watch, that is to say, one station of guards corresponded to another placed opposite it. For this western gate of the temple seems to have had, beyond the others, a double guard, on account of the greater crowding of people there, and therefore the greater danger of tumult. That this is so is clear from verse 18.

Versus 18: IN THE CELLS OF THE DOORKEEPERS AT THE WEST, FOUR ON THE ...

18. In cellulis quoque janitorum ad Occidentem quatuor in via, binique per cellulas, q. d. Ad portam templi Occidentalem, « in cellulis janitorum, » excubabant « quatuor » Levitae janitorum principes, « in via » ascensionis, de qua dictum est vers. 16, ac insuper « bini alii » principes « per cellulas » statas excubitorum distributi agebant excubias cum Levitis minoribus sibi subditis. Hinc vides plures et majores fuisse excubias ad portam Occidentalem, quam ad caeteras templi, ut dixi.

Versus 29: FOR OUTWARD BUSINESS

29. Ad opera forinsecus, puta ad ligna et lapides caedendos et poliendos, ad vineas, prata, et agros templi colendos, etc.
