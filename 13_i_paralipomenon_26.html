Synopsis of the Chapter

Verse 1: THE OFFICERS OF THE ARMY

Verse 1. THE OFFICERS OF THE ARMY — that is, the leaders both political and ecclesiastical; for the 'army' here refers to the entire people, both laymen and Levites, on account of their great number; for they were many and closely packed, and arranged by ranks like battle lines, as soldiers are in an army. The officers of the Levites, in chapter 24:6, are called 'princes of the Sanctuary.'

WHO SHOULD PROPHESY (that is, who should sing and praise God) ON HARPS, ACCORDING TO THEIR NUMBER, EACH SERVING IN THE OFFICE ASSIGNED TO THEM — that is to say, each group sang in their turn in the number to which they had been assigned for singing; for one more numerous family constituting one order of singers had more cantors than the one that contained fewer members in its household. In Hebrew it is 'men of work in their ministry,' that is, diligent men, skillfully performing their duty — that is to say, all these were most expert musicians, says Vatablus.

UNDER THE HAND (under the direction and governance) OF ASAPH, WHO PROPHESIED (sang the praises of God) BESIDE THE KING — namely in the citadel of Zion before the Ark, near the seat of David, where he was accustomed to sit and attend to the praises of God. For it is likely that other singers were deputized to sing in the Tabernacle of Moses, which was in Gibeon; hence some, in order to include both groups, explain 'beside the king' as meaning: according to the king's prescription and arrangement, so that these would sing in Zion and those in Gibeon.

Verse 3: THE SONS OF IDITHUN: GODOLIAS, SORI, SESEIAS, AND HASABIA...

Verse 3. THE SONS OF IDITHUN: GODOLIAS, SORI, SESEIAS, AND HASABIAS, AND MATHATHIAS, SIX. — Only five are listed here, as is evident to anyone counting them. Therefore the sixth is missing, and it is likely that he is 'Semeias,' who is named in verse 17 in the tenth lot; for Semeias is not named among the sons of Asaph nor among the sons of Heman. He was therefore a son of Idithun; for the sons of these three are described here, as is clear from verse 1.

Verse 5: ALL THESE WERE SONS OF HEMAN, THE KING'S SEER IN THE WORD...

Verse 5. ALL THESE WERE SONS OF HEMAN, THE KING'S SEER IN THE WORDS OF GOD. — Heman is called 'Seer,' that is, prophet — that is, a singer of divine praises before David. 'In the words of God' means: when the words of God, that is, His deeds and praises, were being sung. In other words, David had in his court and at his table his own musicians who sang secular music for the king's respectable recreation; but here Heman took care only that sacred songs were sung in the Tabernacle when David devoted himself to divine worship. 'The words of God' therefore were psalms, hymns, and canticles of divine praise.

TO EXALT THE HORN — that is, by singing to exalt the greatness, magnificence, and immense majesty, wisdom, mercy, power, clemency, and beneficence of God. So says Abulensis. Secondly, and more precisely: Heman took care that psalms were sung in which David prays and foretells that the 'horn' — that is, the kingdom, power, and glory both of his own empire and of the antitype, Christ — would be exalted. For to hear this was most pleasing and desirable to David. Hence he frequently treats of this exaltation of the horn in the psalms. Thus Vatablus translates: 'Who was the king's seer in divine words, that the horn might be raised up'; and he explains it thus: Heman prophesied concerning David that he would attain great fame and glory. For 'horn' signifies dignity and glory, likewise strength and power, as if to say: for the exaltation of royal dignity and power. For Asaph and Idithun sang other psalms that pertained to instructing and consoling the people, and stirring them to piety and to praising God with him.

Verse 7: WHO WERE INSTRUCTED IN SINGING

Verse 7. WHO WERE INSTRUCTED IN SINGING — that is, by 'teachers,' as follows, who taught the rest to sing and play skillfully, both with voice and with musical instruments.

Verse 8: THE LEARNED EQUALLY WITH THE UNLEARNED

Verse 8. THE LEARNED EQUALLY WITH THE UNLEARNED — that is, the one skilled in singing as well as the unskilled, the teacher as well as the pupil. In other words, David distributed the singers into 24 classes, and to each class he gave twelve masters skilled in music, who would teach the rest to sing and direct and perfect them in song. Therefore these masters were in total 288, as stated in verse 7. For multiply 24 by 12, and the number 288 results.

Verse 5: BECAUSE THE LORD BLESSED HIM (Obededom)

Verse 5. BECAUSE THE LORD BLESSED HIM (Obededom) — on account of the Ark which he had reverently kept in his house and diligently guarded, 2 Kings chapter 6. For this reason he surpassed the other Levites in the number of his sons, as well as in strength of both body and mind; hence verse 8 says: 'All these were of the sons of Obededom: they and their sons and brothers, most valiant for service, 62 of Obededom.'

Verse 10: FOR HE HAD NOT HAD A FIRSTBORN

Verse 10. FOR HE HAD NOT HAD A FIRSTBORN — that is to say, Hosa at that time did not have a firstborn, because he had already died; therefore in his place he appointed as chief of his family Semri, who was the second-born.

Verse 12: THESE WERE DIVIDED AS DOORKEEPERS, SO THAT THE CHIEFS OF ...

Verse 12. THESE WERE DIVIDED AS DOORKEEPERS, SO THAT THE CHIEFS OF THE WATCHES, ETC., SHOULD MINISTER IN THE HOUSE OF THE LORD. — The dignity of these doorkeepers was therefore great. First, because they were 'chiefs of the watches,' as if to say: just as praetorian soldiers are in charge of guarding the king and the royal house, so these doorkeepers presided over the guard and all the guards of the house of God, namely the temple. Second, because the same men presided over the guardianship of the treasury of both the temple and the king, as is clear from verses 20, 26, and 30. Likewise over the repairs of the temple, verse 27. Third, because from them were appointed teachers of the people, prefects, and judges in both sacred and secular cases, as is clear from verses 29, 30, and 32.

Verse 14: SELEMIA

Verse 14. SELEMIA — This is the one who in verse 1 and following is called 'Meselemias.' For he is called 'Selemias' in abbreviated form for brevity, just as Jechonias is called 'Chonias' by Jeremiah in chapter 22.

Verse 15: IN WHICH PART OF THE HOUSE WAS THE COUNCIL OF THE ELDERS

Verse 15. IN WHICH PART OF THE HOUSE WAS THE COUNCIL OF THE ELDERS — that is to say, in the southern part of the temple was the place of the supreme council, which was called 'Sanhedrin,' and consisted of 72 chief men, like senators; see the comments on Numbers 11.

Verse 16: WHICH LEADS TO THE ROAD OF ASCENT

Verse 16. WHICH LEADS TO THE ROAD OF ASCENT — by which one ascended to Mount Zion and to the Tabernacle erected there; and afterwards to the temple built on the same site by Solomon.

WATCH CORRESPONDING TO WATCH — Vatablus says: a watch opposite to another watch, that is to say, one station of guards corresponded to another placed opposite it. For this western gate of the temple seems to have had, beyond the others, a double guard, on account of the greater crowding of people there, and therefore the greater danger of tumult. That this is so is clear from verse 18.

Verse 18: IN THE CELLS OF THE DOORKEEPERS AT THE WEST, FOUR ON THE ...

Verse 18. IN THE CELLS OF THE DOORKEEPERS AT THE WEST, FOUR ON THE ROAD, AND TWO AT EACH CELL — that is to say, at the western gate of the temple, 'in the cells of the doorkeepers,' 'four' Levite chiefs of the doorkeepers kept watch 'on the road' of ascent, about which verse 16 spoke, and in addition 'two other' chiefs 'at the cells' were distributed at fixed guard stations and kept watch with the lesser Levites subject to them. From this you see that there were more and greater watches at the western gate than at the other gates of the temple, as I said.

Verse 29: FOR OUTWARD BUSINESS

Verse 29. FOR OUTWARD BUSINESS — namely for cutting and polishing wood and stone, for cultivating the vineyards, meadows, and fields of the temple, etc.
