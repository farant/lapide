### Jerome (Hieronymus)
- **Slug**: `saint/jerome`
- **Also known as**: St. Jerome, Hieronymus, Saint Hieronymus
- **Dates**: c. 342–420
- **Role**: Doctor of the Church, translator of the Vulgate Bible; author of both prefaces in this document
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#preamble-p1` — Named as one of the two authors of the work
    text: "St. Jerome / Fr. H. D. Lacordaire, O.P."
  - `#helmeted-prologue` — Heading identifies him as author of the Helmeted Prologue
    text: "THE HELMETED PROLOGUE."
  - `#helmeted-prologue-p29` — Speaking in the first person, defends his translation labor; asks readers not to consider his work a reproach of the ancients; invokes the handmaids of Christ to pray for him against detractors
    text: "Read therefore first my Samuel and my Melachim -- mine, I say, mine. For whatever we have both learned by more frequent translating and hold by more careful emending is ours."
  - `#jerome-to-paulinus` — Heading identifies him as author of the letter to Paulinus
    text: "JEROME TO PAULINUS."
  - `#jerome-to-paulinus-p2` — Speaks in first person throughout; recounts examples of seekers of learning (Pythagoras, Plato, Apollonius); writes to Paulinus about the study of Scripture
    text: "I do not say these things because there is anything of the sort in me that you could either wish to hear from me or desire to learn"
  - `#jerome-to-paulinus-p48` — Addresses Paulinus at the close of the letter; offers to study together; mentions brother Eusebius
    text: "I will receive you with open arms, and (to babble something foolish, after the pomposity of Hermagoras) whatever you seek, I will try to know together with you."
- **Related people**: `saint/paulinus-of-nola`, `cleric/ambrosius`, `saint/paul`, `saint/peter`, `biblical/moses`, `biblical/david`, `classical/plato`, `classical/pythagoras`, `classical/apollonius-of-tyana`
- **Related places**: Bethlehem, Palestine, Rome, Jerusalem
- **Related works**: Vulgate Bible, Helmeted Prologue (Prologus Galeatus), Letter to Paulinus
- **Related subjects**: biblical translation, Hebrew canon, study of Scripture

    text: "Why is the apostle Paul called a vessel of election? Surely because he was an armory of the law and the holy Scriptures."

    text: "one must conquer the spirit of the flesh, as the apostle Saint Paul says"
