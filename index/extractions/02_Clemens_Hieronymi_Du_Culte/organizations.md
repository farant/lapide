# Organizations — 02_Clemens_Hieronymi_Du_Culte

### The Academy (of Athens)
- **Slug**: `institution/academy-of-athens`
- **Also known as**: Plato's Academy, the gymnasia of the Academy
- **Description**: Philosophical school founded by Plato in Athens; Jerome notes that Plato, though powerful in the Academy, preferred to travel abroad and learn modestly from others.
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#jerome-to-paulinus-p2` — Plato's teaching resounded in the gymnasia of the Academy, yet he chose to become a stranger and a student
    text: "whose teaching resounded in the gymnasia of the Academy, might become a stranger and a student"
- **Related people**: plato
- **Related places**: europe/greece/athens

### The Pythagoreans
- **Slug**: `institution/pythagoreans`
- **Also known as**: Pythagorean school
- **Description**: Philosophical school founded by Pythagoras; Jerome notes the Pythagoreans held that Apollonius was a philosopher rather than a magician.
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#jerome-to-paulinus-p2` — The Pythagoreans held that Apollonius was a philosopher, not a magician
    text: "or a philosopher, as the Pythagoreans hold"
- **Related people**: apollonius-of-tyana, pythagoras

### The Presbytery
- **Slug**: `church/presbytery`
- **Also known as**: Council of Elders, presbyterium
- **Description**: The body of ordained priests/elders; Paul writes to Timothy about the grace given by the laying on of hands of the presbytery.
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#jerome-to-paulinus-p2` — Paul exhorts Timothy not to neglect the grace given by the laying on of hands of the presbytery
    text: "the grace which was given him by the laying on of hands of the presbytery"
- **Related people**: paul, timothy

### The Synagogue
- **Slug**: `institution/synagogue`
- **Also known as**: —
- **Description**: Jewish assembly and place of worship; Jerome contrasts the gilded temple of the synagogue with the desert spring of the Church. Lacordaire notes the Synagogue judges Christ.
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#jerome-to-paulinus-p2` — Jerome contrasts the desert spring of the Church with the gilded temple of the synagogue
    text: "more in the desert spring of the Church than in the gilded temple of the synagogue"
  - `#du-culte-p8` — The Synagogue judges Jesus Christ
    text: "The Synagogue judges Him"
- **Related people**: jesus-christ

### The Church
- **Slug**: `church/church-universal`
- **Also known as**: Church of Christ, the Church
- **Description**: The universal body of Christian believers founded by Christ; referenced extensively in both Jerome's letter and Lacordaire's essay as the spiritual kingdom, the inheritor of the prophets, and the edifice founded by the apostles.
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#jerome-to-paulinus-p2` — Holy rusticity builds up the Church of Christ by the merit of its life
    text: "it builds up the Church of Christ by the merit of its life"
  - `#jerome-to-paulinus-p16` — Joshua's conquest describes the spiritual kingdoms of the Church and the heavenly Jerusalem
    text: "describes the spiritual kingdoms of the Church and the heavenly Jerusalem"
  - `#jerome-to-paulinus-p20` — The wars of heretics against the Church are narrated in Kings
    text: "the smallness of the Church and the wars of heretics against the Church are narrated"
  - `#du-culte-p9` — Christ founds His Church through the apostles
    text: "He founds His Church through the apostles"
  - `#du-culte-p8` — Peter descends from the upper room and addresses the multitude; the Church is founded
    text: "addresses the multitude, astonished to hear him"
  - `#du-culte-p47` — The Holy Spirit descends to complete the edifice of the Church
    text: "the Holy Spirit will descend to complete the edifice of the Church"
  - `#du-culte-p51` — John announces to the Church its transfigurations in suffering and glory until the end of ages
    text: "the prophecy of the New Testament and of the entire future of the Church on earth"
- **Related people**: jesus-christ, peter, paul, john
- **Related places**: middle-east/palestine/jerusalem, europe/italy/rome

### Order of Preachers
- **Slug**: `religious-order/order-of-preachers`
- **Also known as**: Dominicans, O.P.
- **Description**: Catholic religious order founded by St. Dominic in 1216. Lacordaire was a member, identified as "Fr. H. D. Lacordaire, O.P."
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#preamble-p1` — Lacordaire identified as a member of the Order of Preachers
    text: "Fr. H. D. Lacordaire, O.P."
- **Related people**: lacordaire

- **Slug**: `publisher/poussielgue-rusand`

- **Occurrences**:

    text: "Paris, 1858, published by Poussielgue-Rusand"
- **Related people**: lacordaire
- **Related places**: europe/france/paris
