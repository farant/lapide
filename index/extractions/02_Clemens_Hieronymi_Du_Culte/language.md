### שְׁמוּאֵל (Samuel)
- **Slug**: `hebrew/shemuel`
- **Transliteration**: shemu'el
- **Old Hebrew**: 𐤔𐤌𐤅𐤀𐤋
- **Meaning**: "Heard of God" or "Name of God" — Hebrew name for the combined books of 1–2 Samuel (1–2 Kings in the Vulgate enumeration)
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#helmeted-prologue-p11` — Jerome identifies Samuel as the third book of the Prophets, corresponding to the first and second of Kings
    text: "Third follows Samuel, which we call the first and second of Kings."
  - `#helmeted-prologue-p2` — Jerome lists Samuel among the five books considered to be "double"
    text: "five books are considered by most to be double: Samuel, Melachim, Dibre hajamim, Ezra, Jeremiah with Cinoth, that is, with its Lamentations."
  - `#helmeted-prologue-p29` — Jerome urges the reader to read "my Samuel and my Melachim"
    text: "Read therefore first my Samuel and my Melachim -- mine, I say, mine."
- **Related**: `person/biblical/samuel`, `hebrew/melachim`

### מְלָכִים (Melachim)
- **Slug**: `hebrew/melachim`
- **Transliteration**: melakhim
- **Old Hebrew**: 𐤌𐤋𐤊𐤉𐤌
- **Meaning**: "Kings" — Hebrew name for the books of Kings (3–4 Kings in the Vulgate enumeration)
- **Root**: מלך
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#helmeted-prologue-p12` — Jerome identifies Melachim as the fourth book of the Prophets, meaning "of Kings"
    text: "Fourth, Melachim, that is, of Kings, which is contained in the third and fourth volume of Kings."
  - `#helmeted-prologue-p13` — Jerome argues it is better to say Melachim ("Kings") than Mamlachot ("Kingdoms"), because it describes one Israelite people, not many nations
    text: "And it is much better to say Melachim, that is, of Kings, than Mamlachot, that is, of Kingdoms. For it does not describe the kingdoms of many nations, but of one Israelite people, which is comprised of twelve tribes."
  - `#helmeted-prologue-p2` — Jerome lists Melachim among the five books considered double
    text: "five books are considered by most to be double: Samuel, Melachim, Dibre hajamim, Ezra, Jeremiah with Cinoth"
  - `#helmeted-prologue-p29` — Jerome calls these books his own translation
    text: "Read therefore first my Samuel and my Melachim -- mine, I say, mine."
  - `#jerome-to-paulinus-p20` — Jerome uses the Hebrew name again in his survey of the biblical books for Paulinus
    text: "Melachim, that is, the third and fourth book of Kings, from Solomon to Jeconiah, and from Jeroboam son of Nebat to Hosea"
- **Related**: `hebrew/mamlachot` (contrast), `hebrew/shemuel`, `person/biblical/solomon`

### λόγος (Logos)
- **Slug**: `greek/logos`
- **Transliteration**: logos
- **Meaning**: "Word, reason, reckoning, and the cause of each thing" — Jerome explains the multiple meanings of the Greek term as applied to Christ
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#jerome-to-paulinus-p2` — Jerome discusses the richness of the Greek word Logos, explaining it means word, reason, reckoning, and the cause through which all things subsist, all of which are rightly understood in Christ
    text: "For the Word (Logos) in Greek means many things: it is both word, and reason, and reckoning, and the cause of each thing through which all individual things that exist subsist -- all of which we rightly understand in Christ."
- **Related**: `person/biblical/john` (author of the prologue), `subject/theology/christology`
