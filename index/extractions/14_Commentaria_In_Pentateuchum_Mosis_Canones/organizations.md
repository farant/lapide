## Councils (`council/`)

### Council of Sirmium
- **Slug**: `council/council-of-sirmium`
- **Also known as**: Sirmian Council
- **Description**: Council held at Sirmium; its canon 14 defines that the one who wrestled against Jacob was the Son of God. Lapide notes its decrees are not definitions of faith nor dogmas of the Church except insofar as they condemn the heresies of Photinus; the Council was an assembly of Arians.
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#canons-p17` — Canon 14 defines the one who wrestled Jacob was the Son of God; but Lapide says its decrees are not definitions of faith and the Council was an assembly of Arians
    text: "what the Council of Sirmium, canon 14, defines — that the one who wrestled against Jacob, Genesis 32, was the Son of God"
  - `#canons-p17` — Its decrees are not definitions of faith except insofar as they condemn the heresies of Photinus; it was an assembly of Arians
    text: "the decrees of this Council are not definitions of faith, nor even dogmas of the Church, except insofar as they condemn the heresies of Photinus; for it is established that this Council was an assembly of Arians"
- **Related people**: `heresiarch/photinus`

### Lateran Council (Lateran IV)
- **Slug**: `council/lateran-iv`
- **Also known as**: Fourth Lateran Council
- **Description**: Ecumenical council; its chapter Firmiter on the Supreme Trinity is referenced for concluding two truths from Genesis 1.
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#canons-p41` — Chapter Firmiter on the Supreme Trinity: from Genesis 1, "In the beginning God created heaven and earth," the Council concludes two truths — that the world had a beginning and that nothing was produced before the world
    text: "this is gathered from the Lateran Council, chapter Firmiter, on the Supreme Trinity"
- **Related subjects**: multiple literal senses of Scripture, creation

## Church (`church/`)

### The Church
- **Slug**: `church/church-universal`
- **Also known as**: Church of Christ, the Church
- **Description**: The universal Christian Church; referenced as the body that condemns Origen's allegories, judges the Vulgate translator as the best, and that Moses was forming from the Synagogue of the Jews.
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#canons-p2` — Condemns the allegories of Origen
    text: "the Church, which here condemns the allegories of Origen"
  - `#canons-p33` — The Vulgate translator is "by the judgment of the Church ... the best of all"
    text: "who by the judgment of the Church is the best of all"
  - `#argumentum-p9` — Moses was forming a Church of God from the assembly and Synagogue of the Jews
    text: "was forming and instructing a commonwealth and Church of God from the assembly and Synagogue of the Jews"
  - `#canons-p45` — David's adultery a type of Christ's love for the Church of the Gentiles
    text: "a type of the love of Christ for the Church of the Gentiles"
- **Related people**: `scholar/origen`, `biblical/moses`
