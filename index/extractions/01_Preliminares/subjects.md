# Subjects — 01_Preliminares

## Theology

### Scripture and Tradition
- **Slug**: `theology/scripture-and-tradition`
- **Description**: The relationship between written Scripture and unwritten apostolic traditions as co-equal sources of divine revelation, both received from Christ and the Apostles under the dictation of the Holy Spirit
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#trent-decrees` ~p1 — "this truth and discipline are contained in written books and in unwritten traditions, which, received by the Apostles from the mouth of Christ Himself, or from the Apostles themselves, the Holy Spirit dictating, have come down to us, transmitted as it were from hand to hand"
  - `#trent-decrees` ~p7 — anathema against those who "knowingly and deliberately contemns the traditions aforesaid"
- **Related people**: Council Fathers of Trent
- **Related verses**: (implicit: 2 Thess 2:15)

### Canon of Scripture
- **Slug**: `theology/canon-of-scripture`
- **Description**: The definitive enumeration of the books of the Old and New Testaments recognised as canonical by the Catholic Church, including the deuterocanonical books
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#trent-decrees` ~p2-6 — full enumeration of Old and New Testament books: "the five books of Moses, to wit, Genesis, Exodus, Leviticus..." through "the Apocalypse of John the Apostle"
  - `#trent-decrees` ~p7 — "if anyone does not receive the said books entire with all their parts... let him be anathema"
  - `#preface-reader` ~p10 — III and IV Esdras and the Prayer of Manasseh placed outside the canonical series as not numbered among canonical books by Trent
- **Related people**: Council Fathers of Trent
- **Related works**: Council of Trent, Session IV decree

### Vulgate Authenticity
- **Slug**: `theology/vulgate-authenticity`
- **Description**: The declaration by the Council of Trent that the old Latin Vulgate edition, approved by centuries of use, is to be held as authentic for public readings, disputations, sermons, and expositions
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#trent-decrees` ~p8 — "the said old and Vulgate edition, which, by the long usage of so many centuries, has been approved in the Church itself, be held as authentic in public readings, disputations, sermons and expositions; and that no one may dare or presume to reject it under any pretext whatsoever"
  - `#preface-reader` ~p1 — "among so many Latin editions of the divine Scriptures He declared by a most solemn decree the sole Old and Vulgate edition... to be authentic"
  - `#preface-reader` ~p3-5 — the excellence of the Old/Italic edition (Augustine) and Jerome's translation praised by Augustine, Gregory, Isidore, and Sophronius
  - `#preface-reader` ~p6 — all major medieval theologians (Remigius through Bonaventure) used Jerome's version exclusively
- **Related people**: St. Jerome, St. Augustine, St. Gregory, St. Isidore, Sophronius, Council Fathers of Trent
- **Related works**: Council of Trent Session IV; Augustine, *De Doctrina Christiana* II

### Biblical Inspiration
- **Slug**: `theology/biblical-inspiration`
- **Description**: The doctrine that God is the single author of both Old and New Testaments, and that traditions were dictated by the Holy Spirit
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#trent-decrees` ~p1 — "receives and venerates with equal affection of piety and reverence all the books both of the Old and of the New Testament — since one God is the author of both"
  - `#trent-decrees` ~p1 — traditions "as having been dictated either by Christ's own word of mouth or by the Holy Spirit"
- **Related people**: Council Fathers of Trent

### Biblical Interpretation — Authority of the Church
- **Slug**: `theology/biblical-interpretation-church-authority`
- **Description**: The exclusive right of the Church to judge the true sense and interpretation of Scripture; private interpretation contrary to the Church's teaching or the unanimous consent of the Fathers is forbidden
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#trent-decrees` ~p9 — "no one, relying on his own prudence... shall dare to interpret the sacred Scripture contrary to that sense which holy mother Church — whose it is to judge of the true sense and interpretation of the holy Scriptures — has held and does hold; or even contrary to the unanimous consent of the Fathers"
- **Related people**: Council Fathers of Trent

### The Gospel as Source of Saving Truth
- **Slug**: `theology/gospel-source-of-truth`

## Exegesis

### Textual Criticism of the Vulgate
- **Slug**: `exegesis/textual-criticism-vulgate`
- **Description**: The scholarly enterprise of restoring the Vulgate to its original purity through comparison of ancient manuscripts, Hebrew and Greek sources, and patristic commentaries
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#preface-reader` ~p2 — "that great variety and diversity of versions could certainly have produced great confusion in the Church of God"
  - `#preface-reader` ~p2 — Jerome's testimony that "there were as many copies as there were manuscripts, since each person added or subtracted according to his own whim"
  - `#preface-reader` ~p7-9 — Popes Pius IV, Pius V, Sixtus V, Gregory XIV, and Clement VIII successively worked on correcting the Vulgate using "the most ancient manuscript codices, inspecting also the Hebrew and Greek sources of the Bible, and finally consulting the commentaries of the ancient Fathers"
  - `#preface-reader` ~p9 — deliberate choices to leave some readings unchanged: "our forefathers, who made Latin versions from the Hebrew and Greek, had a supply of better and more corrected books than those which have come down to us"
  - `#preface-reader` ~p10 — III-IV Esdras and Prayer of Manasseh excluded; no marginal notes, concordances, or variant readings added to text
  - `#preface-reader` ~p11 — permission for concordances, variant readings, and Jerome's prefaces in other editions, but "various readings be not annotated at the margin of the Text itself"
- **Related people**: St. Jerome, Pius IV, Pius V, Sixtus V, Gregory XIV, Clement VIII
- **Related works**: Clementine Vulgate

### The Italic (Old Latin) Version
- **Slug**: `exegesis/italic-old-latin`
- **Description**: The ancient Latin translation of the Bible predating Jerome, called "Italic" by Augustine, "Common and Vulgate" by Jerome, and "Old translation" by Gregory
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#preface-reader` ~p3 — "a certain most ancient Latin edition which St. Jerome calls the Common and Vulgate, St. Augustine the Italic, and St. Gregory the Old translation"
  - `#preface-reader` ~p4 — Augustine judged the Italic "more tenacious of the words while preserving the clarity of the meaning"
- **Related people**: St. Jerome, St. Augustine, St. Gregory
- **Related works**: Augustine, *De Doctrina Christiana* II

### Jerome's Translation Method
- **Slug**: `exegesis/jerome-translation-method`
- **Description**: Jerome's translation of the Bible from Hebrew and Greek, praised for truthfulness and fidelity to original sources
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#preface-reader` ~p5 — Augustine calls Jerome "most skilled in three languages" and confirms "by the testimony even of the Hebrews themselves that his translation is truthful"
  - `#preface-reader` ~p5 — Gregory says Jerome's "new" translation "rendered everything more truly from the Hebrew speech"
  - `#preface-reader` ~p5 — Isidore says it "is commonly received and approved by the Christian churches because it is clearer in words and truer in meaning"
  - `#preface-reader` ~p5 — Sophronius translated Jerome's Psalter and Prophets into Greek
- **Related people**: St. Jerome, St. Augustine, St. Gregory, St. Isidore, Sophronius

## Morals

### Meekness (Mansuetudo)
- **Slug**: `morals/meekness`
- **Description**: The virtue of gentleness and mildness of temper, by which one overcomes adversaries through steady kindness rather than force
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p14 — "Holy Scripture bestows this praise upon Moses: that he was the meekest of all mortals"
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p14 — "Saint Basil, the Christian Moses, vanquished his adversaries by his steady kindness, as Saint Gregory Nazianzen writes"
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p15-16 — Van der Burch's courtesy: "you receive everyone graciously, greet them honourably, and show to all a serene face, a ready word, and a generous spirit"
- **Related people**: Moses, St. Basil, Archbishop Van der Burch
- **Related verses**: Numbers 12:3 (implied)

### Humility
- **Slug**: `morals/humility`
- **Description**: Lowliness of self-estimation despite great learning or accomplishment; regarding oneself as foolish and inadequate before God
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p13 — St. Cyprian on St. Cornelius: "quiet and modest... out of the humility of the diffidence innate to him"
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p20 — Authbert of Cambrai "shone... with marvellous humility and holiness"
  - `#life` ~p2 — "So humble was his opinion of himself amid so vast a learning... that he would affirm: 'Truly and in my conscience, I am the most foolish of men, and the wisdom of men is not with me; I am a little child who does not know his own going out or coming in'"
- **Related people**: Cornelius a Lapide, St. Cornelius (Pope), Authbert of Cambrai
- **Related verses**: Proverbs 30:2-3 (implied: "I am the most foolish of men")

### Charity and Almsgiving

  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p18 — "From my infancy compassion grew up with me, and from my mother's womb it came forth with me" (Job)
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p19 — "nothing you do more willingly, nothing more pleasurable, than to visit hospitals and the homes of the poor and the wretched, to comfort them, to assist them with alms"
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p17 — St. Basil gave away personal inheritance to the poor (Gregory of Nyssa)
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p17 — St. Basil "opening the granaries of the wealthy with his speech and exhortation" during famine
- **Related people**: Archbishop Van der Burch, St. Basil, Gregory of Nyssa
- **Related verses**: Job 31:18 (implied)

### Constancy and Fortitude

### Poverty and Detachment from Wealth

### Love of Money as Spiritual Poison

### Reluctance to Accept Office (Nolo Episcopari)

  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p14c — "how deeply you grieved, how long you resisted, how many means of escape you sought" re: Cambrai appointment
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p11 — John Fisher refused transfer to any wealthier see
- **Related people**: Moses, St. Basil, St. Cornelius (Pope), Archbishop Van der Burch, Bl. John Fisher
- **Related verses**: Exodus 4:10-13

## Pastoral

### Episcopal Duties and Pastoral Governance
- **Slug**: `pastoral/episcopal-duties`
- **Description**: The obligations and activities proper to a bishop: preaching, hearing confessions, visitation, reforming discipline, governing clergy and laity
- **Occurrences**:
