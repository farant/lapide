## Saints

### St. Basil the Great (Basilius Magnus)
- **Slug**: `saint/basil-the-great`
- **Dates**: c. 329–379
- **Role**: Church Father, Bishop of Caesarea, Doctor of the Church, founder of Eastern monasticism
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p5 — compared to Moses as "the Moses of his age"; Gregory Nazianzen's oration quoted; his letter 140 to Libanius cited
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p5 — composed Hexaemeron on Genesis of Moses; Ambrose translated his works
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p6 — studied eloquence and philosophy at Athens with Gregory Nazianzen; spent 13 years reading Moses and Scripture (per Rufinus)
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p8 — descended from illustrious parents Basil and Emmelia; mother followed him into wilderness
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p10 — fled bishopric of Neocaesarea; feigned sickness; reluctantly made bishop (Epistle 164 cited)
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p13 — "the meekest of all mortals"; vanquished adversaries by kindness
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p14 — zeal for his people described by Rufinus; transformed the whole province
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p15 — Ephrem saw a dove whispering sermon in his ear; relieved public famine; fed the poor; gave away personal inheritance (per Gregory of Nyssa)
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p18 — "the life of Basil was for all a rule of living" (Gregory Nazianzen)
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p20 — Moses of cenobitic monks; raised monasteries throughout the East; prescribed monastic constitutions; defended monasticism against heretics (Epistle 63 cited)
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p22 — overcame Julian the Apostate; prayed before image of Our Lady with Mercury the martyr (Damascene cited)
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p23 — confronted Prefect Modestus and Emperor Valens; famous dialogue on threats of confiscation, exile, torture, death
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p25 — Cyrus Theodorus mocked Modestus with verses about Basil
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p27 — funeral mourning described by Gregory Nazianzen
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p29 — "through the single Church of Caesarea, gave light to the whole world"
  - `#life` ~p1 — Lapide in old age pursued solitude "in company with Saint Basil, Gregory, and Jerome"
- **Related works**: Hexaemeron, Epistles (140, 164, 63), monastic constitutions
- **Related subjects**: monasticism, confrontation with Arianism, pastoral care, Scripture study

### St. Jerome (Hieronymus Stridonensis)
- **Slug**: `saint/jerome`
- **Dates**: c. 342–420
- **Role**: Church Father, Doctor of the Church, translator of the Vulgate
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#preface-reader` ~p2 — testified that in his time "there were as many copies as there were manuscripts"
  - `#preface-reader` ~p3 — his translation praised; Augustine calls him "most learned man and most skilled in three languages"; Gregory praises his translation as rendering "everything more truly from the Hebrew"
  - `#preface-reader` ~p3 — Isidore prefers Hieronymian version to all others
  - `#preface-reader` ~p3 — Sophronius translated Jerome's Psalter and Prophets into Greek
  - `#preface-reader` ~p4 — theologians who used his version listed (Remigius, Bede, etc.)
  - `#preface-reader` ~p5 — celebrated as "the greatest Doctor" for Scripture interpretation
  - `#preface-reader` ~p9 — "more than once warned that this should be done, to avoid offending the people"
  - `#preface-reader` ~p11 — "prefaces of St. Jerome" mentioned as aids in other editions
  - `#life` ~p1 — Lapide pursued solitude "in company with Saint Basil, Gregory, and Jerome, whose holy Bethlehem, so earnestly sought by him in Palestine, I have found here in Rome"
- **Related works**: Vulgate, Psalter, translation of the Prophets
- **Related subjects**: Scripture translation, biblical languages, Bethlehem
