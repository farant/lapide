# Years -- 01_Preliminares

### 1572
- **Slug**: `ad/16th-century/70s/1572`
- **Events**: Heretics seize Mechlin by surprise; Van der Burch's father imprisoned and nearly killed; Duke of Alba rescues the city
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p8 (line 125) -- "For in the year 1572, when the heretics seized Mechlin by surprise, he was cast into a degrading prison, subjected to many hardships, and also endured a severe loss of fortune; and had the Duke of Alba not suddenly arrived with his army, he was already destined for death."
- **Related people**: Van der Burch's father (President of the Privy Council), Duke of Alba, Philip II
- **Related places**: Mechlin (Malines)

### 1580
- **Slug**: `ad/16th-century/80s/1580`
- **Events**: Heretics again occupy Mechlin; Van der Burch's father's house pillaged, wife held for ransom
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p8 (line 125) -- "Then in the year 1580, when the same city was again occupied by the heretics, his house was pillaged once more, and all his goods plundered, and moreover he was compelled to pay many thousands of florins to ransom his wife, who had been unable to save herself by flight."
- **Related people**: Van der Burch's father, Van der Burch's mother
- **Related places**: Mechlin (Malines)

### 1604
- **Slug**: `ad/17th-century/00s/1604`

### Pontificates referenced in `#preface-reader` (no explicit years given)

The following popes are mentioned in chronological sequence in `#preface-reader` in connection with the Vulgate revision project, but no specific years are stated in the text:

- **Pius IV** (pontificate 1559-1565) -- entrusted the Vulgate correction to select Cardinals
- **Pius V** (pontificate 1566-1572) -- carried the work forward
- **Sixtus V** (pontificate 1585-1590) -- ordered the completed work printed, then recalled it; died before finishing
- **Urban VII** (pontificate 1590, twelve days) -- mentioned only as having a twelve-day pontificate
- **Gregory XIV** (pontificate 1590-1591) -- undertook to complete the Vulgate revision after Sixtus V
- **Innocent IX** (pontificate 1591, two months) -- died before completion
- **Clement VIII** (pontificate 1592-1605) -- perfected the Vulgate edition; issued the bull dated 9 November 1592
