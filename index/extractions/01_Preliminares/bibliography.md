# Bibliography — 01_Preliminares

## Patristic Works

### Hexaemeron (St. Basil the Great)
- **Slug**: `basil/hexaemeron`
- **Author**: St. Basil the Great
- **Description**: Homilies on the six days of creation (Genesis 1). A commentary on the Genesis of Moses.
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p8 — "the Hexaemeron alone — those works which he so laboriously composed as a commentary on the Genesis of Moses"
- **Related people**: St. Basil the Great, St. Ambrose (translated it into Latin)

### De Hexaemeron / On the Work of the Six Days (St. Ambrose)
- **Slug**: `ambrose/de-hexaemeron`
- **Author**: St. Ambrose
- **Description**: Latin treatise on the six days of creation, based on St. Basil's Hexaemeron. Ambrose "gave to Latin ears not so much his own work as that of Saint Basil."
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p8 — "Saint Ambrose translated them, and gave to Latin ears not so much his own work as that of Saint Basil, in his treatise On the Work of the Six Days"
- **Related people**: St. Ambrose, St. Basil the Great

### Oration in Praise of Saint Basil (St. Gregory Nazianzen)
- **Slug**: `gregory-nazianzen/oration-praise-basil`
- **Author**: St. Gregory Nazianzen (Gregory the Theologian)
- **Description**: Funeral oration for St. Basil the Great, comparing him to Moses and extolling his virtues.
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p8 — "says his peer Blessed Gregory Nazianzen in his Oration in Praise of Saint Basil"
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p10 — "Saint Basil, the Christian Moses, vanquished his adversaries by his steady kindness, as Saint Gregory Nazianzen writes of him"
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p17 — "At the death and funeral of Saint Basil, Saint Gregory Nazianzen writes that there was so great a gathering of mourners"
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p18 — Gregory Nazianzen: "Basil, through the single Church of Caesarea, gave light to the whole world"
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p12 — Gregory Nazianzen: "the life of Basil was for all a rule of living"
- **Related people**: St. Gregory Nazianzen, St. Basil the Great

### Epistle 140 to Libanius (St. Basil the Great)
- **Slug**: `basil/epistle-140`
- **Author**: St. Basil the Great
- **Description**: Letter to Libanius the Sophist, in which Basil describes his study of Moses and Elijah.
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p8 — "Saint Basil himself acknowledges this in letter 140 to Libanius the Sophist: 'We indeed, he says, O distinguished man, converse with Moses and Elijah and such blessed men...'"
- **Related people**: St. Basil the Great, Libanius the Sophist

### Epistle 164 (St. Basil the Great)
- **Slug**: `basil/epistle-164`
- **Author**: St. Basil the Great
- **Description**: Letter in which Basil describes fleeing the bishopric of Neocaesarea.
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p10 — "Saint Basil likewise fled the bishopric of Neocaesarea, as he himself writes in Epistle 164"
- **Related people**: St. Basil the Great

### Epistle 63 (St. Basil the Great)
- **Slug**: `basil/epistle-63`
- **Author**: St. Basil the Great
- **Description**: Letter defending monasticism against heretical critics, describing monks devoted to piety and poverty.
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p12 — "to whom he replied in Epistle 63: 'We are accused,' he says, 'of this way of life too, because we have men who are monks devoted to piety...'"
- **Related people**: St. Basil the Great

### Letters of St. Cyprian — Book IV, Letter 2 to Antonianus
- **Slug**: `cyprian/letter-2-to-antonianus`
- **Author**: St. Cyprian of Carthage
- **Description**: Letter praising Pope St. Cornelius for his humble ascent to the episcopate through all the offices of the Church.
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p9 — "Saint Cyprian praises Saint Cornelius, Bishop of Rome, in book IV, letter 2 to Antonianus"
- **Related people**: St. Cyprian of Carthage, Pope St. Cornelius

### Oration on Images, I (St. John Damascene)
- **Slug**: `damascene/oration-on-images-1`
- **Author**: St. John Damascene
- **Description**: First Oration in defence of holy images, containing the account from Helladius of Basil's prayer before the image of Our Lady and the vision of the martyr Mercury slaying Julian the Apostate.
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p13 — "Damascene writes from Helladius, in his first Oration On Images: 'Basil,' he says, 'the pious, stood before the image of Our Lady...'"
- **Related people**: St. John Damascene, Helladius, St. Basil the Great, Julian the Apostate, St. Mercury (martyr)

### Ecclesiastical History (Rufinus of Aquileia)
- **Slug**: `rufinus/ecclesiastical-history`
- **Author**: Rufinus of Aquileia
- **Description**: Continuation of Eusebius's Church History. Contains account of Basil and Gregory Nazianzen studying Scripture for thirteen years after Athens, and Basil's pastoral zeal in Pontus.
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p8 — "Rufinus attests that after Saint Basil and Saint Gregory Nazianzen had studied eloquence and philosophy at Athens, they devoted thirteen years to reading and meditating upon Moses and the Sacred Scriptures"
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p11 — "Rufinus recounts the zeal and benefactions of Saint Basil toward his own people, Book II, chapter ix: 'Basil,' he says, 'journeying through the cities and countryside of Pontus...'"
- **Related people**: Rufinus of Aquileia, St. Basil the Great, St. Gregory Nazianzen

### Life of Gregory of Nyssa / On St. Basil (Gregory of Nyssa)
- **Slug**: `gregory-nyssa/on-basil`
- **Author**: St. Gregory of Nyssa
- **Description**: Works containing testimony about St. Basil, including the dove vision of St. Ephrem and Basil giving away his inheritance.
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p11 — "as Gregory of Nyssa testifies" (dove whispering to Basil while preaching)
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p11 — "Basil's own brother Gregory of Nyssa adds that at that time Saint Basil also gave away his own personal inheritance to the poor"
- **Related people**: St. Gregory of Nyssa, St. Basil the Great, St. Ephrem the Syrian

### Ecclesiastical History (Theodoretus / Theodoret of Cyrrhus)
- **Slug**: `theodoret/ecclesiastical-history`
- **Author**: Theodoret of Cyrrhus
- **Description**: Church history. Book IV, chapter 17 contains the account of Basil's encounter with Demosthenes, prefect of the imperial kitchen.
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#dedicatory-letter` ~p14 — "Theodoretus, Book IV, chapter 17, adds this: There was present also, he says, a certain man named Demosthenes, prefect of the imperial kitchen..."
- **Related people**: Theodoret of Cyrrhus, St. Basil the Great, Demosthenes (imperial kitchen prefect)

### De Doctrina Christiana / On Christian Doctrine (St. Augustine)
- **Slug**: `augustine/de-doctrina-christiana`
- **Author**: St. Augustine of Hippo
- **Description**: Treatise on biblical interpretation and Christian teaching. Book II contains Augustine's praise of the Italic (Old Latin) edition of the Bible.
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#preface-reader` ~p3 — "there stands the splendid testimony of St. Augustine in the second book of On Christian Doctrine, where he judged that, among all the Latin editions then circulating in great numbers, the Italic should be preferred"
- **Related people**: St. Augustine of Hippo

### Letters/Testimonies of St. Augustine on Jerome
- **Slug**: `augustine/testimony-on-jerome`
- **Author**: St. Augustine of Hippo
- **Description**: Augustine's commendation of Jerome as "a most learned man and most skilled in three languages," confirming Jerome's translation as truthful by the testimony of the Hebrews.
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#preface-reader` ~p4 — "St. Augustine calls him a most learned man and most skilled in three languages, and confirms by the testimony even of the Hebrews themselves that his translation is truthful"
- **Related people**: St. Augustine of Hippo, St. Jerome

### Testimony of St. Gregory the Great on Jerome's Translation
- **Slug**: `gregory-great/on-jerome-translation`
- **Author**: St. Gregory the Great (Pope Gregory I)
- **Description**: Gregory's praise of Jerome's translation as rendering "everything more truly from the Hebrew speech."
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#preface-reader` ~p3 — "St. Gregory praises him so highly that he says his translation (which he calls the new one) has rendered everything more truly from the Hebrew speech"
  - `#preface-reader` ~p3 — "St. Gregory the Old translation" (reference to Gregory's term for the pre-Jerome Latin version)
- **Related people**: St. Gregory the Great, St. Jerome

### Testimony of St. Isidore on Jerome's Version
- **Slug**: `isidore/on-jerome-version`
- **Author**: St. Isidore of Seville
- **Description**: Isidore's preference for the Hieronymian version over all others, affirming it as "clearer in words and truer in meaning" and commonly received by the Christian churches.
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#preface-reader` ~p4 — "St. Isidore, moreover, in more than one place prefers the Hieronymian version to all others and affirms that it is commonly received and approved by the Christian churches"
- **Related people**: St. Isidore of Seville, St. Jerome

### Sophronius's Greek Translation of the Psalter and Prophets
- **Slug**: `sophronius/greek-translation-psalter-prophets`
- **Author**: Sophronius of Jerusalem
- **Description**: Greek translation of the Psalter and the Prophets, rendered from Jerome's Latin version into elegant Greek. Evidence that Jerome's translation was approved by both Latins and Greeks.
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#preface-reader` ~p4 — "Sophronius also, a most erudite man, observing that St. Jerome's translation was highly approved not only by Latins but also by Greeks, valued it so greatly that he translated the Psalter and the Prophets from Jerome's version into elegant Greek"
- **Related people**: Sophronius of Jerusalem, St. Jerome

## Scriptural / Biblical Texts

### The Vulgate Bible (Latin Vulgate)
- **Slug**: `bible/vulgate`
- **Author**: St. Jerome (principal translator/editor); earlier anonymous translators for the Old Latin (Vetus Latina)
- **Description**: The official Latin translation of the Bible declared authentic by the Council of Trent. Contains portions from Jerome's translation from Hebrew/Greek and portions from the older "Common and Vulgate" (Italic/Old Latin) translation. The Clementine edition (1592) was the corrected Vatican printing.
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#trent-decrees` — "the said old and Vulgate edition, which, by the long usage of so many centuries, has been approved in the Church itself, be held as authentic"
  - `#preface-reader` ~p1 — "He declared by a most solemn decree the sole Old and Vulgate edition... to be authentic"
  - `#preface-reader` ~p3 — "received in part from the translation or emendation of St. Jerome, and in part retained from a certain most ancient Latin edition which St. Jerome calls the Common and Vulgate, St. Augustine the Italic, and St. Gregory the Old translation"
  - `#preface-reader` ~p5-8 — extensive discussion of the editorial history under Popes Pius IV, Pius V, Sixtus V, Gregory XIV, Urban VII, Innocent IX, and Clement VIII
  - `#preface-reader` ~p9 — "the Old and Vulgate edition of the Holy Scripture, corrected with as much diligence as could be applied"
  - `#clement-viii` — "the text of the Vulgate edition of the sacred Bible, restored with the greatest labors"
- **Related people**: St. Jerome, St. Augustine, St. Gregory the Great, Pius IV, Pius V, Sixtus V, Gregory XIV, Urban VII, Innocent IX, Clement VIII

## Ecclesiastical / Conciliar Documents

### Decrees of the Council of Trent, Session IV
- **Slug**: `council/trent-session-iv`
- **Author**: Council of Trent
- **Description**: Two decrees: (1) "On the Canonical Scriptures" — defining the canon of Old and New Testament books and affirming unwritten Traditions; (2) "On the Edition and Use of the Sacred Books" — declaring the Vulgate authentic, forbidding private interpretation contrary to the Church or unanimous consent of the Fathers, and regulating printing of sacred texts.
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#trent-decrees` — full text of both decrees
  - `#preface-reader` ~p1 — "the sacred Tridentine Synod"
  - `#preface-reader` ~p5 — "the same most holy Synod of Trent wisely added by its decree"
  - `#preface-reader` ~p10 — "the sacred Tridentine Synod did not number among the canonical books"
- **Related people**: Legates of the Apostolic See

### Lateran Council decree (referenced)
- **Slug**: `council/lateran`
- **Author**: Lateran Council (Fifth Lateran Council, likely)
- **Description**: Referenced for the fine imposed on unauthorized printers of sacred texts. The Trent decree references "the fine imposed in the canon of the most recent Lateran Council."
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#trent-decrees` — "under penalty of anathema and the fine imposed in the canon of the most recent Lateran Council"
- **Related people**: N/A

### Bull of Pope Clement VIII on the Vulgate (Cum Sacrorum)
- **Slug**: `clement-viii/bull-vulgate`
- **Author**: Pope Clement VIII
- **Description**: Papal bull dated 9 November 1592 establishing the corrected Vatican edition of the Vulgate as the authoritative text. Forbids unauthorized printing for ten years, requires future editions to conform to the Vatican copy, and imposes excommunication on violators.
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#clement-viii` — full text of the bull
  - `#preface-reader` ~p9 — "Receive therefore, Christian reader, with the approval of the same Clement, Supreme Pontiff, from the Vatican Press..."
- **Related people**: Pope Clement VIII, M. Vestrius Barbianus (secretary)

## Medieval and Later Scholars Referenced in the Preface

The Preface to the Reader (`#preface-reader` ~p5) lists scholars who used Jerome's version over the preceding nine centuries, effectively making all other Latin versions obsolete. These are mentioned as users/endorsers of the Vulgate rather than as authors of specific cited works, but are significant references:

### Scholars who used St. Jerome's Version (listed collectively)
- **Slug**: `preface/jerome-users`
- **Authors**: Remigius (of Auxerre), Bede (the Venerable), Rabanus (Maurus), Haymo (of Halberstadt), Anselm (of Canterbury or of Laon), Peter Damian, Richard (of St. Victor), Hugh (of St. Victor), Bernard (of Clairvaux), Rupert (of Deutz), Peter Lombard, Alexander (of Hales), Albert (the Great), Thomas (Aquinas), Bonaventure
- **Description**: Collective reference to the most learned medieval theologians and exegetes who exclusively used Jerome's version, causing all other Latin versions to "slip from the hands of theologians and become utterly obsolete."
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#preface-reader` ~p5 — "Furthermore, the most learned men who came after — Remigius, Bede, Rabanus, Haymo, Anselm, Peter Damian, Richard, Hugh, Bernard, Rupert, Peter Lombard, Alexander, Albert, Thomas, Bonaventure, and all the others who have flourished in the Church over these nine hundred years — have used St. Jerome's version"
- **Related people**: All listed above, St. Jerome
