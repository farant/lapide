- **Also known as**: Jesuits, S.J., the Society

### Council of Trent
- **Slug**: `council/council-of-trent`
- **Also known as**: Tridentine Synod, sacred Tridentine Synod, Holy Synod
- **Description**: Ecumenical council of the Catholic Church held at Trent (1545-1563); its Session IV decrees on canonical Scripture and the Vulgate are reproduced in full in this document.
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#trent-decrees` — Header for the reproduced decrees
    text: "DECREES OF THE COUNCIL OF TRENT (SESSION IV)."
  - `#trent-decrees-p3` — The Council described as lawfully assembled in the Holy Spirit with three legates of the Apostolic See presiding
    text: "The sacred, œcumenical and general Council of Trent, lawfully assembled in the Holy Spirit, with the three legates of the Apostolic See presiding over it"
  - `#trent-decrees-p7` — Decree of anathema against those who do not receive the canonical books entire
    text: "if anyone does not receive the said books entire with all their parts... let him be anathema"
  - `#trent-decrees-p9` — Decree on the Vulgate edition to be held as authentic
    text: "ordains and declares that the said old and Vulgate edition... be held as authentic in public readings, disputations, sermons and expositions"
  - `#trent-decrees-p11` — Decree regulating printing of sacred books, referencing the Lateran Council
    text: "penalty of anathema and the fine imposed in the canon of the most recent Lateran Council"
  - `#preface-reader-p2` — The Tridentine Synod declared the Vulgate authentic by solemn decree
    text: "Among the many and great benefits which God has bestowed upon His Church through the sacred Tridentine Synod"
  - `#preface-reader-p9` — The Synod of Trent decreed the Vulgate be printed as correctly as possible
    text: "the same most holy Synod of Trent wisely added by its decree"
  - `#preface-reader-p14` — The Council's intent was to restore the Vulgate, not create a new edition
    text: "it was not the intention of the sacred congregation... to produce some new edition... but rather to restore the Old and Vulgate Latin edition itself"
- **Related organizations**: church/apostolic-see
- **Related places**: europe/italy/trent
