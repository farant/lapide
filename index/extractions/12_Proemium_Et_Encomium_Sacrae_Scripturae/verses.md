## Genesis

### Genesis 1 (general reference)
- **Slug**: `genesis/1`
- **Description**: The creation account — "Let there be light," etc. — cited as the foundation for the doctrine of creation.
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#chapter-ii-p19` — Lapide notes that the contents of St. Thomas's First Part concerning God's essence, predestination, angels, man, and the work of the six days "clearly derives from Genesis chapter 1"
    text: "all of which clearly derives from Genesis chapter 1"
  - `#utility-i-p2` — Genesis 1 cited as the source of our knowledge of creation
    text: "Let there be light, let there be luminaries, let us make man"
- **Related people**: Moses
- **Related subjects**: Creation, six days of creation, faith

### Genesis 14:15 (general reference)
- **Slug**: `genesis/14/15`
- **Description**: Reference to the city of Laish being called Dan — a later editorial insertion into the Mosaic text.
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#moses-most-ancient-p6` — Lapide notes that at Genesis 14:15 the city of Laish is called Dan, though the name postdates Moses
    text: "at Genesis 14:15, the city of Laish is called Dan, although it was called Dan long after the times of Moses"
- **Related people**: Moses, Joshua
- **Related subjects**: Mosaic authorship, editorial additions to the Pentateuch
