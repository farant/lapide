### De Genesi ad Litteram (On the Literal Meaning of Genesis)
- **Slug**: `augustine/de-genesi-ad-litteram`
- **Also known as**: On Genesis Literally; On the Literal Interpretation of Genesis
- **Description**: Augustine's mature exegetical work on Genesis, treating the creation account in its literal and historical sense across twelve books.
- **Year**: ~401–415
- **Occurrences**:
  - `#in-the-beginning-p3` — Cited (Book I, ch. 1) on "in the beginning" meaning "in the first origin of time"
    text: "St. Augustine, book I of On the Literal Interpretation of Genesis, ch. 1; Ambrose and Basil, homily 1 on the Hexameron: "In the beginning," they say, that is, in the first origin or start, not of eternity, not of aeviternity, but of time and of the world"
  - `#heaven-and-earth-p3` — Cited (Book I, ch. XIV) on prime matter endowed with form in the same instant of time
    text: "the same Augustine, book I of On Genesis Literally, chapter XIV, adds that this matter was endowed and adorned with its form in the very same instant of time."
  - `#verse-2-p11` — Cited (Book I, ch. 7) on the Holy Spirit borne over the waters as a craftsman's will over his work
    text: "just as the will and idea of a craftsman is borne over the things to be fashioned, says St. Augustine, Book I of On Genesis Literally, ch. 7."
  - `#verse-5-p7` — Cited (Book IV) as wanting the six days understood mystically, holding all things created simultaneously
    text: "St. Augustine, Book IV of On Genesis Literally, and Book XI of The City of God, ch. 7, wants these days to be understood mystically; for he seems to hold that all things were created simultaneously by God on the first day"
  - `#verse-20-p13` — Cited (Book III, ch. 3) on fish made from denser water, birds from more rarefied water
    text: "St. Augustine responds, Book III On the Literal Meaning of Genesis, chapter 3, and St. Thomas, Part I, Question 71, article 1, that fish were made from denser water; but birds from more rarefied water, which approaches the nature of air."
  - `#verse-22-p4` — Cited (Book III, ch. 13) on the blessing of fish/birds understood for land animals also
    text: "as St. Augustine rightly observes, Book III On the Literal Meaning of Genesis, chapter 13, what is expressed in one case ought equally to be understood in the other similar case."
  - `#verse-24-p7` — Cited (Book III, ch. 14) on tiny animals created potentially in seminal principles
    text: "so St. Augustine, Book III On the Literal Meaning of Genesis, chapter 14, although St. Basil here in Homily 7 seems to teach the contrary."
  - `#verse-24-p5` — Cited (Book III, ch. 16) on whether animals were hostile to each other before Adam's sin
    text: "St. Augustine, Book III On the Literal Meaning of Genesis, chapter 16, although Augustine himself seems to retract this in Book I of the Retractions, chapter 10"
  - `#verse-14-p18` — Cited (Book XIII, ch. 13, in the Unfinished Work) on stars as signs of unchangeable eternity
    text: "St. Augustine, book XIII On Genesis Literally, ch. 13, in the Unfinished Work"
  - `#verse-31-p4` — Cited (Book III, ch. 24) on why God did not say "it was good" after man's creation
    text: "St. Augustine brings two other reasons in Book 3 of On the Literal Meaning of Genesis, ch. 24."
  - `#verse-26-p25` — Cited (Book VI, ch. 12) on the body not properly bearing the image of God but reflecting it
    text: "So St. Augustine, Book VI of On Genesis Literally, chapter 12, and Bernard, Sermon 24 on the Song of Songs."
- **Related people**: Augustine of Hippo
- **Related subjects**: Genesis, creation, patristic exegesis
