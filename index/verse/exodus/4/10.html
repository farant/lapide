Exodus 4:10,13

Vulgate
Obsecro, Domine, non sum eloquens ab heri et nudiustertius; et ex quo locutus es ad servum tuum, impeditoris et tardioris linguae sum... Obsecro, Domine, mitte quem missurus es.
English
I beseech you, Lord, I am not eloquent, neither heretofore nor since you have spoken to your servant; but I am slow of speech and of tongue... I beseech you, Lord, send whom you will send.

Moses' reluctance to accept God's call to lead Israel out of Egypt, declining the commission so persistently that he provoked God's anger. Lapide cites this passage explicitly ("In Exodus iv he says") as a parallel to Van der Burch's own reluctance to accept the archbishopric of Cambrai, establishing the pattern that the truly worthy candidate resists the honour.
