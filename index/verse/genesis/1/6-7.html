    Genesis 1:6-7

    Description
    Let there be a firmament made amidst the waters, and let it divide the waters from the waters — the work of the second day.

    References in Commentary

    • Genesis 1 — Vulgate Text P2 — Full Vulgate text quoted
      "6. And God said: Let there be a firmament made amidst the waters, and let it divide the waters from the waters. 7. And God made a firmament, and divided the waters that were under the firmament from those that were above the firmament. And it was so."
    • Genesis 1 — Verse 6 P2 — Firmament (Hebrew rakia) means to spread out and solidify; the firmament is the starry heaven
      "6. LET THERE BE A FIRMAMENT IN THE MIDST OF THE WATERS, AND LET IT DIVIDE THE WATERS FROM THE WATERS. -- "Firmament" is called in Hebrew rakia"
    • Genesis 1 — Verse 6 P11 — The firmament is the starry heaven and all celestial orbs; true waters exist above the heavens
      "But I say that the firmament is the starry heaven and all the celestial orbs neighboring it, both lower and upper up to the empyrean."
