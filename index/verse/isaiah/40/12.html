    Isaiah 40:12

    Description
    God is He who hangs the mass of the earth by three fingers, who has measured the waters in the hollow of His hand.

    Chapter I (The Six Days of Creation)

    • God (Elohim): Thirteen Definitions — Eleventh definition of God: He who holds the earth, measures the waters, weighs the heavens
      "God is He who hangs the mass of the earth by three fingers, who has measured the waters in the hollow of His hand, and weighed the heavens with a span. Behold, the nations before Him are like a drop from a bucket, and are counted as a speck on a balance, the islands like fine dust. And Lebanon is not sufficient for burning, and its animals are not sufficient for a holocaust. He who sits upon the circle of the earth, and its inhabitants are like grasshoppers"
    • He Created — The world is like a drop from a bucket compared to God
      "the world in relation to God is like a drop from a bucket, and like a drop of dew, as is said in Isaiah XL, 15"
