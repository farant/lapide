A verse on divine providence attributed to "the Sage," applied by Lapide to Van der Burch's pastoral governance. The verse becomes the basis for Van der Burch's motto, "Sweetly and Strongly" (Suaviter et fortiter), encapsulating the ideal pastoral method of combining gentleness with firmness.
