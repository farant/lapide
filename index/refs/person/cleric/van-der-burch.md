Archbishop and Duke of Cambrai, dedicatee of Lapide's entire Pentateuch commentary. The dedicatory letter systematically parallels his life and career with those of Moses and Basil the Great. He rose through the ranks from canon and priest to Dean of the Metropolitan Church of Mechlin, Vicar-General, Bishop of Ghent (where his people called him "our angel"), and finally Archbishop of Cambrai -- each promotion accepted reluctantly. His courtesy, charity, and constancy in defense of discipline are praised at length.
