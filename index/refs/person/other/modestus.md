Prefect of Emperor Valens who confronted Basil the Great, pressing him to conform to the emperor's Arian religion. The dialogue between them is one of the most celebrated exchanges in patristic literature: Basil dismisses threats of confiscation, exile, torture, and death in turn, leaving the prefect to report: "We have been vanquished by the Bishop of this Church." Later, when ill, Modestus was compelled to beg for Basil's help -- an irony mocked by the poet Cyrus Theodorus in verse.
