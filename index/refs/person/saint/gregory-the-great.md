Last of the four great Latin Doctors of the Church and one of the most important popes in history. In the Preliminares he appears as a witness endorsing Jerome's Vulgate translation, praising it as rendering "everything more truly from the Hebrew speech" and declaring it "most worthy that full faith be placed in it."
