Bishop of Seville and Doctor of the Church, known as the last of the Latin Fathers and for his encyclopedic Etymologiae. In the Preliminares he appears as a witness endorsing Jerome's Vulgate, affirming that it is "commonly received and approved by the Christian churches because it is clearer in words and truer in meaning."
