One of the four great Latin Doctors of the Church. Best known for his translation of the Bible into Latin (the Vulgate), which became the standard text of Western Christendom. Praised by Augustine as "most learned man and most skilled in three languages," by Gregory as rendering "everything more truly from the Hebrew," and by Isidore as the version "commonly received and approved by the Christian churches." Spent his later years in a monastic cell in Bethlehem.
