One of the three Cappadocian Fathers and a towering figure in Eastern Christianity. Studied at Athens with Gregory Nazianzen, spent thirteen years immersed in Scripture, and became Bishop of Caesarea. Founded Eastern monasticism through his monastic constitutions, confronted the Emperor Valens and Prefect Modestus with legendary meekness, and is called by Gregory Nazianzen "the Moses of his age." His Hexaemeron on Genesis was translated into Latin by St. Ambrose.
