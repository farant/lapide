Lord Chancellor of England under Henry VIII and martyr for the Catholic faith. Canonized in 1935; the text (written c. 1616) calls him "Blessed Thomas More." Lapide cites him as an exemplar of incorruptibility in public life, noting that after fifty years of service he never increased his annual income to seventy gold pieces.
