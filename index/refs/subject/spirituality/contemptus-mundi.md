Lapide's devotional writings express a radical contemptus mundi: God revealed Himself to Lapide's mind "that I might esteem and seek You alone, and might lightly reckon and disdain all other things as mean, empty and fleeting." This contempt for earthly things was nourished by "the unceasing contemplation of blessed eternity," producing a life aimed at nothing other than "the enduring will, praise, and glory of Christ."
