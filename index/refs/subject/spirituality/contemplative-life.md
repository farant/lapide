Lapide describes his old age as "playing the part of Mary Magdalene," sitting at Christ's feet to "drink in the words of life." He flees courts and shores, pursuing "a solitude and retreat that is pleasant to me and not unprofitable to others, in company with Saint Basil, Gregory, and Jerome." His cell is "more faithful and dearer than all the earth" and seems "a very heaven on earth." The contemplative life is contrasted with his earlier active ministry ("once in my younger days I played Martha"), and Moses' forty years tending sheep in contemplation before assuming pastoral office provides the Old Testament model.
