The biography records that a desire for martyrdom was "divinely implanted from his very first novitiate" and that Lapide "unceasingly besought that crown for himself with all his vows." He nearly attained it in 1604 when Dutch cavalry attacked the shrine of Our Lady of Aspromont on the feast of the Nativity of Our Lady. His prayer to the four prophets (Isaiah, Jeremiah, Ezekiel, Daniel) explicitly asks for "the fourth martyrdom, that of blood," distinguishing it from the three martyrdoms he has already endured: religious life, sickness, and studies.
