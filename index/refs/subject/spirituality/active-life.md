Lapide identifies his earlier years of teaching Scripture at Louvain and Rome with "playing Martha" -- the active life of ministry and service. This is not deprecated but presented as a necessary earlier stage: Moses likewise spent forty years in Pharaoh's court and then took up the pastorate at eighty. Lapide's transition from active teaching to contemplative writing mirrors the movement from Martha to Mary.
