Divine providence is a recurring theme in the Preliminares, appearing in three distinct contexts: the providential arrangement by which Lapide and Van der Burch became friends, the governance of the world described through Wisdom 8:1, and God's preservation of Lapide from death during the 1604 cavalry attack. Van der Burch is exhorted to imitate this providence, combining sweetness with strength.
