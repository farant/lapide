The Council of Trent declared that saving truth and moral discipline are contained both in written books (Scripture) and in unwritten traditions received from the Apostles, "the Holy Spirit dictating." Both are to be received and venerated "with equal affection of piety and reverence," and an anathema is pronounced against those who knowingly and deliberately reject these traditions.
