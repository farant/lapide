The errors of pagan philosophers (Plato, Aristotle, Pythagoras, et al.) which demonstrate the insufficiency of natural reason without divine revelation, and Scripture's role in correcting them. Plato taught God is corporeal and the soul of the world; Aristotle denied providence and the soul's immortality; Democritus and Empedocles confessed nothing can be known.
