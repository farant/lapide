Trent declared the Vulgate, "approved by the long usage of so many centuries," to be the sole authentic edition for all public ecclesiastical use. The preface to the reader traces the Vulgate's history from the pre-Jerome "Italic" translation praised by Augustine through Jerome's own version praised by Gregory, Isidore, and Sophronius, to its exclusive adoption by medieval theologians from Remigius through Bonaventure. The editorial process of restoring the Vulgate to purity under Popes Pius IV through Clement VIII is documented at length.
