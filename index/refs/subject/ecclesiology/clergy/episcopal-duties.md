The dedicatory letter builds an extensive portrait of the ideal bishop using Moses as the primary model and Van der Burch as the living exemplar. The bishop's duties include: giving religious formation to his household, hearing confessions, attending psalmody, assisting the needy, conducting pastoral visitations, restoring ecclesiastical discipline, correcting or removing unworthy clergy, visiting hospitals and the homes of the poor, and governing "nearly eight hundred parishes." Basil's pastoral transformation of Pontus provides the patristic model. The method is "Sweetly and Strongly" (Suaviter et fortiter), from Wisdom 8:1.
