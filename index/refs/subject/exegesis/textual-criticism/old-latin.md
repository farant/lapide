The preface to the reader discusses the pre-Jerome Latin Bible that circulated widely in the Western Church. Augustine judged this Italic version "more tenacious of the words while preserving the clarity of the meaning." Jerome himself called it "the Common and Vulgate" edition, and Gregory the Great referred to it as "the Old translation." This version was eventually superseded by Jerome's translation from Hebrew and Greek, but its existence is important for understanding the textual history behind the Clementine Vulgate.
