The preface to the reader provides a detailed account of the Vulgate's editorial history: Pope Pius IV began the work of correction after Trent's decree, Pius V continued it, Sixtus V advanced it considerably but died before completion, Gregory XIV took it up again, and Clement VIII brought it to fruition. The method involved comparing "the most ancient manuscript codices, inspecting also the Hebrew and Greek sources of the Bible, and finally consulting the commentaries of the ancient Fathers." Some readings were deliberately left unchanged where the original translators were judged to have had better manuscripts than those surviving.
