Typology is the dominant exegetical method of the dedicatory letter. Moses serves as the primary type of the Christian bishop: shepherd of both sheep and men, wielder of both civil and sacred authority, reluctant yet obedient to God's call. Melchizedek, Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob are further types in whom priestly and royal authority were joined. The typology is then applied living: Van der Burch is "our Moses of the Low Countries" and Basil is "the Moses of his age." In the Preliminares this method is applied primarily to episcopal office, but the Genesis commentary extends it to Christological and sacramental typology.
