Marian devotion appears in several contexts. Basil prayed before an image of Our Lady on which the martyr Mercury was also depicted, and received a vision foretelling Julian the Apostate's death. Lapide's near-martyrdom took place at "the shrine of Our Lady of Aspromont, renowned for miracles," and he was preserved "by the aid of Our Lady, whom he implored with an urgent vow." The biography credits Mary with calling Lapide into the Jesuits and directing, aiding, and instructing him throughout his life. The feast of the Nativity of Our Lady (September 8) is noted as the date of the Aspromont attack.
