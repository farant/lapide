Obedience is a defining virtue of Lapide's life: it "was always dearer to him than life," and it was obedience alone that led him to publish his commentaries, which he would otherwise have "condemned to eternal silence." Van der Burch likewise submitted to the pastoral office despite wishing to withdraw, and "discharged both offices with such exactitude that neither the choir ever missed its Dean nor the diocese its Vicar."
