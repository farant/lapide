Constancy under persecution is illustrated through a gallery of examples. Van der Burch's father "stood firm and unshaken in loyalty to his Prince" amid the upheavals of the Dutch Revolt. Thomas More held the highest offices for fifty years without enriching himself. The most dramatic instance is Basil's confrontation with the prefect Modestus, who threatens confiscation, exile, torture, and death -- Basil dismisses each: "none touches us." St. Odo of Cambrai chose exile over receiving staff and ring from Emperor Henry IV.
