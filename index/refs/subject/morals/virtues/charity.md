Charity is portrayed at its most radical in Moses, who loved his people so greatly "he prayed to be blotted out of the book of life" (Exodus 32:32). Basil likewise gave away his personal inheritance to the poor and opened the granaries of the wealthy during famine. Van der Burch's charity is described as innate ("from my infancy compassion grew up with me," quoting Job 31:18), expressed through restoring churches, visiting hospitals, and spending both Church revenues and personal patrimony on works of mercy.
