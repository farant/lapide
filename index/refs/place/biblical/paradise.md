The garden where God placed Adam and Eve after creation. Lapide affirms in Canon 1 that Moses' narrations about paradise, Adam, Eve, and the creation are to be taken historically and properly, against Origen's purely allegorical reading.

## References in Commentary

- `14_Commentaria_In_Pentateuchum_Mosis_Canones.html#canons-p2-s-bc704d1` — Things Moses narrates about paradise are historical, not allegorical
  text: "the things he narrates about paradise, Adam, Eve, and the creation of all things completed successively over the space of six days, etc., are to be taken historically and properly, as they sound."
