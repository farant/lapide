Greek epithet for the book of Ecclesiasticus (Sirach), meaning "all virtue." Lapide explains this etymologically as signifying comprehensive moral wisdom.

  text: "Ecclesiasticus embraces these topics as well — whence it was called Panaretos, as if you were to say, 'all virtue.'"
