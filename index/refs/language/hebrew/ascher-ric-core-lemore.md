Hebrew proverbial adage cited by Lapide to warn against credulity toward philosophers. "He who easily and rashly trusts a teacher, distrusts the Creator."

## References in Commentary

- `12_Proemium_Et_Encomium_Sacrae_Scripturae.html#chapter-ii-p11-s-2dbf3e3` — Lapide cites this Hebrew adage after recounting the errors of Pythagoras on metempsychosis
  text: "Is not the well-known Hebrew adage most true here: ascher ric core lemore lo omen lebore, that is, 'He who easily and rashly trusts a teacher, distrusts the Creator'?"
