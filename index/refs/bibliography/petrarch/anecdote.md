Petrarch's account of Robert, King of Sicily, declaring that letters are dearer to him than his kingdom.

## References in Commentary

- `12_Proemium_Et_Encomium_Sacrae_Scripturae.html#utility-iv-p7-s-ae521b7` — Petrarch cited: Robert of Sicily swore letters dearer than his kingdom
  text: "Francis Petrarch relates that 250 years ago, Robert, King of Sicily, was so delighted with letters, especially sacred ones, that he said to him under oath: "I swear to you, Petrarch, that letters are far dearer to me than my kingdom, and if I had to be deprived of one or the other, I would more calmly part with the crown than with letters.""
