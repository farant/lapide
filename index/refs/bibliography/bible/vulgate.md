The Latin Vulgate is the official Bible of the Catholic Church, declared authentic by the Council of Trent (Session IV, 1546). It is a composite text: portions derive from St. Jerome's new translation from the Hebrew and Greek (completed c. 405), while other parts preserve the older "Common and Vulgate" edition (the Italic or Old Latin), which Augustine praised. The Clementine edition of 1592, promulgated by Pope Clement VIII, established the definitive corrected text, superseding the earlier Sixtine edition. The Preliminares discusses its authority, composition, and history at length across the Trent decrees, the preface to the reader, and the Clement VIII sections.
