Spirituality

  • Active Life — The life of pastoral service, teaching, and outward ministry, exemplified by Martha's hospitality to Christ
  • Contemplative Life — The life of prayer, study, and solitary communion with God, exemplified by Mary Magdalene sitting at Christ's feet
  • Contemptus Mundi — The spiritual disposition of esteeming and seeking God alone while reckoning all worldly things as mean, empty, and fleeting
  • Desire for Martyrdom — Lapide's lifelong burning desire for the martyrdom of blood, divinely implanted from his novitiate and nearly fulfilled at Aspromont in 1604
