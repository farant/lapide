Censorship and Regulation of Sacred Texts

Description
Ecclesiastical regulation of the printing, sale, and distribution of the Bible and religious books, including the requirement of prior approval and penalties for violations

Trent decreed that sacred Scripture be "printed as correctly as possible" and that no books on sacred matters be printed without the author's name, prior examination and approval by the ordinary, and (for regulars) permission from their superiors. Clement VIII's bull of 1592 established a ten-year Vatican printing monopoly for the Vulgate, with penalties including "the sentence of major excommunication ipso facto." The Preliminares includes both the Trent decrees and the Clement VIII bull, plus the censor's approval and imprimatur from Lapide's Jesuit superiors.
