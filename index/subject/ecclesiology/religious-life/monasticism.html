Monasticism

Description
The cenobitic life of poverty, chastity, and obedience; its biblical roots in the Nazirites; its defence against heretical attacks

Basil is called "the Moses of cenobitic monks" who "raised up monasteries throughout the whole East and prescribed monastic constitutions for them." His Epistle 63, quoted at length, defends monasticism against heretical attacks: monks are men "who have renounced the world and all its cares," who "carry about in their bodies the mortification of Jesus" and "each taking up his own cross follows the Lord." Basil traces the practice to Egypt, Palestine, and Mesopotamia, noting that women too have "happily attained an equal rule of life." The Old Testament root is in Moses' institution of the Nazirites.
