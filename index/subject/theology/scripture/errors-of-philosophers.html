    Errors of the Philosophers

    Description
    The errors of pagan philosophers demonstrating the insufficiency of natural reason without divine revelation, and Scripture's role in correcting them

    The errors of pagan philosophers (Plato, Aristotle, Pythagoras, et al.) which demonstrate the insufficiency of natural reason without divine revelation, and Scripture's role in correcting them. Plato taught God is corporeal and the soul of the world; Aristotle denied providence and the soul's immortality; Democritus and Empedocles confessed nothing can be known.

    References in Commentary

    • Proemium Et Encomium Sacrae Scripturae — Chapter Ii P9 — Plato's eight errors: God is corporeal, God is the soul of the world, souls pre-existed the body, knowledge is recollection, wives in common, falsehood as remedy, cyclical return of ages.
      "the grossest errors of Plato are eight in number: for example, that Plato teaches that God is corporeal; that God is the soul of the world"
    • Proemium Et Encomium Sacrae Scripturae — Chapter Ii P12 — Aristotle fixes the First Mover to the East, asserts natural necessity, the world's eternity, denies future contingents and immortality of the soul.
      "Aristotle... fixes the First Mover to the East; asserts that He moves by fate and natural necessity; that this world is eternal; that there is no determinate truth of future contingents"
    • Proemium Et Encomium Sacrae Scripturae — Chapter Ii P13 — Democritus and Empedocles confess nothing can be known; Socrates says he knows only that he knows nothing; Arcesilas says not even that can be known.
      "Democritus and Empedocles straightforwardly confessed that nothing can truly be known by us. Socrates used to say that he knew only this: that he knew nothing"
    • Proemium Et Encomium Sacrae Scripturae — Chapter I P4 — The academy of Plato knew nothing of faith, hope, religion, martyrdom, virginity, or divine virtues; Socrates, Pythagoras, Aristotle are children before Scripture.
      "these things lay hidden from the ancient wise men, though endowed with an almost miraculous and prodigious power of understanding; the academy of Plato knew nothing of these things, here all the school of Pythagoras is silent"
