Canon of Scripture

Description
The definitive enumeration of the books of the Old and New Testaments recognised as canonical by the Catholic Church, including the deuterocanonical books

The Council of Trent, Session IV, defined the complete canon of Scripture: the Pentateuch, historical books (including Tobit, Judith, and Maccabees), wisdom literature (including Wisdom and Ecclesiasticus), the prophets, four Gospels, Acts, Pauline and Catholic Epistles, and the Apocalypse. An anathema is pronounced against anyone who does not receive these books entire with all their parts. The preface to the reader notes that 3-4 Esdras and the Prayer of Manasseh were placed outside the canonical series.
