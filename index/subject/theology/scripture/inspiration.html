Biblical Inspiration

Description
The doctrine that God is the single author of both Old and New Testaments, and that apostolic traditions were dictated by the Holy Spirit

The Council of Trent affirmed that "one God is the author of both" Testaments and that the Church receives and venerates all canonical books "with equal affection of piety and reverence." Traditions not committed to writing were received "as having been dictated either by Christ's own word of mouth or by the Holy Spirit."
