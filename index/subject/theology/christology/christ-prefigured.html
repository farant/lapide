Christ Prefigured in the Old Testament

Description
The theme of Christ hidden and prefigured throughout the Old Testament -- in events, types, and prophecies -- who is the soul and purpose of all history

The theme of Christ hidden and prefigured throughout the Old Testament -- in events, types, and prophecies -- who is the soul and purpose of all history, perceived dimly under figures by the prophets and clearly only in the Gospel.

References in Commentary

  • Clemens Hieronymi Du Culte — Jerome To Paulinus P2 — Christ was predestined and prefigured in the Law and the Prophets; Abraham saw his day and rejoiced.
    "predestined and prefigured in the Law and the Prophets. Whence the Prophets are also called seers, because they saw him whom the rest did not see. Abraham saw his day and rejoiced."
  • Clemens Hieronymi Du Culte — Jerome To Paulinus P38 — David sounds forth Christ on the lyre and raises up the risen one from the underworld.
    "David, our Simonides, our Pindar and Alcaeus, our Horace too, Catullus and Serenus, sounds forth Christ on the lyre, and on the ten-stringed psaltery raises up the risen one from the underworld."
  • Clemens Hieronymi Du Culte — Du Culte P8 — From the first page of Scripture, under the shades of paradise, the Savior is announced; events are preparation and prefiguration.
    "From the very first page, under the stirring shades of the earthly paradise, it announces to us the coming of the Savior of men."
  • Clemens Hieronymi Du Culte — Du Culte P15 — In Moses and the historical books, Christ hides beneath events; in the psalms and prophecies the veil falls and His person takes shape.
    "Considered in the books of Moses and the history of the Hebrew people, Jesus Christ hides beneath events; He is their soul and their purpose, but in a hidden manner that appears only through the revelation of times and of facts."
  • Commentaria In Pentateuchum Mosis Canones — Canons P23 — Christ is called Emmanuel, Wonderful Counselor, and the Orient -- names signifying what he will be
    "Thus in Isaiah 7:14, Christ is called Emmanuel"
  • Commentaria In Pentateuchum Mosis Canones — Canons P47 — The Prophets signify Christ's redemption through paired names of slaughter and salvation
    "Moses and the other Prophets are accustomed to signify the redemption of Christ by a twofold name"
  • Commentaria In Pentateuchum Mosis Canones — Canons P48 — The temporal liberations from Egypt and Babylon prefigured Christ's spiritual liberation
    "Christ therefore slew nations and men, and raised up others — indeed the very same ones"
