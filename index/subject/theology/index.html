Theology

  • Eschatology
    • Eternal Life and Heavenly Glory — The blessed eternity toward which all pastoral and scholarly labour is directed; union with God in heaven as the final end
  • God
    • Providence of God — God's providential governance of the world and of individual lives, reaching "from end to end mightily" and ordering "all things sweetly"
    • The Holy Trinity — The triune God -- "most holy Trinity and threefold Unity" -- as the ultimate end of all study and labour
  • Scripture
    • Authority of Interpretation — The exclusive right of the Church to judge the true sense and interpretation of Scripture; private interpretation contrary to the Church's teaching or the unanimous consent of the Fathers is forbidden
    • Biblical Inspiration — The doctrine that God is the single author of both Old and New Testaments, and that apostolic traditions were dictated by the Holy Spirit
    • Canon of Scripture — The definitive enumeration of the books of the Old and New Testaments recognised as canonical by the Catholic Church, including the deuterocanonical books
    • Scripture and Tradition — The relationship between written Scripture and unwritten apostolic traditions as co-equal sources of divine revelation, both received from Christ and the Apostles under the dictation of the Holy Spirit
    • Vulgate Authenticity — The declaration by the Council of Trent that the old Latin Vulgate edition is to be held as authentic for public readings, disputations, sermons, and expositions
  • Soteriology
    • The Gospel as Source of Saving Truth — The Gospel promised through the prophets, promulgated by Christ, and preached by the Apostles as the fountain of all saving truth and moral discipline
