Meekness

Description
The virtue of gentleness and mildness of temper, by which one overcomes adversaries through steady kindness rather than force

Meekness is presented as Moses' defining virtue ("the meekest of all mortals") and then traced through St. Basil, who "vanquished his adversaries by his steady kindness," to Van der Burch, who receives everyone graciously and shows "a serene face, a ready word, and a generous spirit." The virtue is distinguished from weakness: it is the instrument by which pastoral authority achieves its aims without force.
