Humility

Description
Lowliness of self-estimation despite great learning or accomplishment; regarding oneself as foolish and inadequate before God

Humility is exemplified most strikingly in Lapide's own self-assessment: despite his vast learning, he declares himself "the most foolish of men" and "a little child who does not know his own going out or coming in" (combining Proverbs 30:2-3 with 3 Kings 3:7). The virtue also appears in the portrait of Pope St. Cornelius, who was "quiet and modest... out of the humility of the diffidence innate to him," and Authbert of Cambrai, who "shone with marvellous humility and holiness."
