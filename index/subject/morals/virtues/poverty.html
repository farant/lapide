Poverty and Detachment from Wealth

Description
Voluntary poverty or indifference to worldly goods; refusing to enrich oneself even when holding positions of great power

Detachment from wealth is illustrated through multiple examples. Thomas More, despite fifty years in the highest offices, "did not increase his annual income to seventy gold pieces." Van der Burch's father likewise held great offices without enriching the family. Basil's response to Modestus is the most vivid: "a man who has nothing is not subject to confiscation of goods -- unless perhaps you need these tattered and worn-out rags of mine, and these few books, in which all my wealth and resources consist." Lapide himself refused any exceptional treatment at meals despite frail health.
