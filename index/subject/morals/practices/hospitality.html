Hospitality

Description
The practice of generous reception and care for travelers, pilgrims, and the sick, especially as a pastoral duty

Basil's pastoral hospitality is described in Gregory Nazianzen's account of famine relief in Pontus: collecting food, setting pots of pottage before the hungry, caring for "both the bodies and the souls of the poor," and imitating Christ's service by washing feet. Basil's work during the famine established a model of the bishop as organiser of material relief that Lapide holds up for Van der Burch's imitation.
