Morals

  • Practices
    • Hospitality — The practice of generous reception and care for travelers, pilgrims, and the sick, especially as a pastoral duty
  • Virtues
    • Charity — Love of neighbour expressed through material assistance to the poor, the afflicted, and the needy; motherly love for one's people even to the point of self-sacrifice
    • Chastity — Purity and virginal integrity of life, especially as a mark of episcopal character
    • Constancy — Steadfast adherence to truth, faith, and duty in the face of threats, persecution, exile, and death
    • Humility — Lowliness of self-estimation despite great learning or accomplishment; regarding oneself as foolish and inadequate before God
    • Meekness — The virtue of gentleness and mildness of temper, by which one overcomes adversaries through steady kindness rather than force
    • Obedience — Submission of one's own will to the will of God as mediated through religious superiors; obedience as dearer than life
    • Poverty and Detachment from Wealth — Voluntary poverty or indifference to worldly goods; refusing to enrich oneself even when holding positions of great power
