Aeneid

Also known as
Aeneis
Author
Virgil

Virgil's epic poem, cited by Jerome for lines repurposed in Virgiliocento as pseudo-Christian texts, and by Lacordaire as an example of the heights of human genius.

References in Commentary

  • Clemens Hieronymi Du Culte — Jerome To Paulinus P6 — Jerome quotes Aeneid I.664 as repurposed to depict the Father speaking to the Son
    "My son, my strength, my great power alone."
  • Clemens Hieronymi Du Culte — Jerome To Paulinus P8 — Jerome quotes Aeneid II.650 as repurposed for the crucifixion scene
    "Such things he kept recalling, and remained fixed."
  • Clemens Hieronymi Du Culte — Du Culte P15 — Lacordaire lists "those of Virgil on the misfortunes of Aeneas" as among the greatest works of human genius, still inferior to the psalms and prophecies
    "those of Virgil on the misfortunes of Aeneas"
