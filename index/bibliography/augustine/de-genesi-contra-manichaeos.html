    De Genesi contra Manichaeos

    Also known as
    On Genesis Against the Manichaeans, On Genesis Against the Manichees
    Author
    St. Augustine of Hippo

    Augustine's earlier allegorical commentary on Genesis, written to refute Manichaean objections to the Old Testament.

    References in Commentary

    • Genesis 1 — Heaven And Earth P3 — Cited (Book I, ch. VII) on heaven and earth as prime matter
      "Saint Augustine, book I of On Genesis Against the Manichaeans, chapter VII: Heaven and earth, he says, are here called prime matter"
    • Genesis 1 — Verse 5 P2 — Cited (Book I, ch. 9 and 10) on God calling the light Day
      "So St. Augustine, Book I of On Genesis Against the Manichaeans, ch. 9 and 10."
    • Genesis 1 — Verse 31 P7 — Cited (Book I, ch. 21) on "very good" applied to all things together

    • Genesis 1 — Verse 24 P6 — Cited (Book I, ch. 16) on reasons God created venomous creatures
      "See St. Augustine, Book I On Genesis Against the Manicheans, 16."
