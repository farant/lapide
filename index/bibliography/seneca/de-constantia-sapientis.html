    On the Firmness of the Wise Man

    Also known as
    De Constantia Sapientis, On the Wise Man
    Author
    Seneca

    Seneca's Stoic essay on the wise man's invulnerability to injury and insult, applied by Lapide to Moses's humility.

    Preface and Praise of Sacred Scripture

    • Moses's Humility and Meekness — Seneca quoted on the wise man: removed from contact with inferiors
      "The wise man," says Seneca in his work On the Wise Man, "has been removed by a greater distance from contact with inferiors than that any harmful force should carry its power to him"
