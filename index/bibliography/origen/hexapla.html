Hexapla

Also known as
Hexapla, Octapla
Author
Origen

Origen's monumental six-column edition of the Old Testament presenting Hebrew text and multiple Greek translations side by side, a foundational work of biblical textual criticism.

References in Commentary

  • Proemium Et Encomium Sacrae Scripturae — Chapter Iv P17 — Origen described as the first to compose the Hexapla and Octapla with immense labor
    "for this one reason he thoroughly learned the Hebrew language, and collected from across the whole world the versions of various translators, and was the first by a new example to compose with immense labor the Hexapla and Octapla, and to illuminate them with scholia"
  • Proemium Et Encomium Sacrae Scripturae — The Septuagint P5 — Origen collected all translations to arrange the Tetrapla, Hexapla, and Octapla; corrected the Septuagint
    "Origen collected all of these and from them arranged his Tetrapla, Hexapla, and Octapla; he also corrected the corrupt Septuagint"
