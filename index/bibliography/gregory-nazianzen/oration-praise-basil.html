Oration in Praise of Basil

Description
Funeral oration for St. Basil the Great (Oration 43)
Author
St. Gregory Nazianzen

Gregory Nazianzen's celebrated funeral oration for his lifelong friend Basil, delivered after Basil's death in 379. One of the most quoted patristic texts in the dedicatory letter, providing the principal testimony about Basil's character, pastoral work, and legacy. Gregory compares Basil to Moses, praises his kindness, calls his life "a rule of living," describes the extraordinary funeral mourning, and declares that through Caesarea alone Basil "gave light to the whole world."
