Letter to the Brothers of Mont-Dieu

Also known as
Epistola ad Fratres de Monte Dei
Author
St. Bernard of Clairvaux (often attributed to William of Saint-Thierry)

Letter on the spiritual life, attributed to Bernard, asserting that one must imbibe Paul's spirit to understand him.

References in Commentary

  • Proemium Et Encomium Sacrae Scripturae — Chapter V P21 — Bernard cited: no one enters Paul's meaning without first imbibing his spirit
    "St. Bernard does not hesitate to assert (in his letter to the Brothers of Mont-Dieu) that no one will enter into the meaning of Paul unless he has first imbibed his spirit"
