Dialogues

Also known as
Dialogi de Vita et Miraculis Patrum Italicorum
Author
St. Gregory the Great

Gregory's accounts of the lives and miracles of Italian saints, especially Benedict of Nursia, including the famous vision of the whole world in a single ray of light.

References in Commentary

  • Proemium Et Encomium Sacrae Scripturae — Chapter V P17 — Book II, chapter 35 cited: Benedict's vision of the whole world in a single ray of light
    "St. Gregory reports, II Dialogues, chapter 35, that the Blessed Benedict, praying one evening at a window, saw a light so great that it surpassed the day and put all darkness to flight, and in this light, he says, the whole world, as if gathered under a single ray of sunlight, was brought before his eyes"
