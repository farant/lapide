    Commentaria in Genesim

    Also known as
    Commentary on Genesis
    Author
    Benedictus Pererius

    Pererius's extensive commentary on Genesis, cited by Lapide for discussions of Hebrew semantics and the composition of the Pentateuch.

    References in Commentary

    • Commentaria In Pentateuchum Mosis Canones — Canons P5 — Cited (vol. III, p. 430ff.) for further discussion of the canon on "eternal"
      "see more in Pererius, vol. III on Genesis, p. 430 and following"
    • Commentaria In Pentateuchum Mosis Canones — Argumentum P9 — Cited for the view that Moses wrote Genesis while in exile in Midian
      "Moses wrote Genesis while he was living as an exile in Midian, Exodus 2:15, says Pererius"
    • Genesis 1 — Introduction P5 — Cited as dividing Genesis into four parts treated in four volumes
      "Genesis can be divided into four parts, which Pererius divided and treated in as many volumes."
    • Genesis 1 — Introduction P8 — Cited as excelling among recent writers on Genesis
      "among whom Pererius excels in the variety of his learning."
    • Genesis 1 — Introduction P11 — Listed among recommended authors for moral teachings
      "I would like to advise preachers and all who eagerly seek moral teachings to read St. Chrysostom, Ambrose, Origen, Rupert, Rabanus, Jerome de Oleastro, Pererius"
    • Genesis 1 — Verse 6 P9 — Cited as holding the firmament is the middle region of air
      "Thirdly, Rupert, Eugubinus, Pererius, Gregory of Valencia hold that the firmament is the middle region of the air"
    • Genesis 1 — Verse 3 P9 — Cited on the light being the sun still unformed
      "Pererius and others assert the same."
    • Genesis 1 — Verse 5 P5 — Cited on the thirteen prerogatives of Sunday
      "See the thirteen prerogatives of Sunday in Pererius at the end of his treatment of the first day."
    • Genesis 1 — Verse 24 P25 — Cited on the tropological meaning of the six days
      "so from Eucherius, Origen, and Hugh, says Pererius."
    • Genesis 1 — Verse 31 P2 — Cited on why "it was good" was omitted after man's creation
      "So says Pererius."
    • Genesis 1 — Verse 31 P18 — Cited on God ordering all evils toward good
      "So says Pererius."
