    On the Contemplative Life

    Also known as
    De Vita Contemplativa
    Author
    Philo of Alexandria

    Philo's account of the Therapeutae, an ascetic Jewish community near Alexandria identified by early Christians with the first Alexandrian Christians, who spent their entire days in Scripture reading.

    Preface and Praise of Sacred Scripture

    • Chapter IV: The Judgments and Examples of the Fathers — Philo cited as eyewitness on how the Essenes/Therapeutae spent all day studying Scripture
      "One may see in Philo the Jew, an eyewitness, in his book On the Contemplative Life... how diligently the Essenes — the first, I say, of those Alexandrian Christians — from dawn to night spent the entire day in reading"
