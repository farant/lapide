Bibliography

  • Ambrose
    • De Hexaemeron — Latin treatise on the six days of creation, based on Basil's Hexaemeron
  • Anon
    • Annals of the Church of Cambrai — Historical chronicle of bishops of the Archdiocese of Cambrai
    • Life of St. Lietbert — Hagiographical account of St. Lietbert, Bishop of Cambrai
  • Augustine
    • De Doctrina Christiana — Treatise on biblical interpretation and Christian teaching
  • Basil
    • Epistle 140 — Letter to Libanius the Sophist on studying Moses and Elijah
    • Epistle 164 — Letter describing fleeing the bishopric of Neocaesarea
    • Epistle 63 — Letter defending monasticism against heretical critics
    • Hexaemeron — Homilies on the six days of creation (Genesis 1)
  • Bible
    • 3-4 Esdras — Apocryphal books placed outside the canon by the Council of Trent
    • Prayer of Manasseh — Apocryphal prayer placed outside the canon by the Council of Trent
    • The Vulgate — Official Latin Bible declared authentic by the Council of Trent
  • Clement Viii
    • Cum Sacrorum (Bull on the Vulgate) — Papal bull establishing the corrected Vatican Vulgate (9 November 1592)
  • Councils
    • Council of Trent, Session IV — Two decrees on Scripture — On Canonical Scriptures and On the Edition and Use of Sacred Books (8 April 1546)
  • Crampon
    • Annotations on the Pentateuch — Editorial footnotes added to Lapide's Pentateuch commentaries for the 1891 Vives edition
  • Cyprian
    • Letter 2 to Antonianus — Letter praising Pope St. Cornelius for his humble ascent to the episcopate
  • Cyrus Theodorus
    • Verses on Modestus — Mock verses ridiculing Prefect Modestus after his humiliation by Basil
  • Damascene
    • First Oration on Holy Images — Defense of holy images, containing the account of Basil's prayer before an image of Our Lady
  • Gregory Great
    • On Jerome's Translation — Gregory's praise of Jerome's translation as drawn more truly from the Hebrew
  • Gregory Nazianzen
    • Oration in Praise of Basil — Funeral oration for St. Basil the Great (Oration 43)
  • Gregory Nyssa
    • Works on Basil — Testimonies about St. Basil including the dove vision and giving away his inheritance
  • Hincmar
    • On the Bishops of Cambrai — Praises of the virtues of St. Theodoric and St. John, Bishops of Cambrai
  • Homer
    • Works of Homer — General reference to Homer's epics
  • Horace
    • Ars Poetica — Verse epistle on poetry and literary craft
  • Isidore
    • On Jerome's Version — Isidore's preference for the Hieronymian version of Scripture
  • Lapide
    • Commentaries on Acts — Commentary on the Acts of the Apostles
    • Commentaries on Ecclesiastes — Commentary on Ecclesiastes
    • Commentaries on Ecclesiasticus — Commentary on Ecclesiasticus (Sirach)
    • Commentaries on Ezra through Maccabees — Commentary on Ezra, Nehemiah, Tobit, Judith, Esther, and Maccabees
    • Commentaries on Job — Commentary on Job, left incomplete at Lapide's death
    • Commentaries on Joshua through Chronicles — Commentary on Joshua, Judges, Ruth, Kings, and Chronicles
    • Commentaries on Proverbs — Commentary on the Proverbs of Solomon
    • Commentaries on the Apocalypse — Commentary on the Book of Revelation
    • Commentaries on the Catholic Epistles — Commentary on the epistles of James, 1-2 Peter, 1-3 John, and Jude
    • Commentaries on the Gospels — Commentary on the four Gospels
    • Commentaries on the Major Prophets — Commentary on Isaiah, Jeremiah, Ezekiel, and Daniel
    • Commentaries on the Minor Prophets — Commentary on the twelve minor prophets
    • Commentaries on the Pauline Epistles — Commentary on all fourteen Pauline epistles
    • Commentaries on the Pentateuch — Commentaries on the five books of Moses (Genesis, Exodus, Leviticus, Numbers, Deuteronomy)
    • Commentaries on the Psalms — Commentary on the Psalms, left incomplete at Lapide's death
    • Commentaries on the Song of Songs — Commentary on the Canticle of Canticles
    • Commentaries on Wisdom — Commentary on the Book of Wisdom
  • Rufinus
    • Ecclesiastical History — Continuation of Eusebius's Church History covering the 4th century
  • Sophronius
    • Greek Translation of Jerome's Psalter and Prophets — Greek translation of Jerome's Psalter and Prophets, showing acceptance by both Latins and Greeks
  • Theodoret
    • Ecclesiastical History — Church history covering the 4th–5th centuries
