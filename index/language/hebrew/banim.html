    בָּנִים

    Transliteration
    banim
    Meaning
    sons — etymologically linked to abanim (stones) and bana (he built)

    Hebrew word for "sons." Lapide explains the etymology: the Hebrews call sons *banim*, as if *abanim* ("stones"), from the root *bana* ("he built"), because families are built from sons as from stones. This is an example of catachresis (Canon 10).

    Commentary on the Pentateuch of Moses

    • Canons Bearing a Torch Before the Pentateuch — Lapide explains the etymology linking sons and stones
      "the Hebrews call sons banim, as if abanim, that is, "stones," from the root bana, that is, "he built"; for from sons as from stones the houses and families of parents are built"
