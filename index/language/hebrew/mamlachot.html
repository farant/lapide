מַמְלָכוֹת

Transliteration
mamlakhot
Old Hebrew
𐤌𐤌𐤋𐤊𐤅𐤕
Meaning
Kingdoms — alternative Hebrew title rejected by Jerome in favor of Melachim (Kings)
Root
מלך

An alternative Hebrew title for the books of Kings, meaning "Kingdoms." Jerome rejects this in favor of Melachim ("Kings"), arguing the books describe one Israelite people, not many kingdoms.

References in Commentary

  • Clemens Hieronymi Du Culte — Helmeted Prologue P13 — Jerome argues against using Mamlachot, explaining the books describe one people, not many kingdoms
    "And it is much better to say Melachim, that is, of Kings, than Mamlachot, that is, of Kingdoms. For it does not describe the kingdoms of many nations, but of one Israelite people, which is comprised of twelve tribes."
