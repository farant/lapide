    עוֹלָם

    Transliteration
    olam
    Meaning
    eternal, age, hidden time — signifies an age whose limit is not perceived

    Hebrew word meaning "eternal" or "age." Lapide explains that *olam* does not signify absolute eternity but a hidden period of time whose limit and end is not perceived. It derives from the root *alam* (to hide or conceal). The old law is said to endure "forever" (*olam*) only relative to the Jewish commonwealth.

    Commentary on the Pentateuch of Moses

    • Canons Bearing a Torch Before the Pentateuch — Lapide explains that "eternal" in Hebrew does not mean absolute eternity but a hidden period of time
      "the Hebrew olam, that is, "eternal," signifies an age, inasmuch as it is hidden, or whose limit and end is not perceived. For the root alam means to hide or conceal"
