גַּל עֵינַי וְאַבִּיטָה

Transliteration
gal enai ve'abbitah
Old Hebrew
𐤂𐤋 𐤏𐤉𐤍𐤉 𐤅𐤀𐤁𐤉𐤈𐤄
Meaning
Roll away from my eyes [the veil], and I shall clearly behold
Root
גלל / נבט

The Hebrew text of Psalm 119:18 (Vulgate 118:18). Lapide explains that "gal" means "roll away" (the veil of darkness from the eyes), illustrating the difficulty of Scripture and the need for divine illumination.

References in Commentary

  • Proemium Et Encomium Sacrae Scripturae — Chapter Iv P6 — Lapide gives the Hebrew reading of Psalm 118:18, explaining that "gal" means "roll away" (the veil of darkness from the eyes)
    "where the Hebrew reads, gal enai veabbita — 'roll away from my eyes (the veil of darkness, that is), and I shall clearly behold the wonders of your law.'"
