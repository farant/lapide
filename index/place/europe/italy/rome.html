Rome

Description
Capital of the Roman Empire and seat of the Papacy; where Lapide taught, wrote, and died in 1637
Coordinates
41.9028, 12.4964

Capital of the Roman Empire and seat of the Papacy. Cornelius a Lapide was summoned there by his superiors after twenty years at Louvain, taught Sacred Scripture for many years, and eventually devoted himself entirely to writing. He died in Rome on 12 March 1637. Vitelleschi's permission for the Pentateuch commentary and Clement VIII's bull on the Vulgate were both issued there.
