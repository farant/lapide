  • Low Countries
    • Antwerp — Major port city in the Low Countries; center of printing and publishing for Lapide's commentaries
    • Belgium — Region of the Low Countries / Southern Netherlands, used interchangeably with "Belgium" in the text
    • Bocholt — Birthplace of Cornelius a Lapide, in the region of Eupen in the Low Countries
    • Cambrai — City in the Low Countries; seat of an archbishopric and duchy in the Holy Roman Empire
    • Flanders — County in the Low Countries; Van der Burch's grandfather was President of its Council
    • Ghent — Major city in Flanders; seat of a diocese in the Low Countries, birthplace of Emperor Charles V
    • Hainaut — County/province in the Low Countries; evangelized by St. Authbert, afflicted by plague at Mons
    • Louvain — University city in Brabant where Cornelius a Lapide taught Sacred Scripture for over twenty years
    • Mechlin — City in the Duchy of Brabant; seat of the metropolitan archbishopric of the Low Countries
    • Mons — City in the County of Hainaut; afflicted by plague and relieved through the relics of St. Macarius
    • Valenciennes — City in the Archdiocese of Cambrai that rejoiced at Van der Burch's appointment
