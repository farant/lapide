    Antwerp

    Also known as
    Antwerpen, Anvers
    Description
    Major commercial city in the Spanish Netherlands; location of the Nutius/Moretus printing press
    Coordinates
    51.2194, 4.4025
    © Esri, Maxar, Earthstar Geographics

    Major commercial city in the Spanish Netherlands. Location of the Nutius/Moretus printing press, where several of Lapide's commentaries were published. Charles Scribani, Jesuit Provincial Superior, issued printing permissions from Antwerp. Egbert Spitholdius served as Canon and Parish Priest here.

    Preliminaries

    • Permissions P2 — Scribani grants printing permission to Antwerp printers
      "I, Charles Scribani, Provincial Superior of the Society of Jesus in the province of Flandro-Belgica, by authority granted to me for this purpose by the Very Reverend Father General Mutius Vitelleschi, grant to the heirs of Martin Nutius and to Jan Moretus, printers of Antwerp"
    • Permissions P2 — Permission letter dated at Antwerp
      "I have given these letters written with my own hand and secured with the seal of my office, at Antwerp, 23 August, in the year 1616"
    • Permissions P3 — Spitholdius identified as Canon and Parish Priest of Antwerp
      "Licenciate in Sacred Theology, Canon and Parish Priest of Antwerp, Censor of Books"
    • THE LIFE OF CORNELIUS A LAPIDE. — Multiple works by Lapide published at Antwerp
      "Commentaries on the Pentateuch of Moses, Antwerp 1616, again in 1623 in folio"
