    City in northern England. Alcuin publicly taught the sacred Letters at York. St. Ludger came from Frisia to York to hear him.

    Preface and Praise of Sacred Scripture

    • Chapter IV: The Judgments and Examples of the Fathers — Alcuin publicly taught the sacred Letters at York; St. Ludger came from Frisia to York to hear him
      "He publicly taught the sacred Letters at York in England; whence St. Ludger came from Frisia to York to hear him"

    Chapter I (The Six Days of Creation)

    • Introduction — Thomas the Doctor identified as "the Doctor of York" rather than the Angelic Doctor
      "Thomas the Doctor also wrote -- not the holy Angelic Doctor, but the English one, namely the Doctor of York, around the year of Christ 1400."
