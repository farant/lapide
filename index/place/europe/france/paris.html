    Paris

    Description
    Capital of France; location of publisher Ludwig Vives and Cramoisy press

    Capital of France. Location of the publisher Ludwig Vives, who issued the 1891 Crampon edition of Lapide's commentaries. Also the location of the Cramoisy press, which published Lapide's commentary on the Proverbs of Solomon.

    Preliminaries

    • Title Page — Place of publication of the 1891 Vives edition
      "PARIS Published by Ludwig Vives, bookseller and publisher, 13, Street commonly called Delambre, 13. 1891"
    • THE LIFE OF CORNELIUS A LAPIDE. — Lapide's works published at Paris
      "on the Proverbs of Solomon, Antwerp and Paris, at Cramoisy's, 1635; on Ecclesiastes, Antwerp 1638, Paris 1639"

    Preface and Praise of Sacred Scripture

    • Chapter IV: The Judgments and Examples of the Fathers — Charlemagne established schools of sacred Letters at Paris, described as an ancient academy
      "established schools of sacred Letters both elsewhere and at Paris (so ancient is this academy, which is the mother of Cologne and the grandmother of Louvain)"
