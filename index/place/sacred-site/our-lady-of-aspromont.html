Our Lady of Aspromont

Description
Marian shrine near Louvain, renowned for miracles; site of Lapide's near-martyrdom in 1604
Coordinates
50.87, 4.69

Marian shrine near Louvain, "renowned for miracles." In 1604, on the feast of the Nativity of Our Lady, Cornelius a Lapide was assisting crowds of pilgrims there when Dutch cavalry attacked, laying everything waste with sword and fire. Lapide escaped carrying the Most Holy Eucharist out of the church to prevent its profanation, aided by the intercession of Our Lady. The event was regarded as miraculous.
