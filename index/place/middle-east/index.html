Middle East

  • Assyria
    • Nineveh — Capital of the Assyrian Empire; Jonah called it to repentance and under its name announced salvation to the nations
  • Egypt
    • Alexandria — Major port city in Egypt; great center of learning in the ancient world
    • Memphis — Ancient Egyptian city; site of prophets/priests whom Pythagoras visited
  • Iraq
    • Mesopotamia (alias)
  • Israel
    • Bethlehem (alias)
    • Jerusalem (alias)
  • Jordan
    • Plains of Moab (alias)
  • Mesopotamia
    • Babylon — Capital of the Babylonian Empire; heard the voice of the exiled saints of Judah on the banks of its rivers
    • Nisibis — City in upper Mesopotamia (modern southeastern Turkey); site of schools of sacred Scripture established in the time of Emperor Justinian
  • Palestine
    • Bethlehem — Town in Palestine; sought by St. Jerome as his place of study and asceticism
    • Cana — Village in Galilee; site of the wedding feast where Jesus performed his first miracle
    • Cedron — Valley and seasonal watercourse east of Jerusalem; Jesus crossed it on the night of his arrest
    • Galilee — Northern region of Palestine; where Jesus preached the Sermon on the Mount and conducted much of his public ministry
    • Jericho — Ancient city in Palestine; an anonymous translation of Scripture was found in jars there in the 7th year of Caracalla
    • Jerusalem — Holy city in Palestine; destination of St. Lietbert's pilgrimage
    • Jordan — Principal river of Palestine; Joshua crossed it to enter the promised land; Jesus was baptized in it by John the Baptist
    • Judea — Southern region of Palestine; where Jesus spoke and lived during his public ministry
    • Moresheth — Town in Judah; birthplace of the prophet Micah
    • Nazareth — Town in Galilee; where Jesus grew up in the obscure house of an artisan
    • Palestine — The biblical Holy Land; location of Jerome's Bethlehem and a region full of followers of monastic life
    • Samaria — Capital of the northern kingdom of Israel; mentioned by Jerome in connection with Hosea and Amos
    • Zion — Hill in Jerusalem; symbol of the holy city and the people of God
  • Syria
    • Antioch — Major city of the Roman East; one of the first centers of Christianity
    • Damascus — Ancient city in Syria; Paul visited Damascus and Arabia before going up to Jerusalem to see Peter
  • Arabia — Region east and south of Palestine; Paul visited Arabia after his conversion, before going to Jerusalem
  • Egypt — Ancient land; place of Moses' captivity under Pharaoh and a region full of monastic life
  • Mesopotamia — Ancient region between the Tigris and Euphrates; named as full of followers of monastic Christian philosophy
  • Persia — Ancient empire east of Mesopotamia; Apollonius entered Persia on his journey eastward
  • Phoenicia — Ancient coastal region on the eastern Mediterranean; Cadmus was the first to bring Greek letters from Phoenicia to the Greeks
