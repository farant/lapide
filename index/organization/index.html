Organization

  • Church Institutions
    • Apostolic See — Episcopal jurisdiction of the Pope as Bishop of Rome; supreme authority on Scripture and its preservation
    • Church of Ghent — The local Church (diocese) of Ghent; its renewed discipline under Van der Burch described as a new radiance for all Belgium
    • Metropolitan Church of Mechlin — Cathedral church of the Archdiocese of Mechlin; Van der Burch presided as Dean
    • Vatican Press — Printing press of the Holy See; used for the authoritative edition of the corrected Vulgate under Clement VIII
  • Councils
    • Council of Trent — Ecumenical council of the Catholic Church (1545-1563); Session IV decrees on canonical Scripture and the Vulgate reproduced in this document
    • Fifth Lateran Council — Ecumenical council (1512-1517); referenced by the Council of Trent regarding penalties for unauthorized printing
  • Dioceses
    • Archdiocese of Cambrai — Ancient archdiocese in the southern Low Countries/northern France; Van der Burch was inaugurated as its Archbishop
    • Archdiocese of Mechlin — Catholic archdiocese in the Spanish Netherlands with its see at Mechlin; Van der Burch served as Dean and Vicar-General here
    • Diocese of Amiens — Catholic diocese in northern France; diocese of Augustin Crampon, annotator of this edition
    • Diocese of Ghent — Catholic diocese in Flanders; Van der Burch served as its Bishop before transfer to Cambrai
    • Diocese of Rochester — English diocese; Bl. John Fisher served as its bishop and refused transfer to any wealthier see
  • Governments
    • Council of Flanders — Governing council of the County of Flanders; Van der Burch's grandfather served as its President
    • Holy Roman Empire — Supranational political entity in central Europe; Van der Burch held the title of Prince of the Holy Roman Empire
    • Parliament of Mechlin — Supreme court of the Spanish Netherlands, sitting at Mechlin; Van der Burch's father served as its first President
    • Privy Council — Advisory council to the sovereign of the Spanish Netherlands; Van der Burch's father served as President
    • Spanish Netherlands — Southern provinces of the Low Countries under Spanish Habsburg sovereignty; ruled by Archdukes who governed civil and ecclesiastical affairs
  • Publishers
    • Cramoisy — Parisian publisher; printed Lapide's commentary on the Proverbs of Solomon in 1635
    • Ludwig Vives (Publisher) — Bookseller and publisher in Paris; published the 1891 Crampon edition of Lapide's commentaries
    • Nutius/Moretus — Printers of Antwerp; granted permission to print Lapide's Pentateuch commentaries in 1616
  • Religious Orders
    • Louvain College — Jesuit college at Louvain; mentioned by Lapide as bearing esteem toward Van der Burch
    • Province of Flandro-Belgica — Jesuit administrative province covering the southern Low Countries; Charles Scribani served as Provincial Superior
    • Society of Jesus — Catholic religious order founded by St. Ignatius of Loyola in 1540; Cornelius a Lapide was a member
  • Universities
    • University of Louvain — Catholic university in Louvain; where Lapide held the chair of Sacred Scripture for over twenty years
