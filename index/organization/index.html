Organization

  • Church Institutions
    • Apostolic See — The episcopal jurisdiction of the Bishop of Rome, referenced in conciliar decrees, papal bulls, and permissions
    • Metropolitan Church of Mechlin — Principal church of the Archdiocese of Mechlin; Van der Burch served as its Dean
    • St. Peter's Basilica — Papal basilica in Rome; location given in the dating clause of Clement VIII's bull on the Vulgate
    • Vatican Press — Official printing press of the Vatican that produced the Clementine Vulgate
  • Councils
    • Council of Trent (1545–1563) — Ecumenical council whose Session IV defined the canon of Scripture and declared the Vulgate authentic
    • Fifth Lateran Council (1512–1517) — Ecumenical council referenced for its decree imposing penalties on unauthorized printers
  • Dioceses
    • Archdiocese of Cambrai — Ancient archdiocese in the Low Countries, praised for its many sainted bishops; Van der Burch inaugurated as Archbishop
    • Archdiocese of Mechlin — Metropolitan see of the Low Countries; Van der Burch served as its Archbishop
    • Diocese of Amiens — French diocese where Crampon served as priest; Bishop Jacobus Antonius approved his annotations
    • Diocese of Ghent — Suffragan diocese in the Low Countries; Van der Burch called to its bishopric by Archduke Albert
  • Governments
    • Holy Roman Empire — The supranational political entity of central Europe; Van der Burch held the title Prince of the Holy Roman Empire
    • Spanish Netherlands — The southern Low Countries under Spanish Habsburg rule, the political context of the dedicatory letter and permissions
  • Publishers
    • Cramoisy — Parisian printing house that published Lapide's Proverbs commentary in 1635
    • Ludwig Vives (publisher) — Parisian publishing house at 13 Rue Delambre that published the 1891 Crampon-annotated Lapide edition
    • Nutius & Moretus — Antwerp printers granted permission to print Lapide's Pentateuch commentary
  • Religious Orders
    • Society of Jesus — Catholic religious order founded by St. Ignatius of Loyola in 1540
  • Universities
    • University of Louvain — University where Lapide taught Holy Scripture publicly for more than twenty years
