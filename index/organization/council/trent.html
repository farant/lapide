Council of Trent

Also known as
Tridentine Synod, Concilium Tridentinum
Dates
1545–1563
Description
Ecumenical council whose Session IV defined the canon of Scripture and declared the Vulgate authentic

The nineteenth ecumenical council of the Catholic Church. Its Session IV (8 April 1546) issued two decrees of central importance to the Preliminares: the decree on canonical Scriptures (defining the Old and New Testament canon) and the decree on the authentic edition of the Vulgate. Both decrees are quoted in full in the Preface to the Reader. The council's declaration that the Vulgate was to be held as "authentic" in public readings, disputations, and preaching is the doctrinal foundation for Lapide's commentary project.
