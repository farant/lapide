The Seventy Translators

Also known as
Septuagint translators, LXX
Description
The translators of the Hebrew Bible into Greek (the Septuagint); Lapide attributes the division and naming of the Pentateuch to them

The translators of the Hebrew Bible into Greek (the Septuagint). Lapide attributes the division and naming of the five books of the Pentateuch (Genesis, Exodus, Leviticus, Numbers, Deuteronomy) to them, not to Moses as Philo holds.

References in Commentary

  • Commentaria In Pentateuchum Mosis Canones — Argumentum P2 — Divided and named the five books of the Pentateuch
    "which were thus divided and named not by Moses, as Philo holds, but by the Seventy Translators"
  • Commentaria In Pentateuchum Mosis Canones — Canons P41 — The Septuagint version from time to time gives a different literal sense than the Vulgate
    "the Septuagint version from time to time gives a different literal sense than ours"
