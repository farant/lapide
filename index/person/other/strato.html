    Strato of Lampsacus

    Dates
    c. 335–c. 269 BC
    Role
    Greek philosopher (Peripatetic)

    Peripatetic philosopher who succeeded Theophrastus as head of the Lyceum. Held the erroneous view that the world was ungenerated and eternal.

    Chapter I (The Six Days of Creation)

    • He Created — Named for the error that the world was ungenerated and eternal
      "the error of Strato of Lampsacus, who imagined that the world was ungenerated"
