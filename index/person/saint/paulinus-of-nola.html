St. Paulinus of Nola

Also known as
Paulinus, St. Paulinus
Dates
c. 354–431
Role
Bishop of Nola, correspondent of Jerome

Bishop of Nola and correspondent of Jerome. Addressee of Jerome's famous letter on the study of Scripture.

References in Commentary

  • Clemens Hieronymi Du Culte — Jerome To Paulinus — Named as the addressee of Jerome's letter
    "JEROME TO PAULINUS."
  • Clemens Hieronymi Du Culte — Jerome To Paulinus P2 — Jerome praises his ardor and zeal for learning
    "your ardor and zeal for learning ought to be approved on its own, even without us."
  • Clemens Hieronymi Du Culte — Jerome To Paulinus P48 — Jerome urges him to renounce the world and hasten to join him; praises his prudence and eloquence
    "Hasten, I beg you, and rather cut than untie the rope of the little boat stuck in the surf."
