St. Ephrem the Syrian

Also known as
Ephraem Syrus
Dates
c. 306–373
Role
Deacon, Doctor of the Church, hymnographer

The greatest poet and hymnographer of the Syriac tradition, declared Doctor of the Church in 1920. In the Preliminares he appears in the account of Basil's preaching, having seen a dove whispering the sermon into Basil's ear — a sign of the Holy Spirit's inspiration.
