    St. Albert the Great

    Also known as
    Albertus Magnus, Albert
    Dates
    c. 1200–1280
    Role
    Dominican friar, Bishop of Regensburg, Doctor of the Church, philosopher-theologian

    Dominican friar, Bishop of Regensburg, and Doctor of the Church. Teacher of Thomas Aquinas. Among the scholars who used Jerome's version of Scripture exclusively.

    Preliminaries

    • PREFACE TO THE READER (1) — Listed among scholars who used Jerome's version
      "Alexander, Albert, Thomas, Bonaventure"

    Chapter I (The Six Days of Creation)

    • Verse 14: Let There Be Lights in the Firmament — Cited alongside Avicenna for denying stars are luminous of themselves
      "Albert, Avicenna, Bede, and Pliny (book II, ch. 6) deny this"
    • Verse 22: And He Blessed Them, Saying: Increase and Multiply — Cited for showing that the claim about vultures is fabulous
      "that all these claims are fabulous is shown by Albertus Magnus"
