St. Anselm of Canterbury

Also known as
Anselmus Cantuariensis
Dates
c. 1033–1109
Role
Archbishop of Canterbury, Doctor of the Church

Archbishop of Canterbury and Doctor of the Church, often called the father of Scholasticism. In the Preliminares he is listed among the "most learned men" who used Jerome's Vulgate translation.
