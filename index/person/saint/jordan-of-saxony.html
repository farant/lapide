St. Jordan of Saxony

Also known as
Jordanus, Jordan
Dates
c. 1190–1237
Role
Second Master General of the Dominican Order after St. Dominic

Second Master General of the Order of Preachers, successor to St. Dominic; known for his teaching on the balance of prayer and study.

References in Commentary

  • Proemium Et Encomium Sacrae Scripturae — Saintly Examples P7 — When asked whether prayer or Scripture study was better, replied with an analogy: "Is it better always to drink, or always to eat?" — both needed in alternation
    "St. Jordan, indeed, a doctor, the second Master General of his Order after St. Dominic"
