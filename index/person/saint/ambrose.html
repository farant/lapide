St. Ambrose

Also known as
Ambrosius Mediolanensis
Dates
c. 339–397
Role
Church Father, Bishop of Milan, Doctor of the Church

One of the four great Latin Doctors of the Church and Bishop of Milan. In the Preliminares he is noted for translating Basil's Hexaemeron into Latin, giving "to Latin ears not so much his own work as that of Saint Basil, in his treatise On the Work of the Six Days."
