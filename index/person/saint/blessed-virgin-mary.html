    Blessed Virgin Mary

    Also known as
    Mary, the Virgin, Our Lady
    Dates
    1st century BC–1st century
    Role
    Mother of God, the Blessed Virgin

    Mother of God and the Blessed Virgin. The temple sees the Son of God come in her arms; she remains with Christ at the end.

    References in Commentary

    • Clemens Hieronymi Du Culte — Du Culte P8 — The temple "sees the Son of God come in the arms of a Virgin"
      "it sees the Son of God come in the arms of a Virgin"
    • Clemens Hieronymi Du Culte — Du Culte P47 — Named as "Mary" among the three who remain with Christ at the end
      "it will be in the hands of John, of Mary, and of Magdalene that he will find the last farewell of earth"
    • Genesis 1 — Verse 12 P12 — Named as receiving the Annunciation on March 25, the first day of the world
      "the first day of the world was March 25, on which also the Blessed Virgin received the Annunciation, and Christ was incarnated in her"
    • Genesis 1 — Verse 16 P19 — Named symbolically as the moon in the firmament of the Church
      "the sun is Christ, the moon is the Blessed Virgin, the fixed stars are the other Saints"
    • Genesis 1 — Verse 22 P13 — Referenced in argument against pagans: how could she bear Christ while remaining a virgin?
      "How could the Blessed Virgin, remaining a virgin, bear Christ?"
