St. Augustine of Hippo

Also known as
Augustinus Hipponensis, Aurelius Augustinus
Dates
354–430
Role
Church Father, Bishop of Hippo, Doctor of the Church

One of the four great Latin Doctors of the Church and the most influential theologian of the Western tradition. In the Preliminares he appears as a witness to the superiority of Jerome's Vulgate, calling the Old Latin edition "the Italic" in his De Doctrina Christiana and praising Jerome as "a most learned man and most skilled in three languages."
