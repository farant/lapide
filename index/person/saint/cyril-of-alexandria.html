    St. Cyril of Alexandria

    Also known as
    Cyril, Saint Cyril
    Dates
    c. 376–444
    Role
    Patriarch of Alexandria, Doctor of the Church

    Patriarch of Alexandria and Doctor of the Church; wrote extensively on the Pentateuch and demonstrated the antiquity of Moses relative to all Gentile sages.

    Works

    • Against Julian
    • Glaphyra
    • On Adoration in Spirit and Truth
    • Thesaurus

    Preface and Praise of Sacred Scripture

    • Chapter IV: The Judgments and Examples of the Fathers — Listed among Fathers who labored night and day over sacred books
      "Gregory, Augustine, Ambrose, Eusebius, Origen, Jerome, Cyril, and the whole chorus of holy Fathers"
    • The Fathers' Defense of the Old Testament — Wrote 17 books On Adoration in Spirit and Truth on the Pentateuch
      "On the Pentateuch Cyril wrote 17 books On Adoration in Spirit and Truth"
    • The Septuagint Translation and Greek Translators — Among those who believed the 72 translators of the Septuagint worked separately in different cells
      "if we believe Justin, Cyril, Clement of Alexandria, and Augustine"
    • Praises of Moses from Scripture and the Fathers — In book 1 Against Julian, shows Moses was more ancient than earliest Gentile heroes
      "Saint Cyril, in book 1 Against Julian, shows that Moses was more ancient"
    • Praises of Moses from Scripture and the Fathers — Provides learned chronology comparing Moses's life with Gentile heroes: Prometheus, Atlas, Cecrops, Deucalion, Cadmus, etc.
      "Hear his learned chronology of Moses and the Gentiles"

    Chapter I (The Six Days of Creation)

    • He Created — Cited from Book V of the Thesaurus, ch. 4, on the meaning of "to create"
      "So St. Cyril, book V of the Thesaurus, ch. 4"
    • Introduction — Cited for writing five books plus Glaphyra on Genesis
      "Blessed Cyril wrote five books, to which add his Glaphyra"
    • Verse 20: Let the Waters Bring Forth — Listed among Fathers who hold that birds were produced from water
      "the common opinion of St. Jerome, Augustine, Cyril, Damascene, and other Fathers"
    • Verse 26: Let Us Make Man in Our Image and Likeness — Cited from Book I Against Julian for denouncing the idea that angels created man
      "Cyril in Book I Against Julian"
