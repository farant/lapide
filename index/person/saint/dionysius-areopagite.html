St. Dionysius the Areopagite

Also known as
Pseudo-Dionysius, the divine Dionysius
Dates
traditionally 1st century (writings c. 5th–6th century)
Role
Disciple of Paul (traditional), mystical theologian

Called "the summit of theologians" and "bird of heaven" by all antiquity. Author of On the Divine Names, On Mystical Theology, and the Celestial Hierarchy.

Works

  • De Coelesti Hierarchia

References in Commentary

  • Proemium Et Encomium Sacrae Scripturae — Chapter I P3 — Called "summit of theologians"; professes to proceed from Sacred Scripture as principle; extensive quotation from On the Divine Names and On Mystical Theology
    "This was seen by that divine Dionysius, whom all antiquity regarded as the summit of theologians"
  • Proemium Et Encomium Sacrae Scripturae — Chapter Ii P19 — Opens his Celestial Hierarchy with instruction to proceed to understand Sacred Scriptures as received from the Fathers
    "St. Dionysius, with finger pointing to the sources, opens his Celestial Hierarchy thus"
  • Proemium Et Encomium Sacrae Scripturae — Chapter Iv P21 — As disciple of Paul, teaches Scriptures were handed down to be taught to disciples in continuous succession
    "Thus St. Dionysius, the disciple of the Apostle Paul"
