St. Justin Martyr

Also known as
Justin Philosopher and Martyr, Iustinus
Dates
c. 100–c. 165
Role
Early Christian apologist, philosopher, martyr

Early Christian apologist, philosopher, and martyr. Wandered through philosophical sects before being directed to the Prophets and becoming a Christian.

Works

  • Dialogue with Trypho
  • Exhortation to the Greeks

References in Commentary

  • Proemium Et Encomium Sacrae Scripturae — Chapter I P5 — Wandered through sects (Stoic, Peripatetic, Pythagorean, Platonist) seeking wisdom; met a divine philosopher who directed him to the Prophets; became Christian, Philosopher, and Martyr
    "St. Justin, Philosopher and Martyr, learned this and experienced it to his great benefit."
  • Proemium Et Encomium Sacrae Scripturae — Chapter V P19 — His divine catechist recommended reading the Prophets with prayers: "desire that the gates of light be opened to you"
    "that divine catechist of Justin Martyr, recommending to him the reading of the Prophets"

    "Hear Saint Justin in his Exhortation, or Paranaesis to the Greeks"
