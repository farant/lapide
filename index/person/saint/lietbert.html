St. Lietbert of Cambrai

Also known as
Lietbertus, Lietbertus Cameracensis
Dates
c. 1010–1076
Role
Bishop of Cambrai

Bishop of Cambrai known for his equanimity, contempt for money, and his pilgrimage to Jerusalem drawing three thousand pilgrims. At his death, his grey hairs miraculously returned to youthful color.

References in Commentary

  • Preliminares — Dedicatory Letter P41 — Avoided injuries, bore them equably; despised love of money; departed for Jerusalem drawing three thousand pilgrims; his holiness manifested by miracle: grey hairs after death returned to youthful color
    "On departing for Jerusalem he drew with him three thousand men, who accompanied him on pilgrimage."
