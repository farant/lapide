    St. Leo the Great

    Also known as
    Leo Magnus, Pope Leo I
    Dates
    c. 400–461
    Role
    Pope, Doctor of the Church

    Pope and Doctor of the Church. One of only three popes called "the Great." Known for his Tome on Christology and his confrontation with Attila the Hun.

    Chapter I (The Six Days of Creation)

    • God (Elohim): Thirteen Definitions — Quoted from Sermon 2 On the Passion for the second definition of God
      "Saint Leo, Sermon 2 On the Passion: "God is He whose nature is goodness, whose will is power, whose work is mercy.""
