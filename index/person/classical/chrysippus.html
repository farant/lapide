    Chrysippus

    Dates
    c. 279–c. 206 BC
    Role
    Stoic philosopher

    Third head of the Stoic school. Systematized Stoic philosophy and is often considered the second founder of Stoicism.

    Chapter I (The Six Days of Creation)

    • Verse 24: Let the Earth Bring Forth the Living Creature — Cited (through Plutarch) for saying bedbugs and mice are useful to man
      "Chrysippus, as Plutarch attests in Book V On Nature, said that bedbugs and mice are very useful to man"
