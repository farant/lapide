    Apollo

    Dates
    mythological
    Role
    Greek and Roman god of prophecy, music, and the sun

    Greek and Roman god of prophecy, music, and the sun. Invoked as a contrast to the true God speaking through Scripture. Associated with the oracle at Delphi.

    Preface and Praise of Sacred Scripture

    • Section One — "Not Apollo from his tripod" — contrasted with God speaking through Scripture
      "where you may hear not Apollo from his tripod, but God himself"
    • Moses as the Most Ancient Theologian, Philosopher, Poet, and Historian — Listed among figures preceded by Moses
      "Aesculapius, Apollo"
    • Praises of Moses from Scripture and the Fathers — "The golden Apollo" at Delphi where Dionysus's tomb is said to be
      "whose tomb they say is at Delphi near the golden Apollo"

    Commentary on the Pentateuch of Moses

    • Argumentum — Named among those older than Homer/Socrates whom Moses preceded
      "Orpheus, Linus, Musaeus, Hercules, Aesculapius, Apollo"
