    Plutarch

    Also known as
    Plutarchus
    Dates
    c. 46–c. 120
    Role
    Greek historian, biographer, philosopher

    Greek historian, biographer, and philosopher. Author of the Parallel Lives and Moralia. His work On the Education of Children teaches that memory is the storehouse of learning.

    Works

    • De Tranquillitate Animi
    • On the Education of Children

    Preface and Praise of Sacred Scripture

    • Chapter V: On the Dispositions Required for This Study — In On the Education of Children, teaches memory is the storehouse of learning
      "Plutarch, in his book On the Education of Children, teaches that memory is the storehouse of learning."

    Chapter I (The Six Days of Creation)

    • He Created — Listed among philosophers affirming the world was made by God
      "So Plato, the Stoics, Cicero, Plutarch, and Aristotle"
    • Verse 20: Let the Waters Bring Forth — Cited alongside Pliny and Aldrovandus on the remora fish
      "Pliny, Plutarch, and Aldrovandus likewise report the same about the remora"
    • Verse 24: Let the Earth Bring Forth the Living Creature — Cited for reporting Chrysippus's view on bedbugs and mice
      "Chrysippus, as Plutarch attests in Book V On Nature"
    • Verse 26: Let Us Make Man in Our Image and Likeness — Cited from his book On Tranquillity of Mind on the world as God's temple
      "Diogenes, as Plutarch attests in his book On Tranquillity of Mind"
