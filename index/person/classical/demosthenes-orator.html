Demosthenes (the orator)

Also known as
Demosthenes of Athens
Dates
384–322 BC
Role
Athenian statesman and orator

Athenian statesman and orator. Jerome calls him "the eloquent Demosthenes" who was ignorant of the mystery of the Logos.

References in Commentary

  • Clemens Hieronymi Du Culte — Jerome To Paulinus P2 — His oration against Aeschines was read at Rhodes; all marveled and praised it; Jerome calls him "the eloquent Demosthenes" who was ignorant of the mystery of the Logos
    "that oration of Demosthenes was read which he had delivered against him"
  • Clemens Hieronymi Du Culte — Jerome To Paulinus P2 — Contrasted with John's knowledge of the Word
    "this the eloquent Demosthenes was ignorant of"
  • Proemium Et Encomium Sacrae Scripturae — Chapter V P4 — Augustine says as Demosthenes gave first, second, third place to pronunciation in eloquence, so he gives first, second, third to humility in wisdom
    "just as Demosthenes gave first, second, and third place in eloquence to pronunciation"
