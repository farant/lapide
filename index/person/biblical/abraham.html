Abraham

Also known as
Abrahamus, Abram
Dates
c. 2000 BC (traditional)
Role
Patriarch, father of the faith

Patriarch and father of the faith. Listed among the ancient patriarchs who held both supreme powers (prince and priest) in conjunction.

References in Commentary

  • Preliminares — Dedicatory Letter P5 — Listed among ancient patriarchs who held both supreme powers (prince and priest) in conjunction, along with Melchizedek, Isaac, Jacob
    "in Melchizedek, Abraham, Isaac, Jacob, and the other ancient patriarchs, both supreme powers — that is, of the prince and of the priest — were held in conjunction"
  • Preliminares — Dedicatory Letter P10 — Moses' ancestor: Abraham begot Isaac
    "Abraham begot Isaac, Isaac Jacob, Jacob Levi, Levi Caath, Caath Amram, and Amram Moses."
  • Clemens Hieronymi Du Culte — Jerome To Paulinus P2 — Abraham saw Christ's day and rejoiced
    "Abraham saw his day and rejoiced."

  • Proemium Et Encomium Sacrae Scripturae — Utility Ii P6 — Praised for obedience: resolved to seal obedience to God with sacrifice of Isaac
    "Who would not rather praise Abraham and Isaac, those innocents"
