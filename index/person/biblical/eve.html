    Eve

    Role
    First woman

    First woman, wife of Adam. Named among those given meaningful names in Scripture.

    Commentary on the Pentateuch of Moses

    • Canons Bearing a Torch Before the Pentateuch — The narration of Adam, Eve, and paradise is to be taken historically
      "the things he narrates about paradise, Adam, Eve, and the creation of all things"
    • Canons Bearing a Torch Before the Pentateuch — Listed among those given meaningful names
      "That this is so is clear in the names Adam, Eve, Seth, Cain, Noah"

    Chapter I (The Six Days of Creation)

    • God (Elohim): Thirteen Definitions — Cited as having called God Elohim from the beginning
      "from the beginning of the world Adam and Eve called God Elohim."
    • Verse 27: Male and Female He Created Them — Created as female on the sixth day
      "He created Adam as male and Eve as female."
    • Verse 28: Increase and Multiply — Created in mature age fit for generation
      "Adam and Eve were created in a mature age and stature, and fit for generation"
    • Verse 31: And God Saw All Things That He Had Made, and They Were Very Good — Ambrose's reason: the good of the human race depended on Eve
      "the good of the human race, namely fecundity and propagation, depended on Eve"
