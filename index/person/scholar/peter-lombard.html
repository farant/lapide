    Peter Lombard

    Also known as
    Petrus Lombardus
    Dates
    c. 1096–1160
    Role
    Bishop of Paris, theologian, author of the Sentences

    Bishop of Paris and theologian. Author of the Sentences, the standard theological textbook of the Middle Ages. Among the scholars who used Jerome's version of Scripture exclusively.

    Works

    • Libri Quatuor Sententiarum

    Preliminaries

    • PREFACE TO THE READER (1) — Listed among scholars who used Jerome's version
      "Rupert, Peter Lombard, Alexander"

    Chapter I (The Six Days of Creation)

    • God (Elohim): Thirteen Definitions — Cited as "the Master" alongside the Scholastics for Elohim implying a plurality of persons
      "the Master [Peter Lombard], and the Scholastics teach"
    • Verse 3: And God said: Let there be light — Cited as "the Master" for the view that light was a luminous body
      "Bede, Hugh, the Master, St. Thomas, St. Bonaventure"
    • Heaven and Earth: Four Interpretations — Cited as "the Master" alongside the Scholastics
      "so with Saint Augustine, Gregory, Rupert, and Bede teach the Master and the Scholastics."
