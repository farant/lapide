    Ambrosius Catharinus

    Also known as
    Catharinus
    Dates
    1484–1553
    Role
    Dominican theologian

    Dominican theologian who participated in the Council of Trent and wrote extensively on Genesis.

    Chapter I (The Six Days of Creation)

    • God (Elohim): Thirteen Definitions — Cited for Elohim implying plurality of persons
      "Catharinus, the Master [Peter Lombard], and the Scholastics"
    • Verse 3: And God said: Let there be light — Cited for thinking the first light was the sun
      "Catharinus answers first that it was the most brilliant sun"
    • Verse 6: Let there be a firmament — Cited for the upper waters as crystalline heaven
      "Abulensis, Cajetan, Catharinus"
    • Verse 6: Let there be a firmament — Cited for the reason God did not say "it was good" on the second day
      "Catharinus and Molina respond"
    • Verse 9: Let the waters be gathered together — Cited alongside Basil for the sea being made higher
      "Basil, Burgensis, Catharinus, and St. Thomas"
    • Verse 20: Let the Waters Bring Forth — Cited alongside Cajetan for denying birds were from water
      "Cajetan and Catharinus deny this"
    • Heaven and Earth: Four Interpretations — Listed and cited as asserting the empyrean opinion is the most true
      "and Catharinus to be the most true."
