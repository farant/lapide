    Thomas de Vio Cajetan

    Also known as
    Cajetan, Cardinal Cajetan
    Dates
    1469–1534
    Role
    Dominican theologian, cardinal

    Dominican theologian and cardinal, best known for his commentaries on the Summa Theologica of Thomas Aquinas.

    Chapter I (The Six Days of Creation)

    • God (Elohim): Thirteen Definitions — Named as opponent of the view that Elohim implies plurality of persons
      "against Cajetan and Abulensis"
    • Verse 2: And the earth was without form and void — Cited for identifying the Spirit of the Lord as an angel
      "The Spirit of the Lord -- that is, an angel, says Cajetan"
    • Verse 6: Let there be a firmament — Cited for the view that the upper waters are the crystalline heaven
      "Bonaventure, Lyra, Abulensis, Cajetan, Catharinus"
    • Verse 9: Let the waters be gathered together — Listed among those holding earth and water form one globe
      "Molina, Pererius, Cajetan"
    • Verse 11: Let the earth bring forth — Cited alongside Burgensis for earth actively producing (which Lapide rejects)
      "Cajetan and Burgensis hold"
    • Verse 20: Let the Waters Bring Forth — Cited alongside Catharinus for denying birds were made from water
      "Cajetan and Catharinus deny this"
