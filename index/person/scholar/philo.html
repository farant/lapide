Philo of Alexandria

Also known as
Philo the Jew, Philo Judaeus
Dates
c. 25 BC–c. 50 AD
Role
Hellenistic Jewish philosopher

Hellenistic Jewish philosopher of Alexandria. His works include On the Contemplative Life, describing the Essene Christians, and The Life of Moses.

References in Commentary

  • Proemium Et Encomium Sacrae Scripturae — Chapter Iv P17 — As an eyewitness, in On the Contemplative Life, describes how Essene Christians at Alexandria spent entire day reading Scripture
    "One may see in Philo the Jew, an eyewitness, in his book On the Contemplative Life"
  • Proemium Et Encomium Sacrae Scripturae — Chapter V P14 — Says "The legitimate worshippers of God excel in mind; for the true priest of God is at the same time also a seer"
    "as Philo, though a Jew, rightly says"
  • Proemium Et Encomium Sacrae Scripturae — Moses And Christ P10 — "This is the life, this is the death of Moses, king, legislator, pontiff, prophet" — from book 3 of The Life of Moses
    "Hear Philo, the most learned of the Hebrews"
  • Commentaria In Pentateuchum Mosis Canones — Argumentum P2 — Holds the view that Moses divided and named the Pentateuch (Lapide disagrees, attributing this to the Seventy Translators)
    "which were thus divided and named not by Moses, as Philo holds, but by the Seventy Translators"
