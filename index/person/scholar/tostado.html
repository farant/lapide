    Alfonso Tostado

    Also known as
    Blessed Alfonso Tostado, Bishop of Avila, Tostado
    Dates
    c. 1400–1455
    Role
    Bishop of Avila, biblical scholar

    Bishop of Avila and prolific biblical scholar. Composed individual volumes on the Decateuch and individual Old Testament history books with keen judgment.

    Preface and Praise of Sacred Scripture

    • Saintly Examples of Scripture Study — On the Decateuch and individual OT history books, composed individual volumes with keen judgment; Lapide once pored over him and rereads him
      "I pass over Blessed Alfonso Tostado, Bishop of Avila"

    Commentary on the Pentateuch of Moses

    • Argumentum — Holds the better view that Genesis was written after the departure from Egypt
      "Theodoret, Bede, and Tostatus hold a better view"

    Chapter I (The Six Days of Creation)

    • Introduction — Praised for writing at greater length than all others with great examination and judgment
      "Alphonsus Tostatus, Bishop of Avila, wrote at greater length than all others"
    • Introduction — Given the eulogy: "Here is the wonder of the world"
      "Here is the wonder of the world, who examines every knowable thing."
    • Introduction — Noted for dying in his fortieth year
      "For he died in his fortieth year."
    • God (Elohim): Thirteen Definitions — Named alongside Cajetan as an opponent
      "against Cajetan and Abulensis"
    • Verse 3: And God said: Let there be light — Cited for the view that light was a luminous body
      "Lyra, and Abulensis hold"
    • Verse 6: Let there be a firmament — Cited for the upper waters as crystalline heaven
      "Abulensis, Cajetan"
    • Verse 21: And God Created the Great Sea Creatures — Cited alongside Lyra for reporting the Jewish fable about the whales
      "as Lyra and Abulensis report."
    • Verse 24: Let the Earth Bring Forth the Living Creature — Cited in chapter 13 for the view that carnivorous animals did not exist before sin
      "Abulensis, in chapter 13, where he treats these matters at length."
    • Heaven and Earth: Four Interpretations — Listed among supporters of the empyrean heaven interpretation
      "Junilius, Bede, Abulensis, Catharinus"
