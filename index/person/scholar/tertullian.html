    Tertullian

    Also known as
    Tertullianus, Quintus Septimius Florens Tertullianus
    Dates
    c. 155–c. 240
    Role
    Early Church writer and apologist; later joined Montanism

    Early Church writer and apologist. Cyprian read him daily, calling him "the Master." Left the Church for Montanism and is therefore not canonized.

    Works

    • Adversus Hermogenem
    • Against Marcion
    • Apologeticus
    • On the Apparel of Women

    Preface and Praise of Sacred Scripture

    • Chapter V: On the Dispositions Required for This Study — Jerome records Cyprian reading Tertullian daily, saying "Give me the Master!"
      "of Cyprian reading Tertullian daily"
    • The Fathers' Defense of the Old Testament — Wrote four books Against Marcion in defense of the Old Testament
      "Tertullian wrote for the same cause four books Against Marcion."
    • The Septuagint Translation and Greek Translators — In Apologeticus, testifies the Septuagint was preserved in the Alexandrian library to his own times
      "Tertullian in his Apologeticus testifies"
    • IV. The Old Testament Surpasses the New in Allegorical Richness — In On the Apparel of Women, chapter 22, holds that no pronouncement of the Holy Spirit serves only the present matter
      "Tertullian in his book On the Apparel of Women, chapter 22"
    • VI. The Old Testament as Forerunner to the New — Teaches everywhere against Marcion that agreement of both Testaments bears testimony to Christ
      "as Tertullian teaches everywhere against Marcion"

    Chapter I (The Six Days of Creation)

    • In the Beginning: Nine Interpretations — Cited from the book Against Hermogenes for the fourth interpretation "In sovereignty"
      "God made heaven and earth, says Tertullian, in the book Against Hermogenes."
    • He Created — Cited for rightly objecting against Hermogenes
      "as Tertullian rightly objects against Hermogenes."
    • Verse 2: And the earth was without form and void — Cited from Against Hermogenes, ch. 32, for the interpretation that the Spirit of the Lord is a wind
      "the Hebrews, Theodoret, and Tertullian Against Hermogenes, ch. 32, say"
    • Verse 24: Let the Earth Bring Forth the Living Creature — Quoted from Book I Against Marcion, chapter 14, on the greatness of God in small creatures
      "Hear Tertullian, Book I Against Marcion, chapter 14"
