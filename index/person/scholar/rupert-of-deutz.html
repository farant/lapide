    Rupert of Deutz

    Also known as
    Rupertus Tuitiensis, Rupert
    Dates
    c. 1075–1129
    Role
    Benedictine abbot and theologian at Deutz

    Benedictine abbot and theologian at Deutz. Among the scholars who used Jerome's version of Scripture exclusively.

    Preliminaries

    • PREFACE TO THE READER (1) — Listed among scholars who used Jerome's version
      "Bernard, Rupert, Peter Lombard"

    Chapter I (The Six Days of Creation)

    • Introduction — Listed among those who wrote on Genesis
      "Eucherius, Rupert, and others wrote on Genesis."
    • He Created — Cited from Book I on Genesis, ch. 3, on "create" meaning "from nothing"
      "Rupert, book I on Genesis, ch. 3; Bede and Lyra here."
    • He Created — Cited for three reasons why Moses used "created" rather than "said"
      "So Rupert, who assigns three reasons."
    • Verse 6: Let there be a firmament — Cited for the view that the firmament is the middle region of air
      "Rupert, Eugubinus, Pererius"
    • Verse 20: Let the Waters Bring Forth — Exception among Fathers: holds birds were NOT produced from water
      "the common opinion of St. Jerome, Augustine, Cyril, Damascene, and other Fathers (except Rupert)"
    • Verse 24: Let the Earth Bring Forth the Living Creature — Cited alongside Molina for hybrid animals not needing to be created on the sixth day
      "So Rupert, Molina, and others"
    • Verse 26: Let Us Make Man in Our Image and Likeness — Cited alongside Basil for the Trinity interpretation of "Let Us make"
      "So St. Basil, Rupert, and others cited above"
    • Verse 26: Let Us Make Man in Our Image and Likeness — Cited for marveling at God's deliberation about creating man
      "so Rupert."
    • Verse 26: Let Us Make Man in Our Image and Likeness — Cited for the mystical interpretation of image as the Son and likeness as the Holy Spirit
      "However, secondly, "image" can properly be taken here as a Hebraism"
    • Verse 28: Increase and Multiply — Cited alongside Chrysostom and Augustine for no precept of procreation
      "So say St. Chrysostom, Rupert, and Augustine"
    • Heaven and Earth: Four Interpretations — Cited for angels created simultaneously with the world
      "so with Saint Augustine, Gregory, Rupert, and Bede"
