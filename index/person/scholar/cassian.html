    John Cassian

    Also known as
    Cassian, Joannes Cassianus
    Dates
    c. 360–c. 435
    Role
    Monk, ascetic writer, founder of monasteries at Marseilles

    Monk and ascetic writer who founded monasteries at Marseilles. His writings on monastic life and spiritual practices were influential in Western monasticism.

    Preface and Praise of Sacred Scripture

    • Chapter V: On the Dispositions Required for This Study — Records Theodore, a holy monk so unlettered he didn't know the alphabet, yet so skilled in divine volumes that he was consulted by the most learned; said effort must go to uprooting vices rather than perusing books
      "Cassian records that Theodore, a holy monk"
