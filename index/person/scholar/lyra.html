    Nicholas of Lyra

    Also known as
    Lyra, Lyranus
    Dates
    c. 1270–1349
    Role
    Franciscan biblical exegete

    Franciscan friar and one of the most influential biblical commentators of the Middle Ages. His Postillae perpetuae influenced Luther.

    Chapter I (The Six Days of Creation)

    • He Created — Cited for "to create" meaning "from nothing"
      "Bede and Lyra here."
    • Introduction — Named among commentators after whom more recent authors wrote
      "Many more recent authors also wrote on Genesis after Lyra, Hugo, and Denis the Carthusian"
    • Verse 3: And God said: Let there be light — Cited for the view that light was a luminous body
      "Lyra, and Abulensis"
    • Verse 6: Let there be a firmament — Cited for the upper waters as crystalline heaven
      "Bonaventure, Lyra, Abulensis"
    • Verse 21: And God Created the Great Sea Creatures — Cited alongside Abulensis for reporting the Jewish fable about whales
      "as Lyra and Abulensis report."
    • Heaven and Earth: Four Interpretations — Listed as supporter of the empyrean heaven interpretation
      "Lyranus"
