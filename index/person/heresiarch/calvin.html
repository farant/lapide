    John Calvin

    Also known as
    Calvin, Johannes Calvinus
    Dates
    1509–1564
    Role
    Protestant reformer, founder of Calvinism

    Protestant reformer who misinterpreted Scripture to prove God is the author of evil, constructed a doctrine of reprobation from the hardening of Pharaoh, and established bondage of the will from Jeremiah 18:6.

    Preface and Praise of Sacred Scripture

    • I. The Old Testament Establishes Faith — From David's saying proves God is author of evil; from Exodus constructs fate of reprobation; from Jeremiah establishes bondage of the will
      "Calvin, from that saying of David"
    • Chapter V: On the Dispositions Required for This Study — The apostate Dudecius "fled to the camp of Calvin"
      "that apostate fled to the camp of Calvin."

    Chapter I (The Six Days of Creation)

    • Introduction — Named as a danger in these times regarding extolling faith excessively
      "for he greatly extols faith, which on account of Luther and Calvin is dangerous in these times."
    • Verse 6: Let there be a firmament — Named for laughing at the opinion of waters above the heavens; called foolish
      "Calvin laughs at this; but foolishly"
