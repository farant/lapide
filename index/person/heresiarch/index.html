    Heresiarch

    • Arius (c. 256–336) — Originator of Arianism
    • Audaeus (4th century) — Originator of the Anthropomorphite heresy
    • Epicurus (341–270 BC) — Greek philosopher (atomism)
    • Faustus the Manichaean (fl. late 4th century) — Manichaean bishop, opponent of Augustine
    • Hermogenes (2nd century) — Gnostic heretic who held matter was eternal
    • John Calvin (1509–1564) — Protestant reformer, founder of Calvinism
    • Mani (c. 216–c. 274) — Founder of Manichaeism
    • Marcion (c. 85–c. 160) — Heresiarch, founder of Marcionism
    • Martin Luther (1483–1546) — Protestant reformer, founder of Lutheranism
    • Matthias Flacius Illyricus (1520–1575) — Lutheran theologian
    • Photinus (d. 376) — Bishop of Sirmium; heresiarch who denied the pre-existence of Christ
