    Mani

    Also known as
    Curbicus the Persian, Manes, Manichaeus
    Dates
    c. 216–c. 274
    Role
    Founder of Manichaeism

    Called Curbicus the Persian, whom his own people called Manes and Manichaeus ("as if a pourer-out of manna, by way of honor"). Founded Manichaeism, a heresy that, together with Simon Magus and Marcion, taught the Old Testament was produced by evil angels.

    Preface and Praise of Sacred Scripture

    • Heresies Proscribing the Old Testament — Called Curbicus the Persian; with Simon and Marcion, taught OT was produced by evil angels
      "Curbicus the Persian (whom his own people called Manes and Manichaeus, as if a pourer-out of manna, by way of honor)"

    Chapter I (The Six Days of Creation)

    • God (Elohim): Thirteen Definitions — Named alongside Pythagoras for the error of two principles
      "Pythagoras, the Manichaeans, and the Priscillianists err"
    • Verse 4: And God saw the light that it was good — Named as opponents against whom Moses teaches all things are good
      "against the Manichaeans we may know that nothing evil, but all things good, were produced by God."
