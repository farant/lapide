    Emperor Hadrian

    Also known as
    Hadrian, Emperor Hadrian
    Dates
    76–138
    Role
    Roman Emperor

    Roman Emperor in whose twelfth year Aquila of Pontus translated the Hebrew Scriptures into Greek.

    Preface and Praise of Sacred Scripture

    • The Septuagint Translation and Greek Translators — In the 12th year of Hadrian, Aquila of Pontus translated Hebrew Scripture into Greek
      "in the 12th year of the Emperor Hadrian"

    Chapter I (The Six Days of Creation)

    • Heaven and Earth: Four Interpretations — Questioned the philosopher Secundus about the world, God, Ocean, Earth
      "questioned by the emperor Hadrian"
    • Verse 16: And God Made Two Great Lights — Questioned Secundus and Epictetus about the sun, moon, stars
      "questioned by the Emperor Hadrian"
