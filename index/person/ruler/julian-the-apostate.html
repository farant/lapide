Julian the Apostate

Also known as
Iulianus Apostata, Flavius Claudius Julianus
Dates
331–363
Role
Roman Emperor (361–363); apostate from Christianity

Roman Emperor who renounced Christianity and attempted to restore paganism. Basil the Great overcame him through prayer: standing before an image of Our Lady on which the martyr Mercury was also depicted, Basil saw the martyr's figure appear first dim, then holding a bloodied spear, signifying Julian's death.
