Cornelius a Lapide

Also known as
Cornelius Cornelii a Lapide
Dates
1567–1637
Role
Jesuit priest, Scripture professor at Louvain and Rome, biblical commentator

Jesuit priest and biblical commentator whose life-work of Scripture commentary is one of the most comprehensive ever produced. Born in Bocholt (region of Eupen), he entered the Society of Jesus on 8 July 1592. He taught Scripture at Louvain for over twenty years, was summoned to Rome, and devoted his final years to private writing and contemplation. He harbored a lifelong desire for martyrdom, nearly fulfilled in 1604 at the shrine of Our Lady of Aspromont. He died in Rome on 12 March 1637. His works span nearly the entire Bible, from Genesis through the Apocalypse, with only Job and Psalms left incomplete.
