    Jerome de Oleastro

    Also known as
    Hieronymus ab Oleastro, Oleaster
    Dates
    1507–1563
    Role
    Dominican biblical commentator

    Dominican biblical commentator known for his literal-oriented commentary on the Pentateuch.

    References in Commentary

    • Genesis 1 — He Created P8 — Cited for translating Hebrew bara as "divided" rather than "created" (rejected by Lapide)
      "Jerome de Oleastro translates the Hebrew bara as 'divided.'"
    • Genesis 1 — Introduction P11 — Listed among authors recommended for moral teachings
      "Jerome de Oleastro, Pererius"
    • Genesis 1 — Verse 26 P11 — Cited alongside Eugubinus for thinking God assumed human form to create man
      "Oleaster and Eugubinus in the Cosmopoeia think that God here assumed a human form"
