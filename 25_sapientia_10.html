Synopsis of the Chapter

He commemorates the salvation which through wisdom was obtained by Adam, Noah, Abraham, Lot, Jacob, Joseph, Moses, and the Hebrews crossing the Red Sea, while the Egyptians were drowned in it.

Vulgate Text: Wisdom 10:1-21

1. She preserved him who was first formed by God, the father of the world, when he had been created alone, 2. and brought him out of his sin, and gave him the power to govern all things. 3. But when the unjust departed from her in his anger, he perished through the anger of fraternal homicide. 4. When on his account water was destroying the earth, wisdom healed it again, directing the just man through a contemptible piece of wood. 5. She also, when the nations had conspired together in wickedness, knew the just man and preserved him blameless before God, and kept him strong in his compassion for his son. 6. She delivered the just man fleeing from the perishing ungodly, when fire came down upon the Pentapolis: 7. as a testimony of whose wickedness the desolate smoking land still stands, and trees bearing fruit at uncertain times, and a pillar of salt standing as a memorial of an unbelieving soul. 8. For passing by wisdom, they not only fell so as not to know what is good, but also left to men a memorial of their folly, so that in the things in which they sinned they could not even lie hidden. 9. But wisdom delivered from sorrows those who attend to her. 10. She conducted the just man who fled from his brother's wrath through the right ways, and showed him the kingdom of God, and gave him the knowledge of holy things: she enriched him in his labors, and completed his labors. 11. In the deceit of those who circumvented him she stood by him, and made him honorable. 12. She kept him safe from his enemies, and defended him from seducers, and gave him a strong contest that he might overcome, and might know that wisdom is mightier than all things. 13. She did not forsake the just man when he was sold, but delivered him from sinners: she went down with him into the pit, 14. and did not leave him in bonds, until she brought him the scepter of the kingdom, and power against those who oppressed him: and showed them to be liars who had stained him, and gave him everlasting glory. 15. She delivered the just people and blameless seed from the nations that oppressed them. 16. She entered into the soul of the servant of God, and stood against dreadful kings in wonders and signs. 17. And she rendered to the just the reward of their labors, and conducted them in a wonderful way: and she was to them for a covering by day, and for the light of the stars by night. 18. She brought them over the Red Sea, and carried them through a great water. 19. But she drowned their enemies in the sea, and from the depth of the abyss she brought them out. Therefore the just took the spoils of the ungodly. 20. And they sung to Your holy name, O Lord, and praised with one accord Your victorious hand: 21. for wisdom opened the mouth of the mute, and made the tongues of infants eloquent.
