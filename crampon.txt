Crampon Footnotes
=================

Footnotes added by Augustin Crampon (1826-1894) to his edition of Lapide's
commentaries. Identified by: italic <p><em> blocks beginning with (1), (2), etc.,
containing references to post-Lapide scholars (Gesenius, Rosenmüller, Patrizzi,
Drach, etc.). To be removed from the English text and all translations.


01_genesis_01.html
------------------

- Line 111: "(1) To these add Jean de la Haye, Commentary on Genesis, Lyon 1638;
  Drechsler, The Unity and Authenticity of Genesis, 1838."
  Bibliographic addition.

- Lines 153-155: "(1) Patrizzi, in the work entitled On the Interpretation of the
  Sacred Scriptures..." + continuation paragraph with arguments a) through f) for
  reading "In the beginning" as "In the Son." Cites Patrizzi, Drach, Fabretti.

- Line 225: "(1) Indeed, the word bara in the piel form means 'to cut down, to
  polish'..." Philological note on Hebrew bara. Cites Patricius, Rosenmüller,
  Gesenius, Kocher.

- Line 273: "(1) Elohim is the plural of the noun eloah, from the root alah..."
  Philological note on Elohim. Cites 19th-century Hebrew grammar conventions.

- Lines 297-299: "(1) So also Patricius, at the place cited..." + "The noun
  shamayim, 'heavens,' is not, as is commonly believed, dual, but plural..."
  Two paragraphs. Cites Patricius, Schroeder, Gesenius.

- Line 801: "(Here terrestrial animals are divided into three classes, of which the
  first is called by the name behemah..." Parenthetical gloss on Hebrew animal
  classification (behemah, hayyat, remes). No (1) marker but same editorial style.


01_genesis_02.html
------------------

- Line 163: "In place of rain and irrigation there was a vapor, by which the entire
  surface of the earth was moistened..." Quotes Rosenmüller on the primordial vapor.
  No (1) marker but italic editorial paragraph citing a post-Lapide scholar.


01_genesis_03.html
------------------

- Lines 113: "(1) But not quite correctly. Cunning is here ascribed to serpents..."
  Note on Hebrew aram. Cites Chateaubriand (post-Lapide).

- Line 115: "(2) What Moses related as having transpired between Eve and the serpent,
  interpreters of the Rationalist school count among myths (De Wette...Hartmann...)..."
  Long note defending historicity of the serpent narrative. Cites Patritius, Meignan,
  Eichhorn, Lacordaire. Extensively quotes Patritius.

- Line 247: "(1) The word qol is ambiguous: for it signifies both the voice of one
  speaking and any kind of noise..." Note on Hebrew qol in Gen 3:8.

- Line 249: "(2) Ruach hayom, 'the breathing' or 'respiration of the day,' is the
  evening." Short note on Hebrew ruach hayom in Gen 3:8.

- Line 293: "(1) Not that it feeds on dust alone, but because, since it crawls on the
  ground, it cannot help but take dust into its mouth..." Gloss on serpent eating dust.

- Line 309: "(1) God, passing sentence on the serpent that had deceived Eve, at the same
  time uttered those words which... have received the name 'Protoevangelium.'" Cites
  Sherlock, Patritius, Meignan.

- Lines 311: "(2) 'And between your seed and her seed.' These words first reveal clearly
  enough..." Exegetical note on the Protoevangelium. Extensively quotes Patritius.

- Line 313: "(3) 'We have few authoritative testimonies of any weight that certainly
  support the Vulgate interpreter in rendering the Hebrew hu as the feminine pronoun
  she.'" Textual-critical note on ipsa/ipse. Quotes Patritius at length.

- Line 329: "(1) The verb shuph, which occurs twice in this sentence, has been rendered
  in many ways..." Philological note on shuph. Cites Rosenmüller, Meignan.

- Line 365: "(1) So Rosenmuller. Your husband, even after those most severe pains
  of childbirth, you will desire again..." Short note on Gen 3:16. Cites Rosenmüller.

- Line 367: "(2) In which sense Psalm 123:2 says the eyes of servants are toward
  their masters." Short note on Gen 3:16.

- Line 487: "(1) Cf. Hengstenberg, Moses and Egypt; Meignan, op. cit. p. 135; Obry..."
  Bibliographic note on the Cherubim. Cites Hengstenberg, Meignan, Obry, Haneberg,
  De Saulcy — all post-Lapide scholars.


01_genesis_04.html
------------------

- Line 131: "(1) Because sacrifices seem to have been unknown before the Flood, Grotius
  judges that no victims were then slaughtered..." Cites Grotius (d. 1645, post-Lapide)
  and Drach.

- Line 151: "(2) And Cain was exceedingly angry. Hebrew: and Cain's [nose] burned
  greatly, that is, Aph, nose, meaning anger." Short Hebrew gloss.

- Line 167: "(1) So also Rosenmuller, for whom se'eth means 'elevation,' namely of your
  face, would be for you." Cites Rosenmüller.

- Line 197: "(1) Others: 'And to you shall be its desire,' namely of sin; 'but you shall
  rule over it,' that is, you must resist it." Alternative translation note.

- Line 261: "(1) 'From the earth,' that is, an exile from that land which opened its
  mouth, etc." Short gloss on Gen 4:11.

- Line 309: "(1) The region in which Adam had settled was considered to be in the sight
  of God, because God especially protected it..." Gloss on Cain's fear of exile.

- Line 319: "(2) 'Sevenfold' punishment stands for 'manifold.' Cf. Psalm 12:7; 118:164;
  Proverbs 24:16." Short note on "sevenfold."

- Line 359: "(1) Verse 20. 'And of herdsmen.' The Hebrew is mikneh, which word
  properly designates acquisition, possession, and then cattle." Hebrew gloss.

- Line 361: "(2) 'Organ' here seems to be a pipe or flute." Short gloss.

- Line 443: "On the tetragrammaton name of God, or Jehovah, see the most learned
  discussion by Drach, op. cit. vol. I, p. 319 ff." Bibliographic reference to Drach.
  No (1) marker.
