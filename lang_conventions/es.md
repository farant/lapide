# Spanish

Use formal scholarly/theological Castilian Spanish (registro culto). File suffix `_es`. Latin alphabet, LTR (no `dir` attribute needed). Biblical proper names follow Spanish Bible tradition (Biblia de Jerusalén / Reina-Valera): Moisés (Moses), Abrahán/Abraham (Abraham), Isaac (Isaac), Jacob (Jacob), Esaú (Esau), José (Joseph), Josué (Joshua), Isaías (Isaiah), Jeremías (Jeremiah), Adán (Adam), Eva (Eve), Noé (Noah), David (David), Salomón (Solomon), Elías (Elijah), Eliseo (Elisha), Aarón (Aaron), Gedeón (Gideon), Sansón (Samson), Débora (Deborah), Rut (Ruth), Samuel (Samuel), Ezequías (Hezekiah), Jonás (Jonah), Daniel (Daniel). Saint names use San/Santo/Santa following standard Spanish Catholic convention: San Agustín (St. Augustine), San Jerónimo (St. Jerome), San Basilio (St. Basil), San Ambrosio (St. Ambrose), San Juan Crisóstomo (St. Chrysostom), San Gregorio (St. Gregory), San Bernardo (St. Bernard), San Hilario (St. Hilary), San Cipriano (St. Cyprian), San Justino (St. Justin), San Clemente (St. Clement), San Atanasio (St. Athanasius), San Epifanio (St. Epiphanius), San Ignacio (St. Ignatius), San Juan Damasceno (John of Damascus), San Buenaventura (St. Bonaventure), San Francisco (St. Francis), Santo Domingo (St. Dominic), Santo Tomás (St. Thomas), San León (St. Leo), San Pablo (St. Paul), San Efrén (St. Ephrem). Note: "Santo" is used before Tomás and Domingo; "San" is used before all other male saints. Use Spanish angular quotation marks «...» (guillemets) for quotations and "..." for nested quotes. Spanish Catholic theological terminology: Sagrada Escritura (Holy Scripture), sacramento (sacrament), gracia (grace), pecado (sin), salvación (salvation), Padre de la Iglesia / Santo Padre (Church Father), Evangelio (Gospel), Génesis (Genesis), Éxodo (Exodus), Pentateuco (Pentateuch), Vulgata (Vulgate). Gospel references use Spanish Bible convention (e.g., «Juan 8, 12» or «Jn 8:12»). Psalm numbering follows the Vulgate convention used in the source text. The register should be formal scholarly Spanish suitable for theological commentary — registro culto, avoiding colloquial constructions. Proper use of diacritics (á, é, í, ó, ú, ñ, ü) and inverted punctuation (¿ ¡) is critical. Agents must be explicitly instructed to use guillemets «...» (not English "..." or Spanish comillas "...") for quotations, as they default to English-style quotes. Agents must also be reminded to use inverted question marks ¿ and exclamation marks ¡ — these are frequently omitted.
