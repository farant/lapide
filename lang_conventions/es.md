# Spanish

Use formal scholarly/theological Castilian Spanish (registro culto). File suffix `_es`. Latin alphabet, LTR (no `dir` attribute needed).

## Biblical proper names

Follow Spanish Bible tradition (Biblia de Jerusalén / Reina-Valera): Moisés (Moses), Abrahán/Abraham (Abraham), Isaac (Isaac), Jacob (Jacob), Esaú (Esau), José (Joseph), Josué (Joshua), Isaías (Isaiah), Jeremías (Jeremiah), Adán (Adam), Eva (Eve), Noé (Noah), David (David), Salomón (Solomon), Elías (Elijah), Eliseo (Elisha), Aarón (Aaron), Gedeón (Gideon), Sansón (Samson), Débora (Deborah), Rut (Ruth), Samuel (Samuel), Ezequías (Hezekiah), Jonás (Jonah), Daniel (Daniel).

## Saint names

Use San/Santo/Santa following standard Spanish Catholic convention:

San Agustín (St. Augustine), San Jerónimo (St. Jerome), San Basilio (St. Basil), San Ambrosio (St. Ambrose), San Juan Crisóstomo (St. Chrysostom), San Gregorio (St. Gregory), San Bernardo (St. Bernard), San Hilario (St. Hilary), San Cipriano (St. Cyprian), San Justino (St. Justin), San Clemente (St. Clement), San Atanasio (St. Athanasius), San Epifanio (St. Epiphanius), San Ignacio (St. Ignatius), San Juan Damasceno (John of Damascus), San Buenaventura (St. Bonaventure), San Francisco (St. Francis), Santo Domingo (St. Dominic), Santo Tomás (St. Thomas), San León (St. Leo), San Pablo (St. Paul), San Efrén (St. Ephrem).

Note: "Santo" is used before Tomás and Domingo; "San" is used before all other male saints.

## Quotation marks

Use Spanish angular quotation marks «...» (guillemets) for quotations and "..." for nested quotes.

## Theological terminology

Sagrada Escritura (Holy Scripture), sacramento (sacrament), gracia (grace), pecado (sin), salvación (salvation), Padre de la Iglesia / Santo Padre (Church Father), Evangelio (Gospel), Génesis (Genesis), Éxodo (Exodus), Pentateuco (Pentateuch), Vulgata (Vulgate).

## Bible references and psalm numbering

Gospel references use Spanish Bible convention (e.g., «Juan 8, 12» or «Jn 8:12»). Psalm numbering follows the Vulgate convention used in the source text.

## Register

Formal scholarly Spanish suitable for theological commentary — registro culto, avoiding colloquial constructions. Proper use of diacritics (á, é, í, ó, ú, ñ, ü) and inverted punctuation (¿ ¡) is critical.

## Agent pitfalls

- **English-style quotes**: Agents default to English "..." quotation marks instead of guillemets «...». Must be explicitly instructed.
- **Missing inverted punctuation**: Agents frequently omit inverted question marks ¿ and exclamation marks ¡. Must be explicitly reminded.
