# Swedish

Use formal scholarly Swedish (formellt akademiskt språk). File suffix `_sv`. Latin alphabet, LTR (no `dir` attribute needed). `lang="sv"` on the `<html>` tag.

## Biblical proper names

Follow Swedish Bible tradition (Bibel 2000 / Svenska Folkbibeln): Mose (Moses), Abraham, Isak (Isaac), Jakob (Jacob), Aron (Aaron), David, Josua (Joshua), Jesaja (Isaiah), Jeremia (Jeremiah), Adam, Eva (Eve), Noa (Noah).

**Genitive**: Swedish uses "Mose" as the genitive of Moses (e.g., "Mose Pentateuk", not "Moses Pentateuk"). Agents frequently produce "Moses" — always check.

## Saint names

Use S:t/S:ta (Sankt/Sankta) prefix following Swedish Catholic convention:

S:t Hieronymus (St. Jerome), S:t Basilius (St. Basil), S:t Augustinus (St. Augustine), S:t Ambrosius (St. Ambrose), S:t Johannes Chrysostomos (Chrysostom), S:t Gregorius (Gregory), S:t Bernhard (Bernard), S:t Hilarius (Hilary), S:t Cyprianus (Cyprian), S:t Justinus (Justin), S:t Clemens (Clement), S:t Athanasius (Athanasius), S:t Epifanius (Epiphanius), S:t Ignatius (Ignatius), S:t Johannes av Damaskus (John of Damascus), S:t Bonaventura (Bonaventure), S:t Franciskus (Francis), S:t Dominikus (Dominic), S:t Thomas (Thomas), S:t Leo (Leo), S:t Paulus (Paul), S:t Efraim (Ephrem).

## Quotation marks

Swedish uses "..." (English-style double quotes) as primary quotation marks, with '...' for nested quotes. These ARE the standard in Swedish typography. Agents should NOT use „..." (German/Dutch style) for Swedish.

## Theological terminology

Heliga Skriften (Holy Scripture), sakrament (sacrament), nåd (grace), synd (sin), frälsning (salvation), kyrkofader (Church Father), evangelium (Gospel), Första Moseboken (Genesis), Andra Moseboken (Exodus).

## Bible references and psalm numbering

Gospel references use Swedish Bible convention (e.g., "Joh 8:12" — note colon for verse separator). Swedish Bibles (Bibel 2000, Svenska Folkbibeln) use modern/Hebrew psalm numbering — convert from Vulgate numbering where needed (e.g., Vulgate Psalm 89 becomes Psalm 90).

## Register

Formal scholarly Swedish suitable for theological commentary — formellt akademiskt språk. Avoid colloquial constructions. Proper use of Swedish diacritics (å, ä, ö) is critical.

## Agent pitfalls

- **"Moses" vs "Mose"**: Agents consistently produce "Moses" instead of the correct Swedish genitive "Mose". Must be caught in review.
- **Adjective agreement**: Agents produce plural/definite adjective forms (e.g., "olärda") when singular indefinite ("olärd") or neuter ("olärt") is needed. Swedish adjectives must agree with their noun in number, gender (common/neuter), and definiteness. Check adjective forms match their noun.
- **Title/og:title**: Agents may retain English forms like "St. Jerome" instead of "S:t Hieronymus" in metadata.
- **Typos**: Watch for broken words and triple-letter errors (e.g., "ratttrogna" for "rättrogna").
- **German-style quotes**: Agents may use „..." instead of correct Swedish "...".
