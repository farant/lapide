# Italian

Use formal scholarly/theological Italian (registro elevato/colto). File suffix `_it`. Latin alphabet, LTR (no `dir` attribute needed).

## Saint names

Use San/Santo/Santa/Sant' following standard Italian Catholic convention:

Sant'Agostino (St. Augustine), San Girolamo (St. Jerome), San Basilio (St. Basil), Sant'Ambrogio (St. Ambrose), San Giovanni Crisostomo (St. Chrysostom), San Gregorio (St. Gregory), San Bernardo (St. Bernard), Sant'Ilario (St. Hilary), San Cipriano (St. Cyprian), San Giustino (St. Justin), San Clemente (St. Clement), Sant'Atanasio (St. Athanasius), Sant'Epifanio (St. Epiphanius), Sant'Ignazio (St. Ignatius), San Giovanni Damasceno (John of Damascus), San Bonaventura (St. Bonaventure), San Francesco (St. Francis), San Domenico (St. Dominic), San Tommaso (St. Thomas), San Leone (St. Leo), San Paolo (St. Paul), Sant'Efrem (St. Ephrem).

Note: "Sant'" is used before vowels (Sant'Agostino, Sant'Ambrogio); "San" before consonants (San Girolamo, San Bernardo); "Santo" before certain names beginning with s+consonant or z (Santo Stefano).

## Quotation marks

Use Italian angular quotation marks «...» (virgolette caporali/sergenti) for quotations and "..." for nested quotes. This is the standard convention in formal Italian publishing.

## Register

Formal scholarly Italian suitable for theological commentary — registro elevato, avoiding colloquial constructions. Proper use of Italian accents (à, è, é, ì, ò, ó, ù) is critical. Note the distinction between è (open e, "is") and é (closed e, "and/because") — agents often confuse these.

## Agent pitfalls

- **English-style quotes**: Agents default to English "..." quotation marks instead of guillemets «...». Must be explicitly instructed.
- **Accent confusion**: Agents may confuse è (grave, open — used for "is" and most words) with é (acute, closed — used for perché, poiché, affinché, né, sé). Italian uses grave accents on à, è, ì, ò, ù in most cases; acute only on specific words ending in -ché and on né, sé.
- **Missing accents on monosyllables**: Agents may omit required accents on è (is), già (already), più (more), può (can), ciò (this), là (there), sì (yes), né (neither). These are mandatory to distinguish from unaccented homographs.
- **Gender agreement with Latin-form adjectives**: Agents may use masculine Latin participial forms where Italian requires feminine agreement — e.g., "cose perituri" (masculine) instead of "cose periture" (feminine, agreeing with the feminine noun "cose"). Watch for this with adjectives derived from Latin participles.
