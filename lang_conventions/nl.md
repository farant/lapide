# Dutch

Use formal scholarly Dutch (wetenschappelijk register). File suffix `_nl`. Latin alphabet, LTR (no `dir` attribute needed). `lang="nl"` on the `<html>` tag.

## Biblical proper names

Follow Dutch Catholic tradition (Willibrordvertaling): Mozes (Moses), Abraham, Isaak (Isaac), Jakob (Jacob), Aäron (Aaron), David, Jozua (Joshua), Jesaja (Isaiah), Jeremia (Jeremiah), Adam, Eva (Eve), Noach (Noah).

## Quotation marks

Use Dutch quotation marks „..." for standard quotations and ‚...' for nested quotes.

## Theological terminology

Heilige Schrift (Holy Scripture), sacrament (sacrament), genade (grace), zonde (sin), verlossing/heil (salvation), Kerkvader (Church Father), Evangelie (Gospel), Genesis (Genesis), Exodus (Exodus).

## Bible references and psalm numbering

Gospel references use Dutch Bible convention (e.g., „Johannes 8,12" — note comma for verse separator per Dutch convention).

## Register

Formal scholarly Dutch suitable for theological commentary — wetenschappelijk register, avoiding colloquial constructions. Proper use of Dutch diacritics is critical: trema (ë, ï) marks separate vowel pronunciation (Israël, geïnspireerd, Hiëronymus, Aäron) and must not be omitted. Acute accent (é) appears in loanwords and emphasis.

## Agent pitfalls

- **English-style quotes**: Agents must be explicitly instructed to use Dutch quotation marks „..." (not English "...") as they default to English-style quotes.
- **Trema omission**: Agents may omit the trema on words like Israël, geïnspireerd, Hiëronymus, Aäron.
- **Willibrordvertaling reference**: Dutch Catholic terminology largely parallels German but with distinct spelling; the Willibrordvertaling (Catholic Bible translation) is the reference standard for biblical names and book titles.
