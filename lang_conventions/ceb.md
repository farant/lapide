# Cebuano (Bisaya)

Use pormal nga Sinugbuanon (formal Cebuano). File suffix `_ceb`. Latin alphabet, LTR (no `dir` attribute needed).

## Biblical proper names

Follow Cebuano Catholic tradition (Spanish-influenced): Moises (Moses), Josue (Joshua), Isaias (Isaiah), Jeremias (Jeremiah), Abraham, Isaac, Jacob, Aaron, David, Adan (Adam), Eva (Eve), Noe (Noah). Use forms from the Ang Biblia (Cebuano) and Ang Pulong sa Dios where available.

## Saint names

Use San/Santa prefix (e.g., San Jeronimo (St. Jerome), San Basilio (St. Basil), San Agustin (St. Augustine), San Ambrosio (St. Ambrose), San Crisostomo (St. Chrysostom), San Gregorio (St. Gregory), San Bernardo (St. Bernard)).

## Quotation marks

Use standard double quotation marks "..." for quotations.

## Theological terminology

Religious and ecclesiastical terminology in Cebuano draws heavily from Spanish: Balaang Kasulatan (Holy Scripture), sacramento/sakramento, grasya, sala (sin), simbahan (church), pari (priest), obispo (bishop). Use established Cebuano Catholic liturgical phrasing where it exists.

Key terms:
- Holy Scripture: Balaang Kasulatan
- God: Dios
- Lord: Ginoo
- Church: Simbahan
- Grace: grasya
- Sin: sala
- Faith: pagtuo
- Salvation: kaluwasan
- Soul: kalag
- Heaven: langit
- Paradise: paraiso
- Angel: anghel
- Devil/Satan: yawa/Satanas
- Prophet: propeta
- Apostle: apostol
- Gospel: Ebanghelyo
- Psalm: Salmo

## Bible references and psalm numbering

Use Vulgate psalm numbering (matching the Latin source). Cebuano Catholic Bibles generally follow Vulgate numbering.

## Register

Formal but accessible — literary Cebuano suitable for theological content. Avoid overly colloquial Bisaya while remaining natural. Cebuano has no diacritics concern (standard Latin alphabet). Cebuano uses a verb-initial (VSO) word order — maintain natural Cebuano syntax rather than calquing English SVO structures.

## Differences from Tagalog

Cebuano and Tagalog share many Spanish loanwords but have distinct grammars and vocabularies. Do NOT substitute Tagalog words. Key differences:
- "and": ug (not "at")
- "the/this": ang (shared), but articles and case markers differ
- "in/at": sa (shared in many contexts)
- "not": dili (not "hindi")
- "because": tungod kay / kay (not "dahil" or "sapagkat")
- "if": kung (shared)
- "also": usab (not "rin/din")
- "very": kaayo (not "napaka-")

## Agent pitfalls

- Agents may produce Tagalog instead of Cebuano, especially for common function words. Watch for: hindi→dili, dahil→tungod, sapagkat→kay, ngunit→apan, subalit→apan, rin/din→usab, mga (shared but usage differs).
- Agents may use informal/colloquial Bisaya — maintain formal register throughout.
- Spanish loanwords are more prominent in Cebuano religious vocabulary than in everyday speech — use them for theological terms (sacramento, grasya, ebanghelyo).
