Synopsis of the Chapter

Maxims on the wise son, on guarding the mouth and the rash speaker: on the poor rich man and the rich poor man: on the lamp of the wicked, hasty wealth, the deferring of hope: on the wicked messenger and the faithful one, on the tillage of fathers: all things should be done with counsel, and one should associate with the wise: on sparing the rod, and the insatiable belly of the wicked.

Vulgate Text: Proverbs 13:1-25

1. A wise son hearkens to the instruction of his father: but he who is a scoffer does not hear when he is rebuked. 2. From the fruit of his mouth a man shall be filled with good things: but the soul of transgressors is wicked. 3. He who guards his mouth guards his soul: but he who is reckless in speaking shall experience evils. 4. The sluggard desires and does not desire: but the soul of the diligent shall be made fat. 5. The just man shall detest a lying word: but the wicked man confounds and shall be confounded. 6. Justice guards the way of the innocent: but wickedness overthrows the sinner. 7. There is one who makes himself rich, though he has nothing: and there is one who makes himself poor, though he has great riches. 8. The ransom of a man's life is his riches: but he who is poor does not bear rebuke. 9. The light of the just makes glad: but the lamp of the wicked shall be extinguished. 10. Among the proud there are always quarrels: but those who do all things with counsel are guided by wisdom. 11. Wealth gotten in haste shall diminish: but what is gathered little by little by hand shall be multiplied. 12. Hope deferred afflicts the soul: desire fulfilled is a tree of life. 13. He who speaks against a thing binds himself for the future: but he who fears the commandment shall dwell in peace. Deceitful souls wander in sins: but the just are merciful and show mercy. 14. The law of the wise is a fountain of life, that he may turn aside from the ruin of death. 15. Good doctrine gives grace: in the way of scorners is a pit. 16. The prudent man does all things with counsel: but he who is foolish lays open his folly. 17. The messenger of the wicked shall fall into evil: but a faithful ambassador is health. 18. Poverty and shame to him who forsakes discipline: but he who yields to reproof shall be glorified. 19. Desire, if it be fulfilled, delights the soul: fools detest those who flee from evils. 20. He who walks with the wise shall be wise: the friend of fools shall become like them. 21. Evil pursues sinners: and good things shall be repaid to the just. 22. The good man leaves sons and grandsons as heirs: and the substance of the sinner is kept for the just. 23. Much food is in the tillage of fathers: and for others it is gathered without judgment. 24. He who spares the rod hates his son: but he who loves him corrects him diligently. 25. The just man eats and fills his soul: but the belly of the wicked is never satisfied.

1. A WISE SON HEARKENS TO HIS FATHER'S INSTRUCTION: BUT HE WHO IS A SCOFFER DOES NOT HEAR WHEN HE IS REBUKED.

For "instruction" the Hebrew is musar, that is, discipline, training, education. This maxim provides a sharp spur both to parents, to train their children in every discipline of virtue, and to children, to embrace it with their whole mind. The meaning therefore is, as if to say: A wise son hears the instruction of his father and obeys it. "But he who is a scoffer and mocker" — that is, a fool — who laughs at and scorns his father's admonitions, does not hear his father rebuking him when he is reproved for his vices.
