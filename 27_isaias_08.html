Synopsis of the Chapter

He continues the birth of the virgin prophetess and the origin of Emmanuel. Hence he is commanded to write in a book his name: Make haste to seize the spoils, hasten to plunder. Second, in verse 6, he threatens the unbelieving with the Assyrians, whom he nevertheless announces will perish shortly after. Third, in verse 11, he warns them not to fear the conspiracy of Rezin and Pekah, but the Lord of hosts. Fourth, in verse 19, to those who believe not God but soothsayers and consult them, he threatens destruction.

Vulgate Text: Isaiah 8:1-22

1. And the Lord said to me: Take yourself a large book, and write in it with the pen of a man: Make haste to seize the spoils, hasten to plunder. 2. And I took to me faithful witnesses, Uriah the priest, and Zechariah the son of Barachiah: 3. and I went to the prophetess, and she conceived, and bore a son. And the Lord said to me: Call his name, Hasten to take the spoils; Make haste to plunder. 4. For before the child knows how to call his father and his mother, the strength of Damascus and the spoils of Samaria shall be taken away before the king of the Assyrians. 5. And the Lord spoke to me yet again, saying: 6. Because this people has rejected the waters of Siloam, which go with silence, and has taken up rather with Rezin and the son of Remaliah: 7. therefore behold the Lord will bring upon them the waters of the river, strong and many, the king of the Assyrians and all his glory: and he shall rise up over all his channels and shall flow over all his banks, 8. and he shall pass through Judah, overflowing and going over, he shall reach even to the neck. And the stretching out of his wings shall fill the breadth of your land, O Emmanuel! 9. Gather yourselves together, O peoples, and be overcome; and hearken, all you lands afar off: strengthen yourselves, and be overcome; gird yourselves and be overcome: 10. Take counsel together, and it shall be defeated: speak the word, and it shall not stand: for God is with us. 11. For thus said the Lord to me: As with a strong hand He instructed me, that I should not walk in the way of this people, saying: 12. Say not, A conspiracy; for all that this people speaks is conspiracy: and fear not their fear, nor be afraid. 13. Sanctify the Lord of hosts Himself: let Him be your fear, and let Him be your dread, 14. And He shall be to you a sanctification. But for a stone of stumbling and for a rock of scandal to the two houses of Israel; for a snare and for a ruin to the inhabitants of Jerusalem. 15. And very many of them shall stumble and fall, and shall be broken, and shall be snared, and taken. 16. Bind up the testimony, seal the law among my disciples. 17. And I will wait for the Lord, who has hidden His face from the house of Jacob, and I will look for Him. 18. Behold I and my children, whom the Lord has given me for a sign and for a wonder in Israel, from the Lord of hosts, who dwells in Mount Sion. 19. And when they shall say to you: Seek of soothsayers and diviners, who mutter in their incantations: Should not the people seek of their God, for the living from the dead? 20. To the law rather, and to the testimony. And if they speak not according to this word, they shall not have the morning light. 21. And he shall pass through it, and shall fall and be hungry: and when he is hungry, he will be angry and curse his king and his God, and he will look upward. 22. And he will look to the earth, and behold tribulation and darkness, dissolution and distress, and a mist pursuing, and he will not be able to fly from his anguish.
