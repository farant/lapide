Isaac, by his very name signifies a figure and grace. For Isaac in Latin means laughter: and laughter is the mark of joy. And who does not know that He is the joy of all, who, with the dread of fearful death either suppressed or the sorrow removed, has become for all the remission of sins?

— St. Ambrose, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XXI — "And Abraham Called His Son Isaac"
