"And he died." This is added for each patriarch, so that you may see how effective was the sentence of death pronounced by God upon Adam. Therefore let each of us reflect: Of me too it will shortly be said: "And he died." This is, or will be, the emblem of me and of everyone; this the epitaph: Cornelius lived so many years, and in such a year he died. "He easily despises all things, who always thinks that he is about to die," says St. Jerome.

— Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter V — "And He Died"
