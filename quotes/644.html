Our soul is either governed by a legitimate king, or devastated by a tyrant. If, with God's help, we live piously; if we think about charity, mercy, patience, repentance: although we are still in Egypt, that is, in the flesh, nevertheless we are governed by Christ the King. But if our soul begins to turn away from God and pursue dishonorable things, then it submits its neck to the tyrant.

— Origen and St. Augustine, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Exodus, Chapter I — "He Commanded All the People"
