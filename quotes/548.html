Just as wax, unless it is reheated or softened beforehand, does not easily receive the impression of a seal: so also a man, unless he is tested by the exercise of labors and manifold infirmity, will by no means allow himself to be marked with the seal of divine grace.

— Bl. Antiochus, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XXXIII — "Moral Lesson: Why God Tries His Saints with Tribulation"
