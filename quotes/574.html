Great was the man Joseph, who though sold did not know a servile spirit, when loved did not love in return, when asked did not yield, when seized he fled. Who when by the wife of his master he was confronted, he could be held by his garment but could not be seized in his soul: nor did he even endure her words any longer; for he judged it a contagion if he delayed further, lest through the hands of the adulteress the incentives of lust pass to him. And so he took off his garment and shook off the accusation.

— St. Ambrose, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XXXIX — "He Fled"
