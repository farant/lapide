Was not Jacob happy even when he was leaving his homeland? Indeed he was truly happy, who accepted the hardships of exile so as to soften his brother's anger. For if he is happy who avoids sin, surely it cannot be denied that he is happy who lightens another's guilt and averts a crime.

— St. Ambrose, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XXVIII — "He Came into Mesopotamia"
