Three hundred holy Martyrs at Carthage, when the Governor had ordered a lime kiln to be heated and said to them, "Choose one of two things: either offer incense upon these coals to Jupiter, or be plunged into the lime" — armed with faith, confessing Christ the Son of God, they cast themselves with the swiftest rush into the fire, and amid the fumes of the lime were reduced to dust. From this, that shining army of the blessed merited the name Massa Candida.

— Caesar Baronius, The Roman Martyrology — August 24
