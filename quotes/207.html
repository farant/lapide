When He was treating of individual things, He said only: "God saw that it was good"; but when it was said of all things together, it was not enough to say "good" unless "very" were added. For if individual works of God, when considered by the wise, are found to have praiseworthy measures and numbers and orders, each established in its own kind, how much more all things together, that is, the universe itself, which is completed by these individual things gathered into one? For all beauty that consists of parts is far more praiseworthy in the whole than in the parts.

— Augustine, in Pianciani, On the Work of the Fifth / Sixth Day and the Seventh Day — "The Deep Sleep of Adam; The Command in Paradise"
