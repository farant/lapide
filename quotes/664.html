When any Christian has begun to live well, to be fervent in good works, and to despise the world; in the very newness of his works he suffers reproachers and opponents — lukewarm Christians. But if he shall have persevered, and shall have overcome them by enduring, and shall not have fallen away from good works, those very same people will yield to us, who before were hindering us.

— St. Augustine, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Exodus, Chapter V — "Lord, Why Have You Afflicted This People?"
