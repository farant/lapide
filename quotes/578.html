The bilious dream of slaughter, beatings, and fires; the phlegmatic dream of waters, abysses, suffocations, and slow flight from harmful things — for sluggish phlegm produces these; the sanguine dream of music, banquets, meadows, birds, and flight; the melancholic dream of dark and sad things, of death, of tombs, of Ethiopians and demons.

— Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XL — "Whether It Is Lawful to Divine from Dreams"
