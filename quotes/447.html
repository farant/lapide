The glory of the Lord was seen in the cloud, in darkness, and in fire. In the cloud, indeed, because He protects us from the heat of temptations. In darkness, truly, because the power of His majesty can be fully comprehended by no creature. But in burning fire, because He both irradiates the minds of the faithful with the knowledge of heavenly goods, and kindles them with hope and love.

— Bede the Venerable, On the Tabernacle, Book I, Chapter I — "Moses Ascending the Mountain"
