The herds were increased, the flocks multiplied, abundant riches flowed in, and immediately concord is torn apart. Before there was peace and the bond of charity; now there is quarrel and strife. For where there is mine and yours, there is every kind of litigation; but where these do not exist, there peace and concord dwell secure.

— St. John Chrysostom, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XIII — "The Quarrel of the Herdsmen"
