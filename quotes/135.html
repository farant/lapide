On Holy Saturday, which is the vigil of Easter, the office begins in the morning in the church; and after the office is completed, the Kyrie eleison is sung until, with an angel coming, a light is kindled in the lamps which hang over the [Lord's] sepulchre; from which the patriarch gives to the bishops and the rest of the people, that each may illuminate his own place.

— Bernard the Frank Monk, Itinerary to the Holy Places — Jerusalem
