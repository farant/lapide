A hard heart is one that is not pierced by compunction, not softened by piety, not moved by prayers, does not yield to threats, and is hardened further by scourges. It is ungrateful for benefits, faithless toward counsels, cruel in judgments, shameless before base things, fearless before dangers, inhuman toward human things, reckless toward divine things: forgetful of the past, neglectful of the present, improvident about the future.

— St. Bernard, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Exodus, Chapter VII — "What Is Hardness of Heart?"
