At Nicomedia, many thousands of Martyrs, when they had assembled at the Lord's house on the Nativity of Christ, and the Emperor Diocletian ordered the doors of the church to be shut, and fire to be prepared all around, and a herald to proclaim that those who wished to escape the fire should come out and burn incense to Jupiter — when all with one voice had answered that they would rather die for Christ, the fire was set and they were consumed, and thus on that day they merited to be born in heaven, on which Christ once deigned to be born on earth for the salvation of the world.

— Caesar Baronius, The Roman Martyrology — December 25
