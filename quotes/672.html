The evil of an inflexible and obstinate mind is punished eternally, although committed temporally; because what was brief in time, or in deed, is proven to be long in a pertinacious will: so that if such a person never died, he would never cease to will to sin; indeed, he would always wish to live so that he might always be able to sin.

— St. Bernard, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Exodus, Chapter VII — "How Does God Treat the Hardened?"
