In this way, between progress and decline, there is no middle ground; just as on a wheel that turns, he who sits upon it cannot stand still, but must either ascend or descend. Like an ant, therefore, gather merits in this life, so that by them you may live, and live gloriously, in the eternal life which awaits you; "whatever your hand is able to do, work at it earnestly"; how you will rejoice in eternity over this little time well spent!

— St. Bernard, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XXVIII — "The Ladder as the Rule of Religious Life"
