St. John the Almsgiver, Patriarch of Alexandria, ordered a tomb to be built for himself, but left unfinished; and on solemn feast days, in the sight of many, he wished the workmen to say to him: "Your tomb, Lord, is still unfinished; command therefore that it be completed at last; for it is uncertain at what hour death will come." "It is uncertain," says Seneca, "in what place death awaits you; therefore do you await it in every place."

— Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter V — "And He Died"
