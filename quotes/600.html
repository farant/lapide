Benedict XI, raised from poverty to the Pontificate, when his mother came to him adorned in splendid dress, pretended not to recognize her. So she took off the silken garments and put on her own ragged clothes; then the Pontiff embraced her: "In this dress I left my mother, and such a one I gladly recognize and receive."

— Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XLIII — "Verse 23"
