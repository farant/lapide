Let everyone show himself chaste, let him not covet another's bed, nor defile another's wife with the hope of hiding or the impunity of action. God, the guardian of marriage, is present — whom nothing escapes, no one evades, no one mocks. He watches over the absent husband's place, keeps guard — nay, without guards He catches the guilty before he does what he has planned.

— St. Ambrose, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XII — "The Lord Struck Pharaoh with Great Plagues"
