Dido, having gathered much gold and silver, sailed to Africa and there founded Carthage; and when she was sought in marriage by Hyarbas, king of Libya, she built a funeral pyre in memory of her late husband Sychaeus and cast herself into it, preferring to burn rather than marry another. A chaste woman founded Carthage; again the same city ended in the praise of chastity. For the wife of Hasdrubal, when Carthage was taken and set on fire, seeing that she was about to be captured by the Romans, seizing her two little sons, one in each hand, threw herself into the fire burning beneath her own house.

— Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter II — "Therefore a Man Shall Leave Father and Mother"
