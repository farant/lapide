Both the father is reproved in the son, and the son in the father, sharing a common partnership in foolishness, wickedness, and impiety. Nor could it be that one who had been a worthless son to a good father, degenerate both in nature and in upbringing, would beget a good son.

— St. Ambrose, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter IX — "Cursed Be Canaan"
