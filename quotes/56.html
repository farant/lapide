But, they say, how can properties be in the essence and not determine it, when they are in the persons in such a way that they determine them? We say, however, that this must be firmly held, but we should not be ashamed to confess that we do not know how this is so, together with Hilary who also says: I do not know, I do not inquire, but I will console myself nonetheless: archangels do not know, angels have not heard, the ages do not grasp it, the Prophet did not perceive it, the Apostle did not seek it, the Son Himself did not reveal it. Let the pain of complaints therefore cease.

— Master Bandinus, Sentences I — Distinction XXXIII
