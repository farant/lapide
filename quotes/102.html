Whoever thinks he sings well without knowledge of this art, when, asked about the number or intervals of high and low sounds, he cannot answer, and wishes to trust only his private sense rather than reason the teacher — such a person is more to be compared to a nightingale, which in springtime sings as if rhythmically and sweetly, than to be considered a skilled singer.

— Berno of Reichenau, Music, or Prologue to the Tonary — Section 14
