Man stood between the Lord and the enemy, between life and death, between destruction and salvation. God threatens in order to save; the serpent persuades in order to torment. Through God severity threatens life, through the devil flattery threatens death. And indeed — O shame! — God threatens, and is despised; the devil persuades, and is heeded. With God there is severity, but benign; with the devil, flattery, but harmful.

— Chrysostom, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter II — "Of the Tree of Knowledge Thou Shalt Not Eat"
