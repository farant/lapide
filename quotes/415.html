It belongs to a perfect mind to take nothing from earthly things, nothing from the enticements of the body. As if avoiding the contagion of intemperance, as if fleeing the stain of bodily senses, he rejects the delights of the world, seeking the things that are above the world: this is to stretch out one's hands to the Lord.

— St. Ambrose, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XIV — "From a Thread to a Sandal Strap"
