When the kingdom of heaven and those ineffable goods are set before us, do we go mad with desire for riches — things that are momentary and often do not last until evening — and prefer them to things that are perpetual and everlasting? And what could be worse than this madness? Since we are deprived of those goods on account of our excessive love for these, and can never enjoy these purely.

— St. Chrysostom, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XXV — "He Went Away Thinking Little of It"
