When the venerable Father was pressed by his final illness, the lord bishop visited him daily, weeping and grieving that he would be left behind as a survivor. On the last day, arriving at the break of dawn and seeing him already drawing his final breath, at his nod he approached the altar to celebrate Mass for his passing. After saying it, he returned to the Father, and making his last farewell, he fell upon him with kisses and tears. Then, having commended the soul of so great a friend to God, he washed his body with his own hands and clothed him most honorably from his own possessions.

— Deeds of the Bishops of Verdun — Continuation: Bishop Richard
