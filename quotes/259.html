Saint Paul, condemned to death in Palestine, having obtained a brief time for prayer, first prayed to God with his whole heart for his fellow tribesmen, then for the Jews and Gentiles that they might recognize the truth of the faith, then for the surrounding multitude, and finally for the judge who had condemned him and for the executioner by whom he was to be struck, and received the crown of martyrdom.

— Caesar Baronius, The Roman Martyrology — July 25
