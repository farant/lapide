If you truly love eternal memory, I will show you the way, namely, if you distribute this money into the hands of the poor, leaving behind stones and splendid buildings, villas and baths. This memory is immortal, this memory produces innumerable treasures for you, this memory relieves you from the burden of sins, this memory gains you great confidence before God.

— St. Chrysostom, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XI — "A Tower Whose Top May Reach to Heaven"
