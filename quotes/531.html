The whole life of Christ was a discipline of morals. For Christ taught that all the goods of the world, which He despised, are to be despised; He demonstrated that all the evils which He endured are to be endured — so that neither would happiness be sought in the former, nor unhappiness feared in the latter.

— St. Augustine, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XXX — "I Have Learned by Experience"
