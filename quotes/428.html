This is true companionship, this is the husband's duty, when he does not attend too carefully to his wife's words, but grants some pardon to the weakness of her sex, striving for this one thing alone, that sadness be removed from their midst, and peace and harmony be bound more tightly.

— St. John Chrysostom, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XVI — "Your Maidservant Is in Your Hand"
