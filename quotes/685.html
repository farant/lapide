Aeschines, a poor disciple, said to Socrates: "I find nothing worthy of you that I could give you, and in this way I realize that I am poor. And so I give you the one thing I have — myself." To which Socrates replied: "Why should you not have given a great gift — unless perhaps you value yourself cheaply? I shall therefore take care to return you to yourself better than I received you."

— Socrates, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Exodus, Chapter XIII — "Sanctify to Me Every Firstborn"
