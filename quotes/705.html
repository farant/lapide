Now they bridle the appetite of lust, now they temper the heat of avarice, now they suppress the glory of pride, now they crush the suggestion of envy, now they extinguish the fire of anger. They are therefore kings because, by not consenting to the movements of their temptations, they have learned not to yield but to rule and govern.

— St. Gregory, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Exodus, Chapter XIX — "You Shall Be to Me a Priestly Kingdom"
