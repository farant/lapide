Happy the house, and ever blessed the congregation, where Martha complains about Mary, that is, where the contemplation of divine things so prevails and dominates that the external activity, as it were, complains about it. Unhappy is the congregation in which Mary complains about Martha: because no time is given to Mary, that is, to contemplation, and everything is spent on external affairs.

— St. Bernard, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XXIX — "Tropological: Rachel and Leah as the Contemplative and Active Lives"
