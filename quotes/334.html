The new faith therefore of the renewed, strong, flourishing, gaining increase of virtue — not lax, not weary, not withered by some old age, and sluggish in vigor — is fit for sacrifice, which sprouts with a certain green shoot of wisdom, and blushes with the youthful fervor of divine knowledge.

— St. Ambrose, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter IV — "After Many Days"
