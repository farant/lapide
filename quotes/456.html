To flee burning Sodom is to shun the illicit fires of the flesh; the height of the mountains is the purity of the continent; to stand on the mountain is to cling to the flesh without being carnal. But those who cannot climb to the mountains are saved in Segor, because the conjugal life is neither far removed from the world, nor yet excluded from the joy of salvation.

— St. Gregory the Great, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XIX — "Save Your Life; Do Not Look Back"
