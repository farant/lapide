When summoned to trial by the curule aedile, fearing condemnation, he brought all his farming equipment into the forum, and brought forth his strong daughter, heavy mattocks, weighty plowshares, and well-fed oxen; then he said: "These are my sorceries, citizens, nor can I show you or bring into the forum my late-night labors, vigils, and sweat." Therefore he was acquitted by the votes of all.

— Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XXVI — "God Blesses the Labor of Farmers"
