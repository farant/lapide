Let us not place our effort in collecting riches to leave to our children. But let us teach them virtue, and pray for God's blessing upon them. These are the ineffable riches that are not consumed, that daily increase our substance. For nothing is equal to virtue, nothing more powerful than virtue: unless the one who wears the crown possesses it, he will be more wretched than any ragged pauper.

— St. John Chrysostom, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XLVIII — "Verse 16"
