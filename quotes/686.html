There are three stages of those converted to God: the beginning, the middle, and perfection. In the beginning, they find the enticements of sweetness; in the middle time, the struggles of temptation; but at the end, the perfection of fullness. First, sweet things receive them, which console; afterwards bitter things, which exercise them; and finally delightful and sublime things, which confirm them.

— St. Gregory, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Exodus, Chapter XIII — "God Led Them Not by the Way of the Philistines"
