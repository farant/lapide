Willigis, born the son of a cartwright, suddenly adopted by Otto III as the first among the Electors, lest he become proud, frequently reminded himself: "See who you are; remember who you were." Hence he had wheels painted in his study, with the inscription beneath them: "Willigis, mindful of your former fortune, consider who you now are."

— Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XLIII — "Verse 23"
