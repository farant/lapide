These are the proper names of three sons of Ishmael. The Hebrews use them combined as a proverb, saying, masma, duma, vemassa. By which they wish to signify that much is to be heard, kept silent about, and endured. For masma in Hebrew signifies hearing, duma silence, massa endurance. Which the Italians express as: "Hear, see, be silent, if you wish to live in peace."

— Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XXV — "Mishma Also, and Dumah, and Massa"
