Just as a drunkard, when he swallows a great deal of wine, feels no harm from the wine, but afterwards feels how great the harm is: so sin, as long as it is being committed, darkens the mind, and like a dense cloud corrupts the mind; then conscience rises up, and gnaws at the mind more severely than any accuser, showing the absurdity of the deed.

— St. John Chrysostom, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XLII — "We Deserve to Suffer These Things"
