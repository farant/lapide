A drunkard is a voluntary demon, an animated corpse; a disease that admits no pardon, a ruin that lacks excuse, a common disgrace to our race; where there is drunkenness, there is the devil; where there is excess, there the demons dance their choruses.

— St. John Chrysostom, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XIX — "Moral Excursus on Drunkenness"
