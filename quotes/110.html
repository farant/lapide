I believed I would not escape ridicule among posterity if, amid abundant treasures of wisdom and a vast supply of divine and human letters flowing like a torrent, I, entirely devoid of all eloquence, should presume to utter something, and I not unjustly feared that the following saying might be thrown at me: Do not carry wood into the forest, nor fish into the sea.

— Berno of Reichenau, On the Harmonious Diversity of Tones — Prefatory Letter
