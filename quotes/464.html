God is accustomed to act thus: He not only frees from sorrows those who bear themselves bravely in the dangers into which they fall, but also provides so much joy in adversity that they come to a complete forgetfulness of their troubles, and find themselves in an abundance of good things.

— St. John Chrysostom, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XX — "Moral Reflection from St. Chrysostom"
