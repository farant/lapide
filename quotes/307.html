Until now, O Bethlehem, you alone rejoiced in that common light, and, proud in the privilege of its unaccustomed newness, you could even contend with the very east in equality of splendor — indeed, wondrous to say, in your narrow stable there was more light than the rising of the worldly sun is wont to bring forth. Scarcely did you show that Light to a few shepherds. Scarcely did you admit three men from the East to the manger of the new Light. But now the Sun goes forth from your hidden places to illuminate the whole world.

— Adam of Perseigne, Mariale — Sermon IV: On the Purification
