The highest form of religion is that in sickness one give thanks to God: for just as bodily illness is cured by a strong and powerful medicine, so by bodily illness the vices of the heart are blunted. If you are iron, you lose your rust through the fire of tribulation; if gold, you become more splendid and pure: therefore the holy Fathers always desired to be in sickness.

— Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XXVII — "Verse 1"
