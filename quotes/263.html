Saint Tarsicius, Acolyte, carrying the Sacrament of the Body of Christ, and judging it unworthy to cast pearls before swine, was beaten by the pagans with clubs and stones until he breathed his last; and when they turned over his body, the sacrilegious searchers found nothing of the Sacraments of Christ in the hands or clothing of the slain man.

— Caesar Baronius, The Roman Martyrology — August 15
