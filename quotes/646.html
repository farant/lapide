From the beginning of the ages Christ has suffered and triumphed in all His own. In Abel slain by a brother, in Noah mocked by a son, in Abraham a pilgrim, in Isaac offered in sacrifice, in Jacob a servant, in Joseph sold, in Moses exposed, in the Prophets stoned and cut asunder, in the Apostles tossed about on land and sea, and in the many crosses of the blessed Martyrs frequently put to death.

— St. Paulinus, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Exodus, Chapter II — "And When She Could No Longer Conceal Him"
