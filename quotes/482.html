He commanded the hand to be placed under the thigh, because through that member His flesh was to descend, who would be the son of Abraham by His humanity, and his Lord by His divinity, as if to say: Touch my son, and swear by my God. Whence he commanded the hand to be placed not upon the thigh, but under it; because from there He was to descend, who would be a man indeed, but would come as one above men.

— St. Gregory, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XXIV — "Place Your Hand Under My Thigh"
