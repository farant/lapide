Excellent executioners, I count you all as nothing in this cause; carry on according to your heretical malice — I do not care about you; I have asked no one, I will never ask, I have always despised you. I can and will gladly suffer more for this cause than you together with all others can inflict. Stop threatening me with such things; force them upon deranged women. These things inflame me, they do not dismay me: I laugh at them no differently than at the cackling of so many geese.

— St. John Ogilvie, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XXX — "My Justice Shall Answer for Me"
