Some of them, covered with the skins of wild beasts, were exposed to be torn apart by dogs; others were fastened to crosses; and still others were delivered to the flames, so that when daylight failed, they might serve as torches for the night. All of these were disciples of the Apostles and the firstfruits of the Martyrs, whom the Roman Church, that fertile field of Martyrs, sent to the Lord before the death of the Apostles.

— Caesar Baronius, The Roman Martyrology — June 24
