On account of the hatred of the emperor he found the tunic of Saint Mary torn and rent in many places — that is, the properties of this Church excessively taken away and alienated. On account of their alienation and the affliction of his people, he exerted himself with all his might against the enemies of this Church, until, by God's mercy, he had the tunic of Saint Mary restored whole and returned to its proper state.

— Bertarius of Verdun, Deeds of the Bishops of Verdun — Chapter 18: Bishop Hatto
