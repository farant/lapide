Great and many services rendered to a prince are quickly consigned to oblivion and poorly rewarded; but if you offend the prince even slightly, it remains stored deep in the mind and is severely avenged. But services, even small ones, rendered to God, are preserved in His eternal memory, and are rewarded by Him with great and eternal rewards; while offenses against Him, even grave ones, are so blotted out by light penance that He Himself immediately forgets every injury and freely pardons the punishment.

— Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Exodus, Chapter I — "A New King Arose"
