By the likeness of a furnace, human life seems to be expressed, which, entangled and enwrapped in the iniquities of this world, not having the clarity of true brightness and the splendor of sincere light, inwardly seethes like a furnace with diverse desires, and pants with certain fires of longings; outwardly it is covered as with a certain smoke, so that it may not see the face of truth, until the Lord Jesus directs His heavenly lamps, that is, the brilliance of His glory.

— St. Ambrose, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XV — "The Smoking Furnace and Lamp of Fire"
