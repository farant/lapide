So that what was formerly believed more obscurely may be understood more clearly through his exposition; so that through him posterity may rejoice in understanding what antiquity, not understanding, revered; yet transmitting the same doctrines which he learned, so that when he speaks in a new way, he does not say new things.

— Vincent of Lerins, in Pianciani, On the Work of the First Day / Second Day — "What Caused the Waters to Recede"
