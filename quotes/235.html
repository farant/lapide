God is that which, when spoken of, cannot be expressed; when appraised, cannot be appraised; when defined, grows by the very definition; because He covers the heaven with His hand, He encloses the whole compass of the world in His fist: whom all things do not know, and yet by fearing they know.

— Gregory of Nazianzus, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter I — "God (Elohim): Thirteen Definitions"
