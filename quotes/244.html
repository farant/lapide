So many evils are committed, yet the stars do not deviate from above, fixed in heaven, moving through the heavenly paths which their Creator appointed and established for them: so should the Saints be, but only if their hearts are fixed in heaven. Those therefore who are in the heights, and think of the heights, from those very thoughts of heavenly things become patient. And whatever is committed on earth they do not care about, until they complete their journeys. For whoever has lost patience has fallen from heaven.

— Augustine, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter I — "And God Made Two Great Lights"
