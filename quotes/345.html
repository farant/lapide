The first born was Cain, from those two parents of the human race, belonging to the city of men; the second was Abel, to the city of God. Thus in the whole human race, when those two cities first began to run their course through births and deaths, the first born was a citizen of this world; but the second was a pilgrim in the world, belonging to the city of God, predestined by grace, chosen by grace, a pilgrim below by grace, a citizen above by grace. It is written therefore of Cain that he built a city: but Abel, as a pilgrim, did not build one. For the city of the saints is above, although it begets citizens here, among whom it is on pilgrimage until the time of its kingdom comes, when it will reign with its prince, the King of Ages, without any end of time.

— St. Augustine, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter IV — "His Wife"
