It is not his voice that accuses, not his soul, but the voice of his blood accuses, which you yourself shed: therefore your own deed, not your brother, accuses you. Yet the earth too is a witness, which received the blood. If your brother spares you, the earth does not spare you; if your brother is silent, the earth condemns you. It is both witness and judge against you. There is therefore no doubt that even the higher beings — the heavens, the sun, moon, stars, Thrones, Dominations, Principalities, Powers, Cherubim and Seraphim — condemned him whom the lower things condemned. For how can one be acquitted by that pure and heavenly judgment, whom not even the earth could acquit?

— St. Ambrose, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter IV — "The Voice of Blood"
