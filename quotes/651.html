There is one calm and faithful tranquility, one solid and perpetual security, if a person, extracted from the whirlwinds of a restless world, drawing near to his God in mind, may glory that whatever among others seems lofty and great in human affairs lies beneath his conscience: he who is greater than the world can no longer desire or seek anything from the world.

— St. Cyprian, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Exodus, Chapter III — "Now Moses Was Pasturing the Sheep of Jethro"
