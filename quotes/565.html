"This garment is not mine; take it away. It belongs to another. With these I was joined to an earthly bridegroom; with those to a heavenly one. Naked I came forth from my mother's womb, and naked I shall return there. Wrap in these the worthless material of my wretched flesh, and lay my poor body in its own little place beside the tomb of my brother and of the Lord Emperor Henry, whom I see now calling me." And having said these things, she gave up her virginal spirit to Christ her Bridegroom.

— St. Cunegund, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XXXVII — "He Put On Sackcloth"
