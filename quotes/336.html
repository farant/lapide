Sin, that is, the punishment of sin, which like a dog or Cerberus lying in wait, besieges the doors of sin as the avenger of sin; this, as soon as you do evil, will be at your side, will bark at you, will bite you and tear you apart. This dog is the worm of conscience, the turmoil and indignation of the mind, the anger of God threatening the sinner's head, tribulation, anguish, and all sufferings present and eternal, with which God punishes sins.

— Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter IV — "If You Do Well"
