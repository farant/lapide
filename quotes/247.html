Rightly therefore he was last, as the sum of the whole work, as the cause of the world, for whom all things were made, as the inhabitant of all the elements: he lives among wild beasts, swims with fish, flies above birds, converses with angels; he dwells on earth and serves in heaven; he plows the sea, feeds on air; a tiller of the soil, a traveler of the deep, a fisherman in the waves, a fowler in the air, an heir in heaven, a co-heir of Christ.

— Ambrose, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter I — "Let Us Make Man in Our Image and Likeness"
