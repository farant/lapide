It is next to an article of faith that Enoch, equally as Elijah, have not yet died. Hence Tertullian calls them candidates for eternity: "Candidates for eternity," he says, "they learn immunity of the flesh from all vice, from all harm, from all injury and insult." And Irenaeus calls them "those who share the first beginnings of immortality," that is, receiving its omen and, as it were, its foreshadowing.

— Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter V — "He Was Seen No More"
