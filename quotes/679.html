I dread hell, I tremble at the teeth of the infernal beast, I shudder at the gnawing worm and the scorching fire, the smoke and vapor and sulfur, and the spirit of storms: I shudder at the outer darkness. Who will give water to my head and a fountain of tears to my eyes, that I may anticipate with weeping the weeping and gnashing of teeth?

— St. Bernard, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Exodus, Chapter X — "Horrible Darkness"
