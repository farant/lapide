From God, as from a breast distended with all good things, we abundantly suck all good things. Shaddai therefore signifies that God is sweet as the breast and milk; and that He nourishes all things with that affection of charity and love with which a mother cherishes her infant by applying it to her breasts, and feeds and nourishes it with milk.

— Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XVII — "El Shaddai"
