God chose Moses, thus inarticulate, for this mission, both to keep Moses in humility through this defect, and so that the liberation of the Hebrews would be attributed not to Moses' eloquence, but to God's power; for God chose the weak things of the world to confound the strong. In like manner God chose rough and inarticulate Apostles, through whom He converted the world.

— Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Exodus, Chapter IV — "I Am Not Eloquent"
