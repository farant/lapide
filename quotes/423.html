Whatever we sin comes, as it were, into one heap before God, so that when the measure is filled, certain destruction falls upon us. Let us therefore not think sins are light, even small ones, because they add something to this heap. It is a grace when God quickly punishes sins: for by this the heap of sins decreases. Conversely, it is a great anger of God when He long delays and dissembles: for then the heap of guilt grows, and consequently also of punishment.

— Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XV — "In the Fourth Generation"
