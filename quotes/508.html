Differently the flower of the olive; because sweet is the work of mercy; because like oil it soothes and gives light. Differently the flower of the rose; because the white life of the flesh consists in the incorruption of virginity. Differently the flower of the violet; because great is the virtue of the humble, who holding by desire the last places, do not raise themselves through humility from earth on high, and preserve in their minds the purple of the heavenly kingdom.

— St. Gregory, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XXVII — "Verse 27"
