He crushed riches by the wondrous poverty of the manger, honors by the unheard-of humility of the darkness, and pleasures by the harshness of the stable itself. Hence now poverty claims for itself the name of riches; riches are called want; the disgrace, mockery, and reproaches endured for Christ's sake are called honors; the cross is called glory; death, life; prison, liberty.

— Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Exodus, Chapter XI — "Mystical Meaning of the Slaughter of the Firstborn"
