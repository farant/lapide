St. Hilarion, fearing in death, said to himself: "You have served the Lord for nearly seventy years, and you are afraid to die?" Abbot Pambo, dying, said: "I depart now to my God; but as one who has scarcely begun to truly and rightly worship God." Arsenius said: "Grant, O Lord, that at least now I may begin to live piously." St. Francis near death said: "Brothers, until now we have made little progress; let us now begin to serve God." Anthony said: "Today, consider that you have taken up the religious life." And Agatho, who had lived holily, used to say: "I dread death, because the judgments of God are different from those of men."

— Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter III — "She Shall Crush Your Head"
