Four qualities belong to a good man: first, that he strive to make all men his friends; second, that if he cannot make them friends, at least not make them enemies; third, that if even this is not possible, he depart on good terms; fourth, that if someone pursues him as he withdraws, he defend himself as he can. But the first three of these we recognize in Abraham, not in mere words but in true deeds. The fourth, however, does not apply, since even toward the one who was departing he preserved the affection of a parent, so that not only did he not pursue him, but he even rescued and freed him when captured.

— St. Ambrose, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XIII — "If to the Left or Right"
