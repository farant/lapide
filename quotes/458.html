The corrupters of minds find no avenue of accusation against the life of the just. For, struck with blindness, they go around the house as it were, because in their envy they scrutinize words and deeds: but because on every side a strong and praiseworthy action from the life of the just confronts them, wandering about they grope at nothing but the wall.

— St. Gregory the Great, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XIX — "The Sodomites Struck with Blindness"
