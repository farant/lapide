The five kings are the five senses of our body: sight, smell, taste, touch, and hearing. The four kings are the bodily and worldly allurements; for the flesh of man, like the world, consists of four elements. Our senses therefore easily give themselves over to worldly pleasures and are captured by a certain power of these pleasures. For the bodily delights and allurements of this world are not overcome except by a mind that is spiritual, clinging to God and separating itself entirely from earthly things.

— St. Ambrose, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XIV — "Bela, Which Is Segor"
