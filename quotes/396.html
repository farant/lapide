The first renunciation is that by which we corporally despise all the riches and possessions of the world; the second, by which we reject the former habits and vices and old affections of the soul and the flesh; the third, by which, withdrawing our mind from all present and visible things, we contemplate only what is future and desire those things which are invisible.

— Abbot Paphnutius, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XII — "Go Forth from Your Land"
