One and the same teaching cannot suit everyone: the wise must be taught one way, the foolish another; the rich one way, the poor another; the healthy one way, the sick another; the old one way, the young another; men one way, women another; the celibate one way, the married another; superiors one way, subjects another.

— Bede the Venerable, On the Tabernacle, Book I, Chapter VII — "The Bread of the Presence"
