In place of his brothers' hatred, he gained the favor of the king. In place of exile, exaltation. In place of fetters, a golden chain. In place of the cloak that the adulteress had stripped from him, a linen robe. In place of the lowliness of prison, the royal chariot. In place of the name of a slave, he is called Savior of the world. If God so rewards the labors and afflictions of His own in this life, what will He do in the life to come?

— Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XLI — "Verse 45"
