What is man? Man is the sport of fortune, the image of inconstancy, the mirror of corruption, the spoil of time, says Aristotle; a slave of death, a passing traveler; a ball with which God plays, says Plautus; a weak and fragile body, naked, unarmed, in need of another's help, says Seneca; a bond of corruption, a living death, a sentient corpse, a revolving sepulcher, says Trismegistus; a phantom and a faint shadow, says Sophocles; a dream of a shadow, says Pindar.

— Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter III — "For Dust You Are"
