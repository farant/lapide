Since man is wretched, let him also be temporal, and thus let him be unlike both God and the devil: for God is both eternal and happy, and His is eternal happiness, happy eternity. Of these two, the devil has lost the one, that is, happiness; but he has not lost eternity, and his is eternal unhappiness, unhappy eternity. Let us spare man, says God; and since he has lost happiness, let us also snatch away eternity from the wretched one; so that in neither respect is he as one of Us.

— Rupert of Deutz, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter III — "Behold, Adam Has Become as One of Us"
