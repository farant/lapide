Three epithets are impressed upon the murderer, which aggravate his guilt. First, he is called a man — one who ought to have been mindful of his humanity. Second, he is called a man — one whom it befitted to master his anger and not abuse his strength and power. Third, he is called a brother — one who ought to have been joined to his brother by the closest love, and therefore to defend, not kill, him.

— Delrio, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter IX — "Whoever Shall Shed Man's Blood"
