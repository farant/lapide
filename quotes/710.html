The wisdom of the just is to feign nothing through ostentation, to reveal one's meaning in words, to love true things as they are, to avoid false things, to display good things freely, to endure evil things more willingly than to do them, to seek no revenge for injury, to count insult for the sake of truth as gain.

— St. Gregory, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Exodus, Chapter XXV — "The Cherubim as Symbol of Wisdom"
