O sure salvation, O compendium of life, O sole hope of pardon, O singular sweetness! You are all things to me, my lady; in you is stored up for me the fullness of all goods. With you are laid up the inexhaustible treasures of truth and grace, of peace and mercy, of salvation and wisdom, of glory and honor. You are for me an anchor in the tossing sea, a harbor in shipwreck, a support in tribulation, comfort in sorrow. Whatever I can babble of your praises falls short of your praise — you who are most worthy of all praise — yet I am bound to praise you.

— Adam of Perseigne, Mariale — Fragment II
