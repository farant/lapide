As the flesh is nourished by soft things, so the soul is nourished by hard things, and as hard things wound the flesh, so soft things kill the spirit. The hope of carnal people is therefore called an abomination of the soul, because the spirit perishes forever from that by which the flesh lives sweetly for a time.

— St. Gregory, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Exodus, Chapter VIII — "Shall We Sacrifice the Abominations of the Egyptians?"
