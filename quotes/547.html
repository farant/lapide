What fire is to gold, the file to iron, the winnowing fan to wheat, lye to cloth, salt to meat: this tribulation is to just men. The cautery seems to be a wound, but it is really the remedy for the wound: thus affliction seems to be an evil, but it is really the remedy for evils, and of divine grace.

— Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XXXIII — "Moral Lesson: Why God Tries His Saints with Tribulation"
