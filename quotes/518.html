We are commanded to seek what is sufficient for preserving the nature of the body: we say to God, not luxury, not delicacies, not golden ornaments, not the splendor of gems, not fields, not governorships of nations, not silken garments, not musical entertainments, nor anything by which the soul is drawn away from divine and higher care; but bread.

— St. Gregory of Nyssa, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XXVIII — "The Lord Shall Be My God"
