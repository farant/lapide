Paula used to swear that in Bethlehem she could see with the eyes of faith the infant wrapped in swaddling clothes, the Lord crying in the manger, the Magi adoring, the star shining above, the virgin mother, the diligent foster-father, the shepherds coming by night, and with tears mingled with joy she would say: Hail, Bethlehem, house of bread, in which was born that bread which came down from heaven. Hail, Ephratah, most fertile region, whose fruitfulness is God.

— St. Jerome, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XXXV — "St. Jerome and St. Paula in Bethlehem"
