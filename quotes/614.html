When envoys brought gifts to corrupt Epaminondas, he invited them to dinner; a meager table was set and sour wine; then Epaminondas said: "Go, and tell your master about my meals, by which, since I am content with them, he will not easily lure me with his gifts to treason."

— Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XLVII — "Verse 26"
