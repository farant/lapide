Moses here assigns the five causes of man. The efficient cause is God. The matter is the slime of the earth, that is, earth mixed with water. The form is the breath of life. The exemplar is God: for man is the image of God. The end is that he should be a living soul, that is, a living thing, sensing, moving himself, knowing himself and other things, and exercising all the works of life.

— Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter II — "The Lord God Formed Man from the Slime of the Earth"
