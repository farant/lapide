Elijah had only a sheepskin cloak, and Ahab clothed in purple needed the sheepskin of that man. With this sheepskin he closed heaven and stopped the descent of rains. And the tongue of the Prophet was a bridle for heaven; while the one clothed in purple and crowned with a diadem went about seeking the Prophet.

— St. John Chrysostom, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XVIII — "I Will Not Destroy for the Sake of Ten"
