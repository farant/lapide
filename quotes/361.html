The appearance of the flood is a type of the purification of our soul. And so when our mind has washed itself from the bodily allurements of this world, in which it formerly delighted, it will also wipe away by good thoughts the filth of old desire, as if absorbing with purer waters the bitterness of previously turbid streams.

— St. Ambrose, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter VII — "The Flood Came to Pass"
