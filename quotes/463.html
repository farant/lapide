Arsenius, tutor of the Emperor Arcadius, fleeing into the desert, once came to a river. There was an Ethiopian girl there, who touched his cloak; he rebuked her, but she said: "If you are a monk, go to the mountain." The old man, stung by this word, said to himself: "Arsenius, if you are a monk, go to the mountain"; and there he continually said to himself: "Arsenius, why did you come out?"

— Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XIX — "Save Your Life; Do Not Look Back"
