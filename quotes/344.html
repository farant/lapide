Note here in Cain the effects and punishments of sin. There are six. The first is trembling of the body; the second is exile and flight; the third is fear and dismay of mind — "Whoever finds me will kill me." What do you fear, O Cain? Apart from you and your parents, there is as yet no other person in the world. The fourth: the earth itself pursued Cain, denying him fruits and driving him out as a fugitive. The fifth: besides terrifying lightning and flashes, Cain saw angels threatening him with death with fiery swords; if he cast his eyes to the ground, he seemed to see serpents, lions, and wild beasts rushing upon him. Sixth, Cain was a fugitive on the earth, and finally, hiding in the forests, he was killed by Lamech. Is it not true then, as St. Chrysostom says, that "sin is a voluntary madness and a self-chosen demon?"

— Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter IV — "Behold You Cast Me Out"
