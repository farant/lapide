Saint Genesius, formerly a mime actor, while mocking the Mysteries of the Christians on stage before the watching Emperor Diocletian, was suddenly, inspired by God, converted to the faith. At last, persisting in the confession of Christ and saying, "There is no King but Christ, for whom even if I be killed a thousand times, you will not be able to take Him from my mouth nor from my heart," he earned the palm of martyrdom.

— Caesar Baronius, The Roman Martyrology — August 25
