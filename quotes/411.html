For the faithful man, the whole world is his wealth. Wisdom is mistress and possessor, which considers the gifts of nature as its own, since they were given for the use of men, and it needs nothing, even if the necessities of life are lacking. The wise man judges that whatever belongs to nature is his, for he lives according to nature.

— St. Ambrose, in Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter XIII — "All the Land Which You See"
