As the waters were rising, trembling mothers with their little ones ran about through their houses, not knowing where to go; others rose terrified from the table and sought escape; from the marriage bed husband and wife leaped forth, he fleeing this way, she that way, to escape the surging wave. Oh, how they grieved then that they had not listened to Noah warning them of these things, but had mocked him! We could have once, but we refused; now we want to, but we cannot.

— Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter VII — "The Horror of the Flood"
