God gave one name to both, so that the spouses might know that they are, as it were, one man in two bodies, and that they ought to be united in soul and will, just as they are united in name. By the name Adam they are reminded that they are children of the earth — lowly, clay-made, fragile, mortal, and destined to return to the earth. Remember, Adam, that you are adama, that is, earth and dust, and to dust you shall return.

— Cornelius a Lapide, Commentary on Genesis, Chapter V — "He Called Their Name Adam"
