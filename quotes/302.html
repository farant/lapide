Almighty God, though He loves chastity in His mother and esteems charity far above chastity, yet by some manner prefers humility above all, and the Lord of majesty inclines Himself most familiarly toward it. For humility is capable of the fullness of grace in that, disdaining all that is rigid and swollen, it ever seeks the humble level of what is lower. But if grace is not denied to the humble, what grace could she possess before all others who, surpassing all the highest things with incomparable dignity, surpassed all the lowest things with incomparable humility?

— Adam of Perseigne, Mariale — Sermon I: On the Annunciation
