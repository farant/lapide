Death will be a benefit to me: it will send me more quickly to God, for whom I live and in whose service I am engaged, and whose death I have in large part already died, and toward whom I have long been hastening. Fire and sword, wild beasts, and claws tearing the flesh, are to us a pleasure and a delight rather than a terror. Therefore heap insults upon us, threaten, do whatever you please, enjoy your power; let even the Emperor hear this — you will assuredly never conquer us, nor bring it about that we consent to impious doctrine, even if you were to threaten worse things than these.

— Basil the Great, in Cornelius a Lapide, Preliminaries — Dedicatory Letter
