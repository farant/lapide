Just as hosts do not call guests to dinner before they have prepared everything necessary for the feast, and those who put on athletic games and theatrical shows, before they assemble the spectators in theaters or stadiums, provide an abundance of competitors and of those things which pertain to the delight of the eyes or ears: so the Prince of the whole world, as the giver of a contest or feast, about to call man to the banquet and spectacle, prepared everything that pertained to both: so that upon entering the world, he would immediately find both a feast and a most sacred theater.

— Philo Judaeus
