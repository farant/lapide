Let your tabernacle be gilded within; let it be gilded without as well. Within, on account of the purity of thought; without, on account of the usefulness of action. Within, so that you may meditate on holy things; without, so that you may do useful things. Within, so that you may please God in your heart; without, so that you may benefit your neighbor in your deeds.

— Adam of Dryburgh, On the Threefold Tabernacle — Part III, Chapter III
